Who Changed What

The Most Important Words Translators Got Wrong — and Why

This page tracks the most consequential translation decisions in English Bibles. For each word, you can see: what the original Hebrew or Greek says, what major translations chose, and why the choice matters. These aren’t minor academic quibbles. Each one changed what millions of people believe.

OriginalMeaningKJVMost BiblesWhy It Matters
μετάνοια
metanoia G3341		 Change of mind Repent Repent English “repent” carries guilt/sorrow connotation. The Greek is purely cognitive—a paradigm shift, not a guilt trip. Jerome’s “do penance” created the entire penitential system.
αἰώνιος
aiōnios G166		 Of the age, age-lasting Eternal / everlasting Eternal Creates “eternal punishment” from “age-lasting correction.” Used of the Aaronic priesthood (ended) and Jonah’s time in the fish (3 days). If it means “eternal,” the Aaronic priesthood hasn’t ended.
ἐκκλησία
ekklēsia G1577		 Assembly, congregation Church Church Tyndale translated it “congregation” and was killed. King James ordered “church” to protect institutional authority. “Assembly” implies people gathering; “church” implies an institution with buildings and hierarchy.
βαπτίζω
baptizō G907		 To immerse, dip, submerge Baptize Baptize Not translated at all—transliterated. If translated as “immerse,” it would challenge sprinkling. Every English Bible preserves this non-translation.
שְׁאוֹל / ᾅδης / γέεννα
Sheol / Hades / Gehenna		 Grave / unseen realm / Valley of Hinnom Hell (all three) Hell (varies) Three completely different words with different meanings, all translated “hell.” Sheol is the grave (everyone goes). Hades is the unseen realm (temporary). Gehenna is Jerusalem’s garbage dump. Mashing them together created “eternal hell.”
ποιμήν
poimēn G4166		 Shepherd Pastor Pastor/shepherd Appears as a church role exactly ONCE (Eph 4:11), plural, alongside teachers. The modern “Senior Pastor” as CEO of a church is completely foreign to the NT. “Shepherd” implies care; “pastor” implies office and authority.
πίστις
pistis G4102		 Trust, faithfulness, active reliance Faith Faith English “faith” has become passive mental agreement (“I believe God exists”). Greek pistis is active trust, loyalty, faithfulness—like trusting a chair enough to sit in it. The entire “faith vs. works” debate dissolves when you understand this word.
ἄνωθεν
anōthen G509		 From above Again Again / from above John 3:3—“born again” should be “born from above.” Nicodemus misunderstood it as “again” (re-entering the womb). Many translators preserve his misunderstanding instead of what Jesus meant.
עַלְמָה
almah H5959		 Young woman of marriageable age Virgin Virgin / young woman Isaiah 7:14. The Hebrew word for “virgin” is betulah (H1330). Almah means “young woman.” The LXX translated it as parthenos (virgin), and Matthew quoted the LXX. The original Hebrew is less specific than the translation.
