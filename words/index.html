Word Studies

The original Hebrew & Greek words behind the English translations

Hebrew — עִבְרִית

The language of the Old Testament (Tanakh)

א — Aleph
  • אֶרֶץ Erets Earth, land, ground, territory. The physical realm of creation. H776
  • אֵת Et Untranslatable direct object marker. Composed of Aleph (א) and Tav (ת)—the first and last letters of the Hebrew alphabet. H853
  • אֱלֹהִים Elohim God (plural of majesty). The supreme, mighty one. Used 2,606 times. H430
ב — Bet
  • בָּרָא Bara To create from nothing (ex nihilo). Used exclusively with God as subject. H1254
  • בְּרֵאשִׁית Bereshit In the beginning; the first, the chief, the head of all things. From rosh (head). H7225
  • בְּרִית Berith Covenant, pact, alliance. A solemn, binding agreement sealed with blood. Occurs 284 times. H1285
ד — Dalet
  • דָּם Dam Blood, lifeblood. The vehicle of the nephesh (life/soul). Occurs 360 times. H1818
כ — Kaf
  • כָּפַר Kaphar To cover, to make atonement, to smear, to coat. Root of Yom Kippur. Not “to pay a penalty” but “to cover over.” H3722
  • כָּרַת Karat To cut, to cut off. Used in karat berith (“to cut a covenant”)—the standard Hebrew idiom for making a covenant. H3772
נ — Nun
  • נֶפֶשׁ Nephesh Soul, life force, living being, the animating essence. “The life (nephesh) of the flesh is in the blood” (Lev 17:11). H5315
ר — Resh
  • רוּחַ Ruach Spirit, breath, wind. The animating force of God. Used 389 times. H7307 — Coming Soon
  • ראש Rosh Head, chief, top, summit, beginning, first. Root of Bereshit. H7218
שׁ — Shin
  • שָׁמַיִם Shamayim Heavens, sky. Always plural in Hebrew. Encompasses atmosphere, outer space, and God’s dwelling. H8064
  • שָׁלוֹם Shalom Peace, wholeness, completeness, welfare, prosperity. Far more than absence of conflict. H7965 — Coming Soon
י — Yod
  • יהוה YHWH The covenant name of God. “I AM WHO I AM.” The self-existent, eternal one. H3068 — Coming Soon
  • יֵשׁוּעַ Yeshua “He saves.” The Hebrew name of Jesus. From yasha (to deliver, to save). H3442 — Coming Soon
ת — Tav
  • תַּרְדֵּמָה Tardemah Deep sleep, supernatural unconsciousness. God-induced trance. Used in Genesis 2:21 (Adam) and Genesis 15:12 (Abraham). H8639

Greek — Ἑλληνική

The language of the New Testament

Α — Alpha
  • ἀγάπη Agape Unconditional, self-sacrificing love. The love of God. Not emotion but covenant action. G26 — Coming Soon
  • ἀρχή Arche Beginning, origin, first cause, ruling principle, authority. Greek translation of reshit. G746
Δ — Delta
  • διαθήκη Diatheke Covenant, testament, will. Greek translation of Hebrew berith. Used 33 times in the NT. The word behind “New Testament.” G1242
Λ — Lambda
  • λόγος Logos The Word; reason, logic, divine speech. In John 1:1, the pre-existent Christ. G3056 — Coming Soon
Π — Pi
  • πνεῦμα Pneuma Spirit, breath, wind. Greek equivalent of Hebrew ruach. G4151 — Coming Soon
Σ — Sigma
  • σώζω Sozo To save, deliver, heal, make whole. Encompasses spiritual salvation, physical healing, and total restoration. G4982 — Coming Soon
