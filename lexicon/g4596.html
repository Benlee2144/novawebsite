σηρικός

sērikós — G4596

Transliteration: sērikós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4596

Derivation: from (an Indian tribe from whom silk was procured;

Definition

hence the name of the silk-worm); Seric, i.e. silken (neuter as noun, a silky fabric)

KJV Translation

silk

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σηρῐκός, ή, όν,
(Σήρ) Seric, silken, ἐσθής [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; παραπετάσματα, σκευή, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; written σειρικός, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]:—as substantive, σηρικόν, τό, silken robe, silk, [NT+1st c.BC+]
__2 σηρικά, τά, jujubes, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__3 σηρικόν (perhaps συρικόν), τό, a red pigment,[Refs 5th c.BC+]; Syricum pigmentum, quod Syrii Phoenices in Rubri maris litoribus colligunt, [Refs 6th c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Revelation 18:12
