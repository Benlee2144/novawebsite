Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3112

μακράν

makrán — G3112

Transliteration: makrán

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3112

Derivation: feminine accusative case singular of G3117 (μακρός) (G3598 (ὁδός) being implied);

Definition

at a distance (literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

(a-)far (off), good (great) way off

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μακράν,
Ionic dialect μακρήν, accusative feminine of μακρός used as adverb, far, μ. ἀνωτέρω θακῶν [Refs 4th c.BC+]; μ. λελειμμένος left far behind,[Refs 5th c.BC+]; τοὔργον οὐ μ. λέγεις the business you speak of is not far to seek, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: with genitive, far from, βαρβάρου χθονός [Refs 5th c.BC+] to a greater distance, [Refs 5th c.BC+] as far as possible, with genitive loci, [Refs]
__2 μακρὰν λέγειν speak at length, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II of Time, long, μ. ζῆν, ἀναμένειν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; οὐ μ. shortly, [Refs 5th c.BC+] (but, not at length, [Refs 1st c.BC+]
