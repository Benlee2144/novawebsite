Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G798

ἄστρον

ástron — G798

Transliteration: ástron

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G798

Derivation: neuter from G792 (ἀστήρ);

Definition

properly, a constellation; put for a single star (natural or artificial)

KJV Translation

star

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἄστρον, τό,
mostly in plural, the stars, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τοῦ κατ᾽ ἄστρα Ζηνός, ={τοῦ ἐν οὐρανῷ}, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἄστρων εὐφρόνη, ={εὐφρ. ἀστερόεσσα}, [Refs] probable reading in [Refs 7th c.BC+]; περὶ τὸ ἄ. in the dog-days, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; poetry of the sun, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: seldom of any common star, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of the fixed stars, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ἄστρα πλανητά, opposed to ἀπλανῆ, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to ἐνδεδεμένα, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; opposed to ἀστέρες, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; ἐπὶ τοῖς ἄστροις at the times of the stars' rising or setting, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἄστροις σημαίνεσθαι, τεκμαίρεσθαι, guide oneself by the stars, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; ἄστροις τὸ λοιπὸν ἐκμετρούμενος χθόνα knowing its place only by the stars, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II of something brilliant, admirable, Ἀκροκόρινθον Ἑλλάδος ἄ.[Refs 3rd c.BC+]
