ἐμαυτοῦ

emautoû — G1683

Transliteration: emautoû

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1683

Derivation: genitive case compound of G1700 (ἐμοῦ) and G846 (αὐτός);

Definition

of myself so likewise the dative case , and accusative case

KJV Translation

me, mine own (self), myself

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἐμαυτοῦ, -ῆς, -oῦ
reflex prop. of first of person(s), used only in genitive, dative and accusative sing.,
of myself: Luk.7:7, al.; ἀπ᾽ ἐ, Jhn.5:30 7:17, 28 8:28, 42 10:18 14:10; ὑπ᾽ ἐμαυτόν, Mat.8:9, Luk.7:8
(AS)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐμαυτοῦ, ἐμαυτῆς,
reflexive pronoun of first person, of me, of myself: only genitive, dative, and accusative singular, both masculine and feminine: not found in early Epic dialect; Aeolic dialect ἔμ᾽ αὔτῳ, ἔμ᾽ αὔτᾳ, [Refs 7th c.BC+]; ἐμαυτόν is uncertain in [Refs 6th c.BC+]; Ionic dialect ἐμεωυτοῦ [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐμᾱτοῦ, ἐμᾱτόν, [Refs 1st c.BC+]; ἐν ἐμαυτῷ συννοεῖσθαι in or with oneself, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; but ἐν ἐμαυτοῦ (i.e. οἴκῳ) εἶναι, metaphorically, to be master of oneself, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
