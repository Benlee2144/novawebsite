ἔθος

éthos — G1485

Transliteration: éthos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1485

Derivation: from G1486 (ἔθω);

Definition

a usage (prescribed by habit or law)

KJV Translation

custom, manner, be wont

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἔθος, εος, τό,
(ἔθω) custom, habit, ἔ. τὸ πρόσθε τοκήων (but probably falsa lectio for{ἦθος}) [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐν ἔθει τῇ πόλει εἶναι to be the habit, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἔ. ἐστίν τινι, with infinitive, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ἔθος ἔχειν, with infinitive, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; ἔθει by habit, habitually, opposed to φύσει, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; δι᾽ ἔθος, opposed to ἐκ γενετῆς, [Refs]; ἐξ ἔθους[Refs 3rd c.AD+]-, cf. Latin suesco; see at {βεσόν}.)
