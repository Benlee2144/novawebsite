πολλάκις

pollákis — G4178

Transliteration: pollákis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4178

Derivation: multiplicative adverb from G4183 (πολύς);

Definition

many times, i.e. frequently

KJV Translation

oft(-en, -entimes, -times)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

πολλάκις [ᾰ],
Epic dialect and Lyric poetry πολλάκι, sometimes in Trag. (only Lyric poetry) for the sake of meter, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; never in Prose: (πολύς): adverb
__I of Time, many times, often, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__II of Degree and Number, π. μυρίοι many tens of thousands, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of Quantity, [τὴν] οὐσίαν π. τοσαύτην ἐποίησε [Refs]; of Size, μεῖζον π. [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__II.2 τὸ π. mostly, for the most part, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; very much, altogether, χρὼς ὅμοιος ἐγίνετο πολλάκι θάψῳ [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__III in Attic dialect, after conditional particles, perhaps, σεισμὸς εἰ γένοιτο π. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐάν τι πολλὰ π. πάθω[Refs 5th c.BC+]; also μὴ π. lest perchance, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
