(also Doric dialect, [Refs 4th c.BC+], η, ον, also ος, ον [Refs 5th c.BC+]: Epic dialect δέελος:
__I properly visible, conspicuous, δέελον δ᾽ ἐπὶ σῆμά τ᾽ ἔθηκε [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__II commonly, clear to the mind, manifest, νῦν δ᾽ ἤδη τόδε δ. [Refs 8th c.BC+]

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

δῆλος, -η, -ον
[in LXX for אוּר, etc. ;]
__1. visible.
__2. clear to the mind, evident: Mat.26:73; δ. (sc. ἐστίν), before ὅτι, 1Co.15:27, Gal.3:11.†
SYN.: φανερός, with ref. to outward appearance, manifest as opposite to concealed; δ. with ref. to inner perception, evident, known, under­stood. (AS)
