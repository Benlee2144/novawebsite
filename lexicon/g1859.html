ἑορτή

heortḗ — G1859

Transliteration: heortḗ

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1859

Derivation: of uncertain affinity;

Definition

a festival

KJV Translation

feast, holyday

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἑορτ-ή,
in Ionic dialect Prose ὁρτή (so [Refs 5th c.BC+], probably in Ion Trag.21, but ἑορτή [Refs 6th c.BC+], ἡ, feast, festival, holiday, ἐπεὶ καὶ πᾶσιν ἑ. [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ὁρτὴν ἄγειν keep a feast,[Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 generally, holiday-making, amusement, pastime, παιδιᾶς καὶ ἑορτῆς χάριν [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 proverbial, κατόπιν ἑορτῆς ἥκειν to have come the day after the fair, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἀεργοῖς αἰὲν ἑορτά every day's a holiday to those who don't work, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__4 assembled multitude at a festival, ὄχλος καὶ ἑ. καὶ στρατὸς καὶ πλῆθος [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
