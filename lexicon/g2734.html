Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2734

κατοπτρίζομαι

katoptrízomai — G2734

Transliteration: katoptrízomai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2734

Derivation: middle voice from a compound of G2596 (κατά) and a derivative of G3700 (ὀπτάνομαι) (compare G2072 (ἔσοπτρον));

Definition

to mirror oneself, i.e. to see reflected (figuratively)

KJV Translation

behold as in a glass

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

κατοπτρ-ίζω,
show as in a mirror or by reflexion, τοῦ-ίζοντος [τὴν ἶριν] ἀστέρος [Refs]:—passive, to be mirrored, [Refs]
__II middle, look into a mirror, behold oneself in it, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II.2 behold as in a mirror, ἰδέαν [NT+1st c.AD+] (but here perhaps reflect).

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Corinthians 3:18

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

κατοπτρίζω
(κάτοπτρον, a mirror),
to show as in a mirror. Mid., to see oneself mirrored (see MM, Exp., xv); with accusative of thing(s) (R, txt., but see mg.), to reflect as a mirror: 2Co.3:18 (cf. Abbott, Essays, 94).†
