καῦμα

kaûma — G2738

Transliteration: kaûma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2738

Derivation: from G2545 (καίω);

Definition

properly, a burn (concretely), but used (abstractly) of a glow

KJV Translation

heat

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

καῦμα, ατος, τό,
(καίω) burning heat, especially of the sun, καύματος ἔξ after sun-heat, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; πρὶν ἂν τὸ κ. παρέλθῃ the heat of the day, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: in plural, also of frost, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 fever heat, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of inflamed conditions, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II in plural, holes burnt by cautery, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III brand on cattle, [Refs]
__IV embers of sacrifices, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__V firewood, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
