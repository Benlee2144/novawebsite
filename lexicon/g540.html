Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G540

ἀπάτωρ

apátōr — G540

Transliteration: apátōr

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G540

Derivation: from G1 (Α) (as a negative particle) and G3962 (πατήρ);

Definition

fatherless, i.e. of unrecorded paternity

KJV Translation

without father

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀπάτωρ [πᾰ], ορος, ὁ, ἡ,
(πατήρ) without father, of deities, αὐτοπάτωρ, ἀ.[NT+4th c.AD+]; fatherless, orphan, ἀοίκους ἀπάτοράς τε[Refs 5th c.BC+]; disowned by the father, [Refs 5th c.BC+] not having me for a father, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 of unknown father, like{σκότιος}, [Refs 1st c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Hebrews 7:3
