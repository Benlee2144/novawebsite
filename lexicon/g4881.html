Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4881

συναπόλλυμι

synapóllymi — G4881

Transliteration: synapóllymi

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4881

Derivation: from G4862 (σύν) and G622 (ἀπόλλυμι);

Definition

to destroy (middle voice or passively, be slain) in company with

KJV Translation

perish with

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

συναπόλλῡμι,
destroy together, μετά τινος [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σ. τοὺς φίλους destroy one's friends as well as oneself, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σ. τὰ Χρήματα lose the money also, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; τινί τι one thing with another, [Refs 1st c.AD+]:—passive, perish together, [NT+5th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Hebrews 11:31
