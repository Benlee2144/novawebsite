βοηθός

boēthós — G998

Transliteration: boēthós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G998

Derivation: from G995 (βοή) and theo (to run);

Definition

a succorer

KJV Translation

helper

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Included with: βοηθ-όος,
Doric dialect βοᾱ-, ον, (βοή, θέω, compare βοη-δρόμος) hasting to the cry for help or the call to arms, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; β. ἅρμα a chariot hasting to the battle, [Refs]
__II aiding, helping, [Refs 5th c.BC+]:— substantive, helper, probably [Refs 3rd c.BC+]:—in Prose βοηθός, όν, assisting, auxiliary, νῆες [Refs 5th c.BC+]; frequently as substantive, assistant, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τοῦ στρατηγοῦ [Refs 3rd c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Hebrews 13:6
