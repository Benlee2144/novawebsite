ὑπόδημα

hypódēma — G5266

Transliteration: hypódēma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5266

Derivation: from G5265 (ὑποδέω);

Definition

something bound under the feet, i.e. a shoe or sandal

KJV Translation

shoe

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὑπόδημ-α, ατος, τό,
(ὑποδέω) sole bound under the foot with straps, sandal, ποσὶν.. ὑποδήματα δοῦσα [Refs 8th c.BC+]; whereas ὑπόδημα κοῖλον is a shoe or half-boot, which covered the whole foot (see. κοῖλος 1.1); ὑπόδημα is sometimes used alone in this sense,[Refs 5th c.BC+]; εἰς ὑποδήματα γράφειν put down as paid for shoes, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; δεξιὸν εἰς ὑ., ἀριστερὸν εἰς ποδάνιπτρα, of one who is ready for anything, perhaps alluding to Theramenes (see. κόθορνος 3)[Refs 5th c.BC+]; similar words are ascribed to [Refs 4th c.BC+].ιά (where ὑπόδησις is used); τὸ ὑ. ἔρραψας μὲν σύ, ὑπεδήσατο δὲ Ἀρισταγόρης [Refs 5th c.BC+]
