Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G136

αἶνος

aînos — G136

Transliteration: aînos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G136

Derivation: apparently a prime word;

Definition

properly, a story, but used in the sense of G1868 (ἔπαινος); praise (of God)

KJV Translation

praise

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

αἶνος, ου, ὁ,
[in LXX for הלל pi, עֹז ;]
poët, and Ion.,
__1. = μῦθος, a tale (Horn., al.).
__2. = Att. ἔπαινος, praise (Horn., al.): Mat.21:16 (LXX), Luk.18:43.
__3. In π., a decree (MM, VGT, see word).

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

αἶνος, ὁ,
(αἰνέω) poetry and Ionic dialect word, tale, story, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; αἰνεῖν αἶνον to tell a tale, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: especially story with moral, fable, [Refs 8th c.BC+]: generally, saying, proverb, παλαιὸς αἶ.[Refs 5th c.BC+]; riddle, [Refs]
__II = Attic dialect ἔπαινος, praise, [NT+8th c.BC+]
__III decree, resolution, τῶν Ἀχαιῶν[Refs]; κατ᾽ αἶνον, opposed to κατὰ ψήφισμα, [Refs]
