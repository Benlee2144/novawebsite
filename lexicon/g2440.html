ἱμάτιον

himátion — G2440

Transliteration: himátion

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2440

Derivation: neuter of a presumed derivative of ennumi (to put on);

Definition

a dress (inner or outer)

KJV Translation

apparel, cloke, clothes, garment, raiment, robe, vesture

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἱμάτιον [ῑμᾰ-], τό,
in form a diminutive of ἷμα (i.e. εἷμα), a piece of dress; in usage always of an outer garment, formed by an oblong piece of cloth worn above the χιτών, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; θοἰμάτιον by crasis for τὸ ἱμ-, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of the Roman toga, [Refs 1st c.AD+], of civilians,= togati, [Refs]; but ἱ. Ἑλληνικόν, opposed to the toga, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 ἱμάτια, τά, generally, clothes, [Refs 6th c.BC+]; of grave-clothes, ἐν εἱμ. τρισὶ [θάπτειν] [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 metaphorically, ἱμάτια πόλεως τείχη [Refs]
__II generally, cloth, [Refs 5th c.BC+] in Attic dialect Inscrr., [Refs 1st c.BC+]
