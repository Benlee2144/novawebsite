Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1041

βωμός

bōmós — G1041

Transliteration: bōmós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1041

Derivation: from the base of G939 (βάσις);

Definition

properly, a stand, i.e. (specially) an altar

KJV Translation

altar

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

βωμός, ὁ,
(βαίνω) raised platform, stand, for chariots, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; base of a statue, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__2 mostly, altar with a base, ἱερὸς β. [Refs 8th c.BC+]; of suppliants, ποτὶ βωμὸν ἵζεσθαι [Refs 8th c.BC+]; βωμοῖσι προσῆσθαι, προσπεσόντα βωμῷ καθῆσθαι, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: also in Prose, β. ἱδρύσασθαι [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 later, tomb, cairn, [Refs]
__4 title of poems by Dosiades and Besantinus, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__5 altar-shaped cake, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__6 Ζεὺς Βωμός, probably a Syrian god, [Refs]
__7 central fire in the system of Philolaus, accusative to [Refs]
__8 in plural, ={ἔμβολοι}, [Refs 5th c.AD+]
