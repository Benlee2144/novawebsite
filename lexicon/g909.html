βαπτισμός

baptismós — G909

Transliteration: baptismós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G909

Derivation: from G907 (βαπτίζω);

Definition

ablution (ceremonial or Christian)

KJV Translation

baptism, washing

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

βαπτ-ισμός, ὁ,
dipping in water, immersion, [NT+2nd c.AD+]
__2 metaphorically, εἰς κακίας β. οἰχήσεται [Refs]
__3 lethargic sleep, Archig.and [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
__4 baptism, [Refs 1st c.AD+]

Appears In (5 verses)

Colossians 2:12Hebrews 6:2Hebrews 9:10Mark 7:4Mark 7:8

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

βαπτισμός, -οῦ, ὁ (βαπτίζω), prop., the act of which βάπτισμα is the result;
__1. a dipping, washing, lustration: Mrk.7:4; of Jewish ceremonial. Heb.9:10; in Heb.6:2, βαπτισμῶν διδαχήν (-ῆς), "the pl. and the peculiar form seem to be used to include Christian baptism with other lustral rites" (Westc, in l.).
__2. baptism: FlJ, Ant., 18, 5, 2 (of John's baptism), and some Fathers (see Soph., see word). Not so in NT, unless ἐν τ. βαπτισμῷ, in the act of baptism, Col.2:12, be read with Tr. (Rec., WH, R, -ματι).
