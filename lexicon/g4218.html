Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4218

ποτέ

poté — G4218

Transliteration: poté

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4218

Derivation: from the base of G4225 (πού) and G5037 (τέ);

Definition

indefinite adverb, at some time, ever

KJV Translation

afore-(any, some-)time(-s), at length (the last), (+ n- )ever, in the old time, in time past, once, when

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Included with: πότε,
Ionic dialect κότε, Doric dialect πόκα [Refs 3rd c.BC+]:—interrogative Particle used in direct and indirect questions, when? at what time? [Refs 8th c.BC+]; πότ᾽ ἆρ; ={πότε} with ἆρα [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II indefinite ποτε (Attic dialect, also [Refs 4th c.BC+], Ionic dialect κοτε, Doric dialect ποκα, Aeolic dialect ποτα [Refs 7th c.BC+]
__II.1 at some time or other, at some time, τάχ᾽ ἄν ποτε θυμὸν ὀλέσσῃ [Refs 8th c.BC+]; π. καὶ ἄλλοτε at certain other times, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 in hypothetical clauses, questions, etc., at any time, ever, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; compare εἴ ποτε: also with relatives (compare δήποτε), Ζεὺς ὅστις ποτ᾽ ἐστίν [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ὅποι ποτέ, ὅπου π., etc., [Refs 5th c.BC+]; after πω, πη, see at {πώποτε, πήποκα}: very frequently with negatives, οὔτε ποτ᾽..
