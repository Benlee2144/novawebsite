Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3470

μῶμος

mōmos — G3470

Transliteration: mōmos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3470

Derivation: perhaps from G3201 (μέμφομαι);

Definition

a flaw or blot, i.e. (figuratively) disgraceful person

KJV Translation

blemish

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μῶμος, ὁ,
blame, reproach, disgrace, μῶμον ἀνάψαι to set a brand upon one, [Refs 8th c.BC+]: also in later Prose, as [LXX+1st c.BC+]
__2 blemish, [LXX+NT]
__II personified, [Refs 8th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Peter 2:13

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

μῶμος, -ου, ὁ
[in LXX, of physical blemishes : Lev.21:17 ff., Deu.15:21, Sng.4:7, al. (מאוּם); of mental defect, \2, al. ;]

__2. In LXX, perb. because of resemblance to a physical blemish (cf. ἄμωμος, 1Pe.1:19, and see Hort., in l); metaphorically, of licentious persons, 2Pe.2:13.†
