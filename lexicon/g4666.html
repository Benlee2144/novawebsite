🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σμύρνα,
frequently written ζμύρνα as in [Refs 3rd c.BC+], myrrh, the gum of an Arabian tree, Balsamodendron Myrrha (itself called σμύρνα [Refs 2nd c.AD+], used for embalming the dead, [NT+5th c.BC+]; called σμύρνης ἱδρώς by [Refs 5th c.BC+]; burnt as incense, βωμὸς ἀτμίζων πυρὶ σμύρνης σταλαγμούς [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὑποθυμιῆν σ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; used as an unguent or salve, σμύρνῃ κατάλειπτος [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II Indian bdellium, Balsamodendron Mukul, [Refs 3rd c.BC+] môrâh; compare κιννάμωμον.)
