ἔκγονον

ékgonon — G1549

Transliteration: ékgonon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1549

Derivation: neuter of a derivative of a compound of G1537 (ἐκ) and G1096 (γίνομαι);

Definition

a descendant, i.e. (specially) grandchild

KJV Translation

nephew

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἔκγονος, ον
(η, ον [Refs 5th c.BC+], Doric dialect, [Refs 4th c.BC+], etc.:— born of, sprung from, especially substantive, child, whether son or daughter, [Refs 8th c.BC+] (anapaest meter): plural, ἔκγονοι descendants, [Refs 5th c.BC+], etc.; ἐκγόνων ἔκγονοι children's children, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; also of interest as the child of the principal, [Refs]
__b grandchild, [Refs]
__2 neuter, ἔκγονά τινος one's offspring, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; [ποιηταὶ] ἔ. ἑαυτῶν καταλείπουσιν [Refs 5th c.BC+]; compare ἔγγονος.

Appears In (1 verse)

1 Timothy 5:4
