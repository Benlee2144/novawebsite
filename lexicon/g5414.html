φόρτος

phórtos — G5414

Transliteration: phórtos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5414

Derivation: from G5342 (φέρω);

Definition

something carried, i.e. the cargo of a ship

KJV Translation

lading

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

φόρτος, -ου, ὁ
(φέρω),
a load: esp. of a ship's cargo (Hom., Hdt., and late prose writers), Act.27:10, Rec.†
(AS)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

φόρτος, ὁ,
(φέρω) load, freight, cargo, [Refs 8th c.BC+], and later Prose, as [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; ἐποιήσαντό με φ., explained as πεπραγμάτευμαι, προδέδομαι, φόρτος γεγένημαι, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; φ. ἔρωτος, of Europa on the bull, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
__2 metaphorically, heavy load or burden, φ. χρείας, κακῶν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; compare φορτίον.
__II Attic dialect, vulgar stuff, rubbish, balderdash, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III mass of detail, 'stuff', in semi-colloquial sense, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
