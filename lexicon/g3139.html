μάρμαρος

mármaros — G3139

Transliteration: mármaros

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3139

Derivation: from (to glisten);

Definition

marble (as sparkling white)

KJV Translation

marble

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μάρμᾰρος, ὁ,
a crystalline rock, which sparkles (μαρμαίρει) in the light, μάρμαρος ὀκριόεις [Refs 8th c.BC+]: as adjective, πέτρος μ. ὀκριόεις [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__II later, marble, μάρμαρον ἢ λίθον λευκήν [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 work in marble, i.e. tombstone, τυκτὴ μ. [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__II.3 chips made by cutting marble (masculine), [Refs 1st c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Revelation 18:12
