μίασμα

míasma — G3393

Transliteration: míasma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3393

Derivation: from G3392 (μιαίνω) (\

Definition

(morally) foulness (properly, the effect)

KJV Translation

pollution

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μί-ασμα [ῐ], ατος, τό,
(μιαίνω) stain, defilement, especially by murder or other crime, taint of guilt, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II that which defiles, pollution, of persons, χώρας μ. καὶ θεῶν ἐγχωρίων [Refs]; πατροκτόνον μ. καὶ θεῶν στύγος, of Clytaemnestra, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: in Prose more generally, πνεῦμα μεμιασμένον νοσηροῖσι μιάσμασι [Refs 5th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Peter 2:20
