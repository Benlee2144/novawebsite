Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3039

λικμάω

likmáō — G3039

Transliteration: likmáō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3039

Derivation: from , the equivalent of (a winnowing fan or basket);

Definition

to winnow, i.e. (by analogy), to triturate

KJV Translation

grind to powder

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

λικμ-άω,
future -ήσω [Refs 5th c.BC+]: aorist ἐλίκμησα [Refs 5th c.BC+]:— part the grain from the chaff, winnow, ἀνδρῶν λικμώντων [Refs 8th c.BC+]; καρπὸν ἀπ᾽ ἀσταχύων B.[same place]: metaphorically, scatter like chaff, [LXX]; make away with,[LXX]; crush, destroy, ἐλίκμησάν μου τὸ λάχανον [Refs 3rd c.AD+]; ἐφ᾽ ὃν δ᾽ ἂν πέσῃ (i.e. ὁ λίθος) λικμήσει αὐτόν [NT]
