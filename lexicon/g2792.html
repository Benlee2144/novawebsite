🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

κιννᾰμωμ-ον, τό,
= Hebrew [kudot]innamon, a superior kind of cassia, Cinnamomum Cassia, [Refs 5th c.BC+], etc.: κῐνάμωμον, [Refs 2nd c.AD+], also in codices of [Refs 1st c.BC+], variant in Hdt.[same place]; compare κίνναμον.
__II name of a fabulous Indian bird, said to make its nest of twigs of κιννάμωμον (compare κινναμολόγος), [Refs 4th c.BC+]
