ἀπάγχομαι

apánchomai — G519

Transliteration: apánchomai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G519

Derivation: from G575 (ἀπό) and (to choke;

Definition

akin to the base of G43 (ἀγκάλη)); to strangle oneself off (i.e. to death)

KJV Translation

hang himself

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀπάγχω,
strangle, throttle, ὁ μὲν λάε νεβρὸν ἀπάγχων[Refs 8th c.BC+]; ὃ μάλιστά μ᾽ ἀπάγχει chokes me with anger, [Refs 5th c.BC+] —middle, aorist ἀπηγξάμην, and passive, hang oneself, to be hanged, [Refs 7th c.BC+]am ready to choke, [Refs 5th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Matthew 27:5
