Strong’s: G1393

Definition

gazelle; Dorcas, a Christian woman

KJV Translation

Dorcas

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

δορκάς, άδος [ᾰ], ἡ,
(δέρκομαι, δέδορκα) an animal of the deer kind (so called from its large bright eyes), in Greece, roe, Cervus capreolus, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; in Syria and Africa, gazelle, Antilope dorcas, [Refs 5th c.BC+]—Other forms:—δόρξ, δορκός, ἡ, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: δόρκος, ὁ, [Refs 1st c.AD+]: δόρκων, ωνος, ὁ, Palamed. cited in [LXX+3rd c.BC+]: ζορκάς (see. above): ζόρξ, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]: ἴορκος, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
