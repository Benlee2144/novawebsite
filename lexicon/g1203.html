δεσπότης

despótēs — G1203

Transliteration: despótēs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1203

Derivation: perhaps from G1210 (δέω) and (a husband);

Definition

an absolute ruler (\

KJV Translation

Lord, master

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

δεσπότ-ης, ου, ;
vocative δέσποτᾰ: Ionic dialect accusative δεσπότεα [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—master, lord, properly the master of the house, δόμων [Refs 4th c.BC+]: plural, of a family, [Refs]; in respect of slaves, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὦναξ δέσποτα[Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 despot, absolute ruler, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of the Roman Emperors, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__3 of the gods, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__4 dominant planet, [Refs]
__II generally, master, lord, owner, κώμου, ναῶν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἑπτὰ δεσποτῶν, of the seven [Refs 8th c.BC+]

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

δεσπότης, -ου, ὁ
[in LXX chiefly for אָדוֹן, אֲדֹנָי; in \2, δ. τ. οὐρανῶν κ. τ. γῆς];
a master, lord, correlative of δοῦλος, οἰκέτης: 1Ti.6:1, 2, 2Ti.2:21, Tit.2:9, 1Pe.2:18; as title of God, voc., δέσποτα (so usually in LXX), Luk.2:29, Act.4:24; ὁ δ. = voc. δέσποτα (cf. B1., § 33, 4), Rev.6:10; of Christ, 2Pe.2:1, Jude 4, R, txt. (but cf. mg.).†
SYN.: κύριος (which see), implying limitation of authority and a more general relation than δ., which "denoted absolute ownership and uncontrolled power" (Thayer). (AS)
