Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3902

παράσημος

parásēmos — G3902

Transliteration: parásēmos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3902

Derivation: from G3844 (παρά) and the base of G4591 (σημαίνω);

Definition

side-marked, i.e. labelled (with a badge (figure-head) of a ship)

KJV Translation

sign

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

παράσημ-ος, ον,
(σῆμα) marked amiss or falsely, counterfeit, especially of money, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; δόξα π. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; δύναμις π. αἴνῳ power falsely stamped with praise, I. e. praised by a wrong standard, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 of words and phrases, false, incorrect, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; Ἀττικῶν παράσημος (to be read -σήμων) ἕν (i.e. βιβλίον) [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; τὸ π. eccentricity of style, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__3 marked by the side, noted, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; π. τινί marked, notorious for a thing, [Refs]; π. ἐπιτηδεύων τι remarked as studying it, [Refs]; τὸ π. φεύγουσαι (of women) conspicuousness, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__4 indicative, with genitive, τὸ π. ὄνομα τῆς πρὸς ὑμᾶς ἔχθρας [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__II adverb -μως with false accent, [Refs]
__II.2 with a distinguishing prefix, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
