Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1456

ἐγκαίνια

enkaínia — G1456

Transliteration: enkaínia

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1456

Derivation: neuter plural of a presumed compound from G1722 (ἐν) and G2537 (καινός);

Definition

innovatives, i.e. (specially) renewal (of religious services after the Antiochian interruption)

KJV Translation

dedication

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐγκαίν-ια, τά,
(καινός) feast of renovation or consecration, [LXX+2nd c.AD+]; especially that established by Judas Maccabaeus at the reconsecration of the Temple, [NT]

Appears In (1 verse)

John 10:22
