ὀρεινός

oreinós — G3714

Transliteration: oreinós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3714

Derivation: from G3735 (ὄρος);

Definition

mountainous, i.e. (feminine by implication, of G5561 (χώρα)) the Highlands (of Judæa)

KJV Translation

hill country

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὀρεινός, ή, όν,
(ὄρος) mountainous, hilly, χώρη [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to πεδινός, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to πεδιάς, [Refs 4th c.BC+]hill-country, [Refs]
__II of or from the mountains, dwelling on the mountains, οἱ ὀ. Θρᾶκες [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of birds, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of plants, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; τὸ ἄγριον καὶ τὸ ὀ. his wild and mountain nature, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III in Egypt, belonging to the edge of the desert, especially as epithet of canals, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
