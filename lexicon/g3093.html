Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3093

Μαγδαλά

Magdalá — G3093

Transliteration: Magdalá

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3093

Derivation: of Chaldee origin (compare H04026);

Definition

the tower; Magdala (i.e. Migdala), a place in Palestine

KJV Translation

Magdala

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Μαγαδάν
Magadan, an unidentified place on the coast of the Sea of Galilee: Mat.15:39 (Rec. Μαγδαλά; cf. Mrk.8:10, where for Δαλμανουθά, D* has Μελεγαδά, D1 Μαγαιδά, some cursives Μαγαδά, and Euseb. Onomast. Μεγαιδάν; cf. DB, iii, see word).†
Μαγδαλά (Aram. מַגְדְֺּלָא = Heb. H4026, which in Jos.15:37 B is rendered Μαγαδά),
Magdala: Mat.15:39 (Rec. for Μαγαδάν, which see).†
(From Abbott-Smith. LSJ has no entry)
