ἀκροθίνιον

akrothínion — G205

Transliteration: akrothínion

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G205

Derivation: from G206 (ἄκρον) and (a heap);

Definition

properly (in the plural) the top of the heap, i.e. (by implication) best of the booty

KJV Translation

spoils

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀκρο-θίνιον [θῑ], τό,
[Refs 5th c.BC+]; mostly plural ἀκρο-θίνια or ἀκρό-θῑνα, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: singularἄκρο-θις, ἡ, accusative -θινα[Refs 4th c.BC+]: (ἄκρος, θίς):—topmost or best part of heap; hence, firstfruits of the field, booty, etc., offered to the gods, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἀκρόθινα πολέμου, in [Refs 5th c.BC+], of the Olympic games, as founded from spoils taken in war, compare [Refs].—Properly neuter adjective, [Refs 4th c.BC+] offerings of firstfruits. Post-[Refs 8th c.BC+]
