παιδίον

paidíon — G3813

Transliteration: paidíon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3813

Derivation: neuter diminutive of G3816 (παῖς);

Definition

a childling (of either sex), i.e. (properly), an infant, or (by extension) a half-grown boy or girl; figuratively, an immature Christian

KJV Translation

(little, young) child, damsel

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

παιδί-ον
(paroxytone), τό, diminutive of παῖς (never in Trag.), little or young child (up to 7 years, accusative to [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐκ παιδίου from a child, [Refs 5th c.BC+] 'chip of the old block', [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__II young slave, male or female, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III τὸ παιδίον, a disease of children, probably convulsions, uncertain reading in [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III.3 παίδι-ος, ὁ, barbarism for {παιδιόθεν}, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
