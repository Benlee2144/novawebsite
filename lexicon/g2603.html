Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2603

καταβραβεύω

katabrabeúō — G2603

Transliteration: katabrabeúō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2603

Derivation: from G2596 (κατά) and G1018 (βραβεύω) (in its original sense);

Definition

to award the price against, i.e. (figuratively) to defraud (of salvation)

KJV Translation

beguile of reward

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

καταβρᾰβεύω,
give judgement against one as βραβεύς, and so, deprive one of the prize, deprive one of one's right, with accusative, [NT+8th c.BC+]passive, ὑπὸ Μειδίου καταβραβευθέντα being cast in his suit by means of Meidias, Test. cited in [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 with genitive, rule over, ἡ γῆ κ. τῶν λοιπῶν [Refs 2nd c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Colossians 2:18
