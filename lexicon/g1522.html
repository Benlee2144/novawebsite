Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1522

εἰσακούω

eisakoúō — G1522

Transliteration: eisakoúō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1522

Derivation: from G1519 (εἰς) and G191 (ἀκούω);

Definition

to listen to

KJV Translation

hear

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

εἰσᾰκούω,
hearken or give ear to one, ὡς ἔφατ᾽· οὐδ᾽ ἐσάκουσε.. Ὀδυσσεύς [Refs 8th c.BC+]; give way, yield to a request, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of God, τινός or τῆς προσευχῆς [LXX]:—so in passive, of the prayer, [NT]; of the person, [NT]
__2 in Poets, simply, hear, τούτου λέγοντος εἰσήκουσ᾽ ἐγώ, ὡς.. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τίνος βροτῶν λόγον τόνδ᾽ εἰ.; [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 perceive, feel effect of, τοῦ ἐγκεφάλου ἐσακούσαντος τοῦ τρώματος [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II passive in strict sense, ἔξωθεν εἰς τὰς οἰκίας εἰσακούεται μᾶλλον ἢ ἔσωθεν ἔξω [Refs 4th c.BC+]

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

είσ-ακούω
[in LXX chiefly for שָׁמַע, also for עָנָה, etc. ;]
to listen to, in two senses;
__(a) to obey: 1Co.14:21 (cf. Deu.1:43, \2)
__(b) to listen, assent to; pass., to be heard: of persons praying, Mat.6:7, Heb.5:7; of the prayer offered, Luk.1:13, Act.10:31 (cf. Psa.4:2, \2); see Cremer, 624).†
