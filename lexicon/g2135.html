εὐνοῦχος

eunoûchos — G2135

Transliteration: eunoûchos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2135

Derivation: from (a bed) and G2192 (ἔχω);

Definition

a castrated person (such being employed in Oriental bed-chambers); by extension an impotent or unmarried man; by implication, a chamberlain (state-officer)

KJV Translation

eunuch

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

εὐνοῦχος, ὁ,
(εὐνή, ἔχω) castrated person, eunuch, employed to take charge of the women and act as chamberlain (whence the name, ὁ τὴν εὐνὴν ἔχων), [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 of animals, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__3 of dates, without stones, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II as adjective, watching the bed, sleepless, λαμπάδες εὐνούχοισιν ὄμμασιν [Refs 5th c.BC+]
