Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4388

προτίθεμαι

protíthemai — G4388

Transliteration: protíthemai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4388

Derivation: middle voice from G4253 (πρό) and G5087 (τίθημι);

Definition

to place before, i.e. (for oneself) to exhibit; (to oneself) to propose (determine)

KJV Translation

purpose, set forth

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

προτίθημι
(also thematic forms, 3rd.pers. singular προτιθεῖ [Refs 5th c.BC+], 3rd.pers. plural προτιθεῖσι [Refs], future -θήσω[Refs 5th c.BC+]: aorist προὔθηκα [Refs 8th c.BC+] —middle (see. below):—passive, aorist 1 προὐτέθην [Refs 5th c.BC+]; but present and imperfect passive are usually supplied by πρόκειμαι:—set before, set out, especially of meals, τραπέζας νίζον καὶ πρότιθεν (Epic dialect for προὐτίθεσαν) [Refs 8th c.BC+]:—middle, set before oneself, have set before one, δαῖτα [Refs 1st c.AD+]; also προτίθεσθαί τισι ἄριστον cause it to be set before them, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__b π. τινὰ κυσίν throw him to the dogs, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τινὰ θηρσὶν ἁρπαγὴν π. [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__c hand over for burial, τινά τινι [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 expose a child, [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, ὁ θανάτῳ προτεθείς [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 set up, institute, propose, especially of contests, ἅμιλλαν λόγων [Refs 5th c.BC+]
