ἐξουθενέω

exouthenéō — G1848

Transliteration: exouthenéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1848

Derivation: a variation of G1847 (ἐξουδενόω) and meaning the same

KJV Translation

contemptible, despise, least esteemed, set at nought

ἐξ-ουθενέω, -ῶ
(οὐθείς, which see), [in LXX (see: ἐξουθενέω): 1Ki.2:30, al., and as seel. for -δενέω, -όω, -θενόω* ;]
to set at nought, despise utterly, treat with contempt: with accusative of person(s), Luk.18:9 23:11, Rom.14:3, 10, 1Co.16:11; with accusative of thing(s), Gal.4:14, 1Th.5:20; pass., of persons: Mrk.9:12 (T, -όω), 1Co.6:4; of things λίθος, Act.4:11 (LXX ἀπεδοκίμασαν); λόγος, 2Co.10:10; τὰ ἐξουθενημένα, 1Co.1:28.†
SYN.: ἀθετέω, καταφρονέω (see DCG, i, 453b) (AS)
