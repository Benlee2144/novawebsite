υἱοθεσία

huiothesía — G5206

Transliteration: huiothesía

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5206

Derivation: from a presumed compound of G5207 (υἱός) and a derivative of G5087 (τίθημι);

Definition

the placing as a son, i.e. adoption (figuratively, Christian sonship in respect to God)

KJV Translation

adoption (of children, of sons)

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

υἱο-θεσία, ἡ,
adoption as a son, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; in a religious sense, πνεῦμα υἱοθεσίας [NT]; frequently in Inscrr., in the phrase καθ᾽ υἱοθεσίαν, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

υἱοθεσία, -ας, ἡ
(cf. the cl. phrases, υἱὸν τίθεσθαι, θετὸς υἱός), frequently in Inscr. (see Deiss., BS, 239), adoption of a son (or daughter); metaphorically, of God's relation established
__(a) with Israel: Rom.9:4;
__(b) with Christ­ians: Rom.8:15, Gal.4:5, Eph.1:5; of its consummation, Rom.8:23.†
