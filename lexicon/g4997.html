σωφροσύνη

sōphrosýnē — G4997

Transliteration: sōphrosýnē

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4997

Derivation: from G4998 (σώφρων);

Definition

soundness of mind, i.e. (literally) sanity or (figuratively) self-control

KJV Translation

soberness, sobriety

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σωφροσύνη [ῠ],
Doric dialect σωφροσ-ύνα, Epic dialect and poetry σᾰοφροσύνη (as in [Refs 8th c.BC+] and in later poetry, [Refs], ἡ, soundness of mind, prudence, discretion, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; the common form first in [Refs 6th c.BC+]; sanity, opposed to μανία, [NT+5th c.BC+]
__2 moderation in sensual desires, selfcontrol, temperance, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σ. τὸ περὶ τὰς γυναῖκας (i.e. ἔργον) [NT+5th c.BC+]
__3 in a political sense, a moderate form of government, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
