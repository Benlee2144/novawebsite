τοιόσδε

toiósde — G5107

Transliteration: toiósde

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5107

Derivation: (including the other inflections); from a derivative of 5104 and G1161 (δέ);

Definition

such-like then, i.e. so great

KJV Translation

such

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

τοιόσδε, άδε
(Ionic dialect ήδε), όνδε, a form of τοῖος, bearing the same relation to τοιοῦτος as ὅδε to οὗτος, such as this, in [Refs 8th c.BC+] and Attic dialect much more so; sometimes antecedent to οἷος, as ἀοιδοῦ τοιοῦδ᾽ οἷος ὅδ᾽ ἐστί [Refs 8th c.BC+] here am I such as you see, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; frequently with implications, so great, so bad, etc.; οὔ κε κακοὶ τοιούσδε τέκοιεν [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τοιάδε λαίφεα such clothes, i. e. so bad, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; to ὅς, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; rarely to a conjunction, as ὡς, [Refs 4th c.BC+]: with a qualifying word, τοιόσδ᾽ ἠμὲν δέμας ἠδὲ καὶ ἔργα [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἐν τῷ τοιῷδε in such circumstances, [Refs 5th c.BC+] in such wise, [NT+5th c.BC+] such a one, [Refs 5th c.BC+], frequently in Attic dialect, [Refs 5th c.BC+] is, properly, as follows, τοιαῦτα as afore
