πλαστός

plastós — G4112

Transliteration: plastós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4112

Derivation: from G4111 (πλάσσω);

Definition

moulded, i.e. (by implication) artificial or (figuratively) fictitious (false)

KJV Translation

feigned

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

πλαστός, ή, όν,
formed, moulded, especially in clay or wax, γυνή [Refs 8th c.BC+]; π. εἰκών statue, opposed to painting, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__2 that can easily be moulded, plastic, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; in a thick paste, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II metaphorically, fabricated, forged, counterfeit, ἐκ λόγου πλαστοῦ [Refs 5th c.BC+]; π. βακχεῖαι sham inspirations, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; πλαστὸς πατρί a supposititious son, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; π. ἐπιχείρημα hypothetical case, [Refs 2nd c.AD+] adverb -τῶς, opposed to ὄντως[Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to ἀληθῶς, [Refs]; π. ὀδυρόμενοι feignedly, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III see at {πλατός}.
