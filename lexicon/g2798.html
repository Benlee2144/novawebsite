Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2798

κλάδος

kládos — G2798

Transliteration: kládos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2798

Derivation: from G2806 (κλάω);

Definition

a twig or bough (as if broken off)

KJV Translation

branch

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

κλάδος [ᾰ], ου, ὁ,
branch, shoot of a tree, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; twig, opposed to ἀκρεμών, [Refs 4th c.BC+]: generally, branch, τῆς ἐλαίης τοὺς κλάδους [Refs 5th c.BC+]: presented by suppliants, ἐλαίας θ᾽ ὑψιγέννητον κλάδον [Refs 5th c.BC+]; also of laurel branches used in temples, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 plank, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
__3 branch of a blood-vessel, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__4 metaphorically, ἀπὸ νώτοιο δύο κλάδοι ἀΐσσονται two arms, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__5 κ. ἐλέας, of a young girl, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; τῇ κ. [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; compare κλάδα[ν]· κλάδον, [Refs 5th c.AD+]; genitive plural κλαδέων probably in [Refs 5th c.BC+], Epic dialect κλαδέεσσι [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
