Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G812

ἀτακτέω

ataktéō — G812

Transliteration: ataktéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G812

Derivation: from G813 (ἄτακτος);

Definition

to be (i.e. act) irregular

KJV Translation

behave self disorderly

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀτακτ-έω,
of a soldier, to be undisciplined, opposed to εὐτακτέω, [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, πολλὰ γὰρ ἠτάκτητο αὐτοῖς[Refs 1st c.AD+]: generally, neglect one's duty, fail to discharge obligation, [NT+5th c.BC+]
__2 generally, lead a disorderly life, [Refs 5th c.BC+] to desert it, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__3 raise a riot or rebellion, [Refs 4th c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Thessalonians 3:7

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἀτακτέω -ῶ
(ἄτακτος),
primarily, of soldiers marching, to be out of order, to quit the ranks; hence, metaphorically, to be remiss, fail in the performance of duty (in π., of truancy on the part of an apprentice): 2Th.3:7 (on ἀ. and its cognates, see M, Th., 152 ff.; MM, s.vv.).†
