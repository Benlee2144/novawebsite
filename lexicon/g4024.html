περιζώννυμι

perizṓnnymi — G4024

Transliteration: perizṓnnymi

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4024

Derivation: from G4012 (περί) and G2224 (ζώννυμι);

Definition

to gird all around, i.e. (middle voice or passive) to fasten on one's belt (literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

gird (about, self)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

περι-ζώννυμι,
gird upon a person, π. τὸ ξίφος [τινί] [Refs 1st c.AD+]; gird, τὸ παιδίον probably in [Refs 2nd c.AD+]:—more frequently in middle with perfect passive, gird oneself with, ᾤαν λουτρίδα Theopomp. Comedy texts [Refs]; ἐσθῆτα, τήβεννον, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; γυμνὸς ὢν τοῦτον τὸν ἄνδρα περιεζώσατο put him on as a defence, παρ᾽ ὑπόνοιαν for ἐπίτροπον ἐποιήσατο, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; περιεζῶσθαι τὴν φορβειάν to have their halter girded round them. [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of a cook, περιεζωσμένος with his apron on, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of athletes, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; of a dancer, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
