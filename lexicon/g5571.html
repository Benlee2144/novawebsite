Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5571

ψευδής

pseudḗs — G5571

Transliteration: pseudḗs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5571

Derivation: from G5574 (ψεύδομαι);

Definition

untrue, i.e. erroneous, deceitful, wicked

KJV Translation

false, liar

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ψευδής, ές
(the neuter singular ψευδές is not found in early writers, ψεῦδος being used instead, compare ψεῦδος 111; it is found in later Gr., [Refs 1st c.AD+]; old Attic dialect accusative plural ψευδᾶς [Refs]: (ψεύδομαι):— lying, false, untrue, of things, opposed to ἀληθής, ψ. λόγοι [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τρέπεσθαι ἐπὶ ψευδέα ὁδόν to betake oneself to falsehood, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ψ. κατηγορία, αἰτίαι, false charges, [Refs 5th c.BC+] are also fallacies, in Logic, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ἥδε ἡ ψ. οὐσία this unreal Being (i.e. the world of sense), [Refs 3rd c.AD+]: irregular superlative ψευδίστατος, εἴδη [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 of persons, lying, false, and as substantive, liar, οὐ γὰρ ἐπὶ ψευδέσσι πατὴρ Ζεὺς ἔσσετ᾽ ἀρωγός [Refs 8th c.BC+]; perhaps ψεύδεσσι from ψεῦδος[Refs 5th c.BC+]; ψ. φανήσεσθαι to be detected in falsehood, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: irregular superlative ψευδίστατος arrant liar, [Refs]
__3 τὰ ψευδῆ falsehoods, li
