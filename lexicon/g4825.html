Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4825

σύμβουλος

sýmboulos — G4825

Transliteration: sýmboulos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4825

Derivation: from G4862 (σύν) and G1012 (βουλή);

Definition

a consultor, i.e. adviser

KJV Translation

counsellor

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σύμβουλ-ος, ὁ,
adviser, counsellor, in public or private affairs, [NT+5th c.BC+]: with genitive person, one's adviser, [Refs 5th c.BC+], advise, with infinitive, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II as a title,
__II.1 at Athens, the θεσμοθέται were empowered to appoint σύμβουλοι (perhaps in a private capacity), [Refs]
__II.2 at Sparta, a board of advisers sent with the general, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.3 officers at Thurii, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II.4 = Latin legatus, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; also used to explanation Latin con-sul, [Refs 1st c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Romans 11:34
