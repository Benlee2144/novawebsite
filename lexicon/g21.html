ἀγαλλιάω

agalliáō — G21

Transliteration: agalliáō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G21

Derivation: from agan (much) and G242 (ἅλλομαι);

Definition

properly, to jump for joy, i.e. exult

KJV Translation

be (exceeding) glad, with exceeding joy, rejoice (greatly)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀγαλλι-άω,
late form of ἀγάλλομαι, rejoice exceedingly, [NT+3rd c.AD+]: future -άσομαι[LXX]: aorist ἠγαλλιᾱσάμην[LXX+NT]—This family of words seems also to have been used in malam partem, ἀγαλλιάζει· λοιδορεῖται, ἀγάλλιος· λοίδορος, ἀγαλμός· λοιδορία, [Refs 5th c.AD+]

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἀγαλλιάω, -ῶ, Hellenistic form of cl. ἀγάλλω, to glorify, mid. -ομαι, to exult in;
[in LXX (most frequently in Pss.) chiefly for גיל, רנן pi. ;]
to exult, rejoice greatly: before ἐπί, with dative, Luk.1:47; with dative mod., 1Pe.1:8, Rev.19:7. Mid., with same sense: Mat.5:12, Luk.10:21, Act.2:26, 16:34, 1Pe.4:13; before ἵνα, Jhn.8:56; ἐν, Jhn.5:35 (1 aor. pass. perh. as mid.; but see Mozley, Psalter, 5), 1Pe.1:6 (Cremer, 590).†
