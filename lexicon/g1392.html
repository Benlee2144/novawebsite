δοξάζω

doxázō — G1392

Transliteration: doxázō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1392

Derivation: from G1391 (δόξα);

Definition

to render (or esteem) glorious (in a wide application)

KJV Translation

(make) glorify(-ious), full of (have) glory, honour, magnify

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

δοξάζω
(δόξα), [in LXX chiefly for כָּבַד ni., pi., also for פָּאַר, etc. ;]
__1. to think, suppose, hold an opinion (Æsch., Plat., al.).
__2. To bestow δόξα (which see) on, to magnify, extol, praise (Thuc., iii, 45; Plut., al.): with accusative, Mat.5:16, Mrk.2:12, Jhn.8:54, Rom.15:6, 1Pe.2:12, al.; id. before ἐπί Luk.2:20; ἐν, Gal.1:24, 1Pe.4:16.
__3. In LXX and NT (see: δόξα, 3), to clothe with splendour, glorify (Exo.34:29-30, Psa.37:20, Isa.44:23, Est.3:1, al): Rom.8:30, 2Co.3:10, 2Th.3:1, 1Pe.1:8, al.; of Christ, Jhn.7:39 8:54, al. (on the Johannine use, see Cremer, 211; Westc., Jo., Intr.); of the Father, Jhn.13:31-32, 1Pe.4:11, al. (cf. ἐν-, συν-δοξάζω).
