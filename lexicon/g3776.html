οὐσία

ousía — G3776

Transliteration: ousía

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3776

Derivation: from the feminine of G5607 (ὤν);

Definition

substance, i.e. property (possessions)

KJV Translation

goods, substance

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

οὐσί-α,
Ionic dialect -ιη[Refs 5th c.BC+]; Doric dialect ἐσσία, ὠσία (which see): ἡ: (ὀντ-, participle of εἰμί sum):—that which is one's own, one's substance, property, [Refs 5th c.BC+][same place], etc.; opposed to τὰ σώματα (civil status), [Refs 5th c.BC+]; εἰ ἐκεκτήμην οὐ. if I had been a man of substance, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; φανερὰ οὐσία real property, immovables, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to ἀφανής, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; frequently of estates in Egypt, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
__II in Philos., like Ionic dialect φύσις (with which it is interchanged in various uses, e. g. [Refs 5th c.BC+], stable being, immutable reality, opposed to γένεσις, ὅτιπερ πρὸς γένεσιν οὐσία, τοῦτο πρὸς πίστιν ἀλήθεια [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὁδὸς εἰς οὐσίαν [Refs]: hence, being in the abstract, opposed to non-being (τὸ μὴ εἶναι), [Refs 5th c.BC+]
