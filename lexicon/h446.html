אֱלִיאָב

ʼĔlîyʼâb — H446

Transliteration: ʼĔlîyʼâb

Pronunciation:

Language: Hebrew

Strong’s: H446

Derivation: from H410 (אֵל) and H1 (אָב); God of (his) father;

Definition

Eliab, the name of six Israelites

KJV Translation

Eliab.

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

A man of the tribe of Levi living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at 1Sa.1.1;
son of: Tohu (H8459);
father of: Jeroham (H3395); also called Eliab at 1Ch.6.27; Eliel at 1Ch.6.34;
Another name of e.li.hu (אֱלִיהוּ "Elihu" H0453G)
§ Eliab = "my God is father" or "God is father"
1) son of Helon, leader of Zebulun in the wilderness
2) a Reubenite chief, father of Dathan and Abiram
3) David's oldest brother
4) a Levite musician
5) a Gadite warrior for David
6) a Kohathite
