Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2799

κλαίω

klaíō — G2799

Transliteration: klaíō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2799

Derivation: of uncertain affinity;

Definition

to sob, i.e. wail aloud (whereas 1145 is rather to cry silently)

KJV Translation

bewail, weep

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

κλαίω
[in LXX chiefly for בָּכָה ;]
of any loud expression of pain or sorrow, esp. for the dead, to weep, lament;
__(a) intrans.: Mrk.5:38-39 14:72 (M, Pr., 131) Mrk.16:10, Luk.7:13, 32 7:38 8:52, Jhn.11:31, 33 16:20 20:11, 13 20:15 Act.9:39 21:13, 1Co.7:30, Php.3:18, Jas.4:9 5:1, Rev.5:5 18:15, 19; πολύ, Rev.5:4; πολλά, Act.8:24 (WH, mg.); πικρῶς, Mat.26:75, Luk.22:62; opposite to γελάω, Luk.6:21, 25; χαίρω, Rom.12:15; before ἐπί, with accusative, Luk.19:41 23:28, Rev.18:9;
