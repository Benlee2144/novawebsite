Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1280

διαπορέω

diaporéō — G1280

Transliteration: diaporéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1280

Derivation: from G1223 (διά) and G639 (ἀπορέω);

Definition

to be thoroughly nonplussed

KJV Translation

(be in) doubt, be (much) perplexed

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

__2 to be in want, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II go through all the ἀπορίαι, [Refs]
__II.2 commonly only a stronger form of ἀπορέω, raise an ἀπορία, start a difficulty, [Refs 4th c.BC+]:—middle, διαπορεῖσθαί τι περί τινος [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, to be matter of doubt or discussion, [Refs 5th c.BC+] a question arises about.., [Refs]
