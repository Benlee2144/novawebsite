ἄνοια

ánoia — G454

Transliteration: ánoia

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G454

Derivation: from a compound of G1 (Α) (as a negative particle) and G3563 (νοῦς);

Definition

stupidity; by implication, rage

KJV Translation

folly, madness

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἄνοια,
Epic dialect ἀνοίη [Refs 6th c.BC+], ἡ:— the character of an ἄνοος, want of understanding, folly, ἀνοίη in folly, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; πολλῆ ἀνοια χρῆσθαι to be a great fool, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἄνοιαν ὀφλισκάνειν to be thought a fool, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; but opposed to μανία, [Refs]: plural, follies, [Refs 5th c.BC+]

Appears In (2 verses)

2 Timothy 3:9Luke 6:11
