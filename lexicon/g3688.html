ὄνος

ónos — G3688

Transliteration: ónos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3688

Derivation: apparently a primary word;

Definition

a donkey

KJV Translation

an ass

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὄνος, ὁ
and ἡ, ass, once in [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ὄνοι οἱ τὰ κέρεα ἔχοντες, together with a number of fabulous animals, [Refs 5th c.BC+] —frequently in proverbs:
__1 ὄ. λύρας (i.e. ἀκούων), of one who can make nothing of music, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ὄ. κάθηται, of one who sits down when caught in the game of ὀστρακίνδα, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; the two proverbs combined by [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 περὶ ὄνου σκιᾶς for an ass's shadow, i.e. for a trifle, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 ὄνου πόκαι or πόκες, see at {πόκος} [Refs]; ὄνον κείρεις, of those who attempt the impossible, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__4 ἀπ᾽ ὄνου καταπεσεῖν, of one who gets into a scrape by his own clumsiness. with a pun on ἀπὸ νοῦ πεσεῖν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__5 ὄνος ὕεται an ass in the rain, of being unmoved by what is said or done, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__6 ὄ. ἄγω μυστήρια, i.e my part is to carry burdens, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__7
