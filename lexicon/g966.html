Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G966

Βηθσαϊδά

Bēthsaïdá — G966

Transliteration: Bēthsaïdá

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G966

Derivation: of Chaldee origin (compare H01004 and H06719);

Definition

fishing-house; Bethsaida, a place in Palestine

KJV Translation

Bethsaida

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Βηθσαιδά (Rec. -σαϊδά), and -δάν (Mt, Mk, ll. with), ἡ, indecl. (Syr., house of fish),
Bethsaida, a town on NE. shore of the Sea of Galilee: Luk.9:10. It is generally supposed that a second B. on the W. shore is referred to in Mat.11:21, Mrk.6:45 8:22, Luk.10:13, Jhn.1:45 12:21 (DB, i, 282f.; but see Swete on Mrk.6:45).
__2. 2. Βηθζαθά, ἡ, indecl. (perh. Aram. בֵּית זַיְתָה, house of olives) Jhn.5:2..†
(From Abbott-Smith. LSJ has no entry)
