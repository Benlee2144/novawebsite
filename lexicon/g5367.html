φίλαυτος

phílautos — G5367

Transliteration: phílautos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5367

Derivation: from G5384 (φίλος) and G846 (αὐτός);

Definition

fond of self, i.e. selfish

KJV Translation

lover of own self

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

φῐλαυτ-ος, ον,
(αὑτοῦ) loving oneself, in good sense, τὸν ἀγαθὸν δεῖ φ. εἶναι [Refs 4th c.BC+]: more frequently in bad sense, selfish, [NT+4th c.BC+]; τὸ φ. ={φιλαυτία}, [Refs 1st c.AD+] adverb -τως[Refs 2nd c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Timothy 3:2

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

φίλ-αυτος, -ον
loving oneself (Arist.); in bad sense (ib.), selfish: 2Ti.3:2.†
SYN.: αὐθάδης (see Tr., Syn., § xciii) (AS)
