ἀνθίστημι

anthístēmi — G436

Transliteration: anthístēmi

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G436

Derivation: from G473 (ἀντί) and G2476 (ἵστημι);

Definition

to stand against, i.e. oppose

KJV Translation

resist, withstand

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀνθ-ίστημι,
set against, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἀ. τροπαῖον set up a trophy in opposition, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; weigh against, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἀ. τινὸς τὴν ὁλκήν outweigh him, [LXX]
__2 match with, compare, ἀντιστῆσαι καὶ παραβαλεῖν[Refs 1st c.AD+]
__II [Refs 8th c.BC+]passive, with intransitive aorist 2 ἀντέστην. aorist 1 passive ἀντεστάθην[Refs 5th c.BC+]: perfect ἀνθέστηκα[NT]; Attic dialect contraction participle ἀνθεστώς[Refs 5th c.BC+]: future ἀντιστήσομαι[Refs 5th c.BC+]:—stand against, especially in battle, withstand, Ἥρῃ δ᾽ ἀντέστη.. Ἄρτεμις[Refs 8th c.BC+]
__II.2 of things, turn out unfavourably to one, ἀντιστάντος αὐτῷ τοῦ πράγματος[Refs 5th c.BC+]
