ἀπειπόμην

apeipómēn — G550

Transliteration: apeipómēn

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G550

Derivation: reflexive past of a compound of G575 (ἀπό) and G2036 (ἔπω);

Definition

to say off for oneself, i.e. disown

KJV Translation

renounce

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀπεῖπον,
infinitive ἀπειπεῖν, Epic dialect ἀπο?~Xειπεῖν, ἀπο?~Xειπέμεν, and participle ἀποειπών, i.e. ἀποϝειπών, [Refs 8th c.BC+]: less frequently aorist I ἀπεῖπα[Refs 5th c.BC+]future in use is ἀπερῶ, perfect ἀπείρηκα, mostly in signification [Refs]:—middle, aorist I ἀπειπάμην[Refs 5th c.BC+], but never in correct Attic dialect:—passive, future ἀπορρηθήσομαι[Refs 5th c.BC+]: aorist ἀπερρήθην[Refs 5th c.BC+] —present and imperfect are supplied by ἀπαυδάω, ἀπόφημι, and in Attic dialect Prose by ἀπαγορεύω:—speak out, tell out, declare, μῦθον[Refs 8th c.BC+]; μνηστήρεσσιν ἀπειπέμεν (probably μνηστῆρσ᾽ ἀποειπέμεν) to give them full notice,[Refs 8th c.BC+]; ῥῆσιν ἀπερέοντα to deliver a verbal message, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: in aorist middle, ἀπείπασθαι θανάτῳ ζημιοῦν τοὺς πλευσομένους[Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II deny, refuse, ὑπόσχεο καὶ κατάνευσον, ἢ ἀπόειπ᾽[Refs 8th c.BC+]; σύμφαθι ἢ ἄπειπε[Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III forbid [Refs] forbid one to do, tell him not to do, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
