ὡραῖος

hōraîos — G5611

Transliteration: hōraîos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5611

Derivation: from G5610 (ὥρα);

Definition

belonging to the right hour or season (timely), i.e. (by implication) flourishing (beauteous (figuratively))

KJV Translation

beautiful

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὡραῖος, α, ον,
produced at the right season (ὥρα), seasonable, timely: especially of the fruits of the earth, βίος or βίοτος ὡ. store of fruits gathered in due season, [Refs]; ὡ. καρποί the fruits of the season, καρποὺς.. κατατίθεσθαι ὡραίους to store them up in season, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὡραῖα.. ἀποτελεῖν ἱερά to render fruits of the season as sacred offerings, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σῦκα [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; also of animals, ὡ. ἄρνες yearling, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; of tunnies at a year old (from six months to one year they were called πηλαμύδες), πηλαμὺς.. ὡραία θέρους τῷ Βοσπορίτῃ [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὡ. θύννοι Ps.-[Refs 8th c.BC+]; τάριχος ὡ. fish salted or pickled in the season, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; σαργάναι ὡ. pickling-tubs, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]: hence generally, agricultural produce, εἶναι ἐνεχυρασίαν Αἰξωνεῦσιν ἐκ τῶν ὡ. τῶν ἐκ τοῦ χωρίου [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 τὰ ὡραῖα, ={τὰ καταμήνια}, especially at their first appearance, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 substantive ἡ ὡραία (in full, ὥρη ἡ ὡραίη [Refs 5th c.BC+],
