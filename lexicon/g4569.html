Σαῦλος

Saûlos — G4569

Transliteration: Saûlos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4569

Derivation: of Hebrew origin, the same as G4549 (Σαούλ);

Definition

Saulus (i.e. Shaul), the Jewish name of Paul

KJV Translation

Saul

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σαῦλος, η, ον,
an adjective descriptive of gait and carriage, defined as τὸ φαῦλον καὶ διερρυηκός by [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σαῦλα ποσὶν βαίνουσα, applied to the gait of the tortoise, straddling, waddling, [Refs]; of the loose, wanton gait of courtesans or Bacchantes, σ. βαίνειν [Refs 6th c.BC+]; also of a prancing horse, σ. βαίνειν, ἵππος ὡς κορωνίης [Refs 7th c.BC+] σαῦλος, η, ον,
