ἁμάρτημα

hamártēma — G265

Transliteration: hamártēma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G265

Derivation: from G264 (ἁμαρτάνω);

Definition

a sin (properly concrete)

KJV Translation

sin

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἁμάρτ-ημα, ατος, τό,
failure, fault, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; frequently in Attic dialect Prose, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; midway between ἀδίκημα and ἀτύχημα, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; sinful action, opposed to κατόρθωμα, [Refs 5th c.BC+]

Appears In (5 verses)

1 Corinthians 6:18Mark 3:28Mark 3:29Mark 4:12Romans 3:25

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἁμάρτημα, -τος, τό
(ἁμαρτεῖν, see supr.), [in LXX for חַטָּאָה, עָוֺן etc. ;]
an act of disobedience to divine law (Lft., Notes, 273), a sinful deed, a sin: Mrk.3:28-29, Rom.3:25, 1Co.6:18, 2Pe.1:9, WH, mg.; αἰώνιωv ἀ. (DCG, i, 788a), Mrk.3:29 (for exx. from π., see MM, VGT, see word).†
SYN.: ἀγνόημα, ἁμαρτία, ἀνομία, ἀσέβεια, ἥττημα, παράβασις, παρακοή, παρανομία, παράπτωμα (ν. Cremer, 100; Tr., Syn., § lxvi; DB, iv, 532; DCG, l.with; Westc, Eph., 165 f.) (AS)
