ἐπίκειμαι

epíkeimai — G1945

Transliteration: epíkeimai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1945

Derivation: from G1909 (ἐπί) and G2749 (κεῖμαι);

Definition

to rest upon (literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

impose, be instant, (be) laid (there-, up-)on, (when) lay (on), lie (on), press upon

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐπίκειμαι,
serving as passive to ἐπιτίθημι, to be laid upon, and so,
__I. of doors, to be put to or closed [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__I.2. generally, to be placed, lie in or on, with dative, ἐπισκύνιον ἐπέκειτο προσώπῳ [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__I.3. of islands, νῆσοι ἐπὶ Λήμνῳ (-ου codices) ἐπικείμεναι lying off Lemnos, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐπὶ [τῇ Λακαίνῃ χώρῃ[Refs] the islands off the coast, [Refs]; πάσῃ ἐ. τῇ θαλάσσῃ lies right across the sea, of Crete, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
__II. to be laid upon, ἐμοὶ σφρηγὶς ἐπικείσθω τοῖσδ᾽ ἔπεσι [Refs 6th c.BC+]; ἐπίκειται ἀγνώμων σῇ κεφαλῇ στέφανος [NT+8th c.BC+]; of a duty, οἷς ἐπέκειτο φροντίζειν 1st-2nd c.AD: Plutarchus Biographus et Philosophus 2.786f.
__II.2. press upon, be urgent in entreaty, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; press upon a retreating enemy, attack, Βοιωτοῖσι [Refs]; to be urgent against, [Refs]; ἐπεκείμην αὐτοῖς ἐνοχλῶν “PLips.” 36.7 [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of a crowd, ἐ. τινί NT.Luke.5.1.
__II.
