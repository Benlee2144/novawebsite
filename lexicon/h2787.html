Lexicon • Hebrew • Strong’s H2787

חָרַר

chârar — H2787

Transliteration: chârar

Pronunciation:

Language: Hebrew

Strong’s: H2787

Derivation: a primitive root;

Definition

to glow, i.e. literally (to melt, burn, dry up) or figuratively (to show or incite passion)

KJV Translation

be angry, burn, dry, kindle.

Appears In (7 verses)

Ezekiel 15:4Ezekiel 24:10Isaiah 24:6Jeremiah 6:29Job 30:30Psalms 102:3Psalms 69:3

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

1) to burn, be hot, be scorched, be charred
1a) (Qal)
1a1) to be hot, be scorched
1a2) to burn, be burned
1b) (Niphal)
1b1) to be scorched, be burned
1b2) to burn
1b3) to be dry, be angry
1c) (Pilpel) to cause to burn
Aramaic equivalent: cha.rakh (חֲרַךְ "to singe" H2761)
