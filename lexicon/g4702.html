σπόριμος

spórimos — G4702

Transliteration: spórimos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4702

Derivation: from G4703 (σπόρος);

Definition

sown, i.e. (neuter plural) a planted field

KJV Translation

corn(-field)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σπόρῐμος, ον
(ᾱ, ον [Refs]; η, ον, see #1.3), (σπείρω) sown, to be sown, fit for sowing, αὖλαξ [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; γῆ σ. seed-land, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; so ἡ σ (i.e. γῆ) [Refs 4th c.BC+]; τὰ σ. the corn-fields, [NT]; γένοιτο αὐτῷ τὰ σ. ἄσπορα Tab.Defix. in [Refs 5th c.AD+]; πεδίων σπορίμαν βάσιν, i.e. solid corn-fields, [Refs]
__2 σ. σπέρμα fit for sowing or bearing seed, [LXX]
__3 μὴν σ. a month for sowing, [Refs 1st c.AD+] day of conception, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__4 μέτρον σ. a measure of seed-corn, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__II active, αἰδὼς σ.,= τὸ αἰδοῖον, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
