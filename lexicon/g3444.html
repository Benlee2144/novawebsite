μορφή

morphḗ — G3444

Transliteration: morphḗ

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3444

Derivation: perhaps from the base of G3313 (μέρος) (through the idea of adjustment of parts);

Definition

shape; figuratively, nature

KJV Translation

form

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μορφ-ή, ἡ,
form, shape, twice in [Refs 8th c.BC+] thou hast comeliness of words, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; so probably ἄλλος μὲν.. εἶδος ἀκιδνότερος πέλει ἀνήρ, ἀλλὰ θεὸς μορφὴν ἔπεσι στέφει God adds a crown of shapeliness to his words, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; μορφῆς μέτρα shape and size, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; μορφῆς σχῆμα, τύπωμα, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὀνειράτων ἀλίγκιοι μορφαῖσιν[Refs 5th c.BC+]; of plants, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; especially with reference to beauty of form, ὑπέρφατον μορφᾷ [Refs 5th c.BC+]; οἷς ποτιστάξῃ χάρις εὐκλέα μ.[Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 generally, form, fashion, appearance, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; outward form, opposed to εἶδος, ἑκατέρω τῶ εἴδεος πολλαὶ μ. [
