ἐκκλησία

ekklēsía — G1577

Transliteration: ekklēsía

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1577

Derivation: from a compound of G1537 (ἐκ) and a derivative of G2564 (καλέω);

Definition

a calling out, i.e. (concretely) a popular meeting, especially a religious congregation (Jewish synagogue, or Christian community of members on earth or saints in heaven or both)

KJV Translation

assembly, church

⚠ Our Analysis Differs

Εκκλησία meant “a called-out assembly” in everyday Greek — a civic gathering, not a religious building or institution. The word “church” comes from the Germanic kirche, not from this Greek word at all. See our study.
