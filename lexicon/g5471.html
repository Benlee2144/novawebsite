χαλκεύς

chalkeús — G5471

Transliteration: chalkeús

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5471

Derivation: from G5475 (χαλκός);

Definition

a copper-worker or brazier

KJV Translation

coppersmith

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

χαλκ-εύς, έως, ὁ:
plural χαλκεῖς, Attic dialect -ῆς[Refs 5th c.BC+]:—coppersmith, opposed to τέκτων (joiner, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__2 generally, worker in metal, of a goldsmith, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; of a worker in iron. [Refs]; hence later, blacksmith, smith (χαλκέας τοὺς τὸν σίδηρον ἐργαζομένους [LXX+5th c.BC+]
__II John Dory, Zeus faber, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; distinguished from χαλκίς, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Timothy 4:14
