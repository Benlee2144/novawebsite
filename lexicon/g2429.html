ἱκμάς

hikmás — G2429

Transliteration: hikmás

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2429

Derivation: of uncertain affinity;

Definition

dampness

KJV Translation

moisture

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἰκμ-άς, άδος, ἡ,
moisture, e.g. of oily leather, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἀνιεὶς ἐκ τοῦ σώματος ἰκμάδα, of a corpse exposed to the sun, [Refs 5th c.BC+] of moisture in the soil, [NT]; also θανόντων ἰσὶν οὐκ ἔνεστ᾽ ἰκμάς no blood, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of the bodily humours, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of all kinds of animal juices or moist secretions, τὸ περίττωμα τῆς ὑγρᾶς ἰ. ὃν καλοῦμεν ἱδρῶτα [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ἡ τῶν καταμηνίων ἰ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἰ. Βάκχου, i.e. wine, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
