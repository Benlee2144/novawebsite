horse

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἵππος, ὁ,
horse, ἡ, mare, most frequently feminine in Poets; in full θήλεες ἵπποι [Refs 8th c.BC+] team of chariot-horses, [Refs 8th c.BC+]: hence, of the chariot itself, ἀφ᾽ ἵπποιιν, ἀφ᾽ ἵππων, from the chariot, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἵππων ἐπιβησόμενος intending to mount his chariot, [Refs]; opposed to πεζοί, πλῆτο δὲ πᾶν πεδίον πεζῶν τε καὶ ἵππων [Refs 8th c.BC+]; of riders, νῶθ᾽ ἵππων ἐπιβάντες ἐθύνεον [Refs 8th c.BC+]; frequently of race-horses, ἵ. ἀκαμαντόποδες [Refs 5th c.BC+]., of ships, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__2 the constellation [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__3 title of Hecate in the Mithraic cult, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
__4 perhaps an instrument of torture, Latin eculeus, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__II as Collective Noun, ἵππος, ἡ, horse, cavalry, ἡ τῶν Θεσσαλῶν ἵ. [Refs 5th c.BC+] a thousand horse, [
