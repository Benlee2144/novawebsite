Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4485

ῥῆγμα

rhēgma — G4485

Transliteration: rhēgma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4485

Derivation: from G4486 (ῥήγνυμι);

Definition

something torn, i.e. a fragment (by implication and abstractly, a fall)

KJV Translation

ruin

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ῥῆγ-μα, ατος, τό,
(ῥήγνυμι) breakage, fracture, joined with σπάσμα, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; with στρέμμα (a strain), [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 laceration, rupture, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__3 rent, tear, in clothes, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__4 cleft, chasm, ῥ. τῆς γῆς [Refs 4th c.BC+]; chink, ἐν τοίχοις [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; breach in a dyke, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__II lesion or rupture of tissue, ὅταν ὑπὸ βίης διαστέωσιν αἱ σάρκες ἀπ᾽ ἀλλήλων [Refs 5th c.BC+]:hence ῥηγμᾰτίας, ου, ὁ, one who has such a rupture, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; but ῥηγματίας πλεύμονος perhaps = pleurisy, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ῥηγμᾰτώδης, ες, [Refs]

Luke 6:49
