στοιβάς

stoibás — G4746

Transliteration: stoibás

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4746

Derivation: from a primary (to \

Definition

a spread (as if tramped flat) of loose materials for a couch, i.e. (by implication) a bough of a tree so employed

KJV Translation

branch

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

στῐβάς, άδος, ἡ,
(στείβω) bed of straw, rushes, or leaves, whether strewn loose [NT], or stuffed into a mattress, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__b straw strewn at a sacrifice, hence as name of the ceremony, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 mattress, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; especially one used by soldiers, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 generally, bed, [Refs]
__4 nest or lair of mice, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of the fish φυκίς, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
