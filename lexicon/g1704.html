Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1704

ἐμπεριπατέω

emperipatéō — G1704

Transliteration: emperipatéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1704

Derivation: from G1722 (ἐν) and G4043 (περιπατέω);

Definition

to perambulate on a place, i.e. (figuratively) to be occupied among persons

KJV Translation

walk in

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐμπερι-πᾰτέω,
walk about in, [ἐμβάταις] [Refs 1st c.AD+]; ἐ. ἐν ὑμῖν tarry among you, [LXX+NT]: absolutely, walk about, ἅμα τῷ συμποσίῳ [Refs 2nd c.AD+] walk several times to and fro, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
__II walk about upon, τὴν ὑπ᾽ οὐρανόν (i.e. γῆν) [LXX]; trample on, [Refs]: metaphorically, insult, τινί [Refs 1st c.AD+]
