🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὠρύομαι [ῡ],
future -ύσομαι[LXX]: aorist ὠρῡσάμην [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—Ionic dialect and poet. Verb, very rarely used in Attic dialect (see. below), howl, properly of wolves and dogs, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; of lions, roar, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; of animals generally, [LXX+1st c.AD+]; ὄρθιον ὤρυσαι Pi.[same place],[LXX]; of primitive folk, either in mourning, [Refs 5th c.BC+], or in joy, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of the sea, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II transitive, howl over, τῆνον μὰν θῶες, τῆνον λύκοι ὠ. [Refs 3rd c.BC+]—The active [Refs] (Sanskrit rauti (plural ruvanti), ruváti 'bellow', Latin ru-mor, Slavonic rev-ą, raju-ti.)
