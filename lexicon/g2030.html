ἐπόπτης

epóptēs — G2030

Transliteration: epóptēs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2030

Derivation: from G1909 (ἐπί) and a presumed derivative of G3700 (ὀπτάνομαι);

Definition

a looker-on

KJV Translation

eye-witness

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐπόπτ-ης, ου, ὁ,
(ἐπόψομαι) overseer, watcher, especially of a god, Πυθῶνος [LXX+5th c.BC+]; title of Poseidon, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; of the Sun, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; δαίμονες ἐ. τῶν ἀνθρωπίνων [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐ. γῆς καὶ θαλάσσης, of Pompey, [Refs]; of Augustus, [Refs]; ἐ. εἰρήνης, of a police magistrate, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
__2 simply, spectator, πόνων [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__3 inspector, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
__II one admitted to the highest grade of the mysteries, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; τινος [NT]

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Peter 1:16
