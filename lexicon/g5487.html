Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5487

χαριτόω

charitóō — G5487

Transliteration: charitóō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5487

Derivation: from G5485 (χάρις);

Definition

to grace, i.e. indue with special honor

KJV Translation

make accepted, be highly favoured

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

χαρῐτ-όω,
show grace to any one, τῆς χάριτος ἧς ἐχαρίτωσεν ἡμᾶς [NT]middle χαριτώσομαι I will bestow favour upon thee, [Refs 4th c.AD+]:—passive, to have grace shown one, to be highly favoured, [LXX+NT]; πρὸς πάντας ἀνθρώπους[Refs 4th c.AD+]; ὄμμα στροφαῖς -ούμενον probably in [Refs 4th c.AD+]

(χάρις), [in LXX: \2 (ἀνδρί κεχαριτωμένῳ; Vg., justificato; Syr., saintly) * ;]
to endow with χάρις (which see), i.e.
__1. (a) to make graceful;
__(b) to make gracious (Sir, l.with).
