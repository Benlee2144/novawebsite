ὄντως

óntōs — G3689

Transliteration: óntōs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3689

Derivation: adverb of the oblique cases of G5607 (ὤν);

Definition

really

KJV Translation

certainly, clean, indeed, of a truth, verily

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὄντως,
adverb participle of εἰμί (sum), really, actually, verily, with Verbs, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὄντως τε καὶ ἀληθῶς really and truly, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to to ὡς ἔπος εἰπεῖν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; to εἰκότως, [Refs 5th c.BC+]participle ὤν, οὖσα, ὄν, to imply real existence, [Refs]: also with Nouns, τὰ ὄντως ἀγαθά [Refs 5th c.BC+]; more freq in the later dialogues of [Refs 5th c.BC+]
