Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3340

μετανοέω

metanoéō — G3340

Transliteration: metanoéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3340

Derivation: from G3326 (μετά) and G3539 (νοιέω);

Definition

to think differently or afterwards, i.e. reconsider (morally, feel compunction)

KJV Translation

repent

⚠ Our Analysis Differs

The traditional definition above adds “repentance from sin” and “involving amendment” — but these are theological interpretations, not what the Greek word means. μετανοέω literally means “to change one’s mind.” The phrase “repent of your sins” does not appear in Scripture.
