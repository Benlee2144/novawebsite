Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5400

φόβητρον

phóbētron — G5400

Transliteration: phóbētron

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5400

Derivation: neuter of a derivative of G5399 (φοβέω);

Definition

a frightening thing, i.e. terrific portent

KJV Translation

fearful sight

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

φόβ-ητρον, τό,
scarecrow, bugbear, terror, [LXX]: elsewhere always in plural terrors, [NT+5th c.BC+]; Τισιφόνης τὰ φ., probably tragic masks of the Furies, [Refs 1st c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Luke 21:11

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

φόβητρον (LTr., WH, -θρον), -ου, τό
(φοβέω), [in LXX: Isa.19:17 (חָגָא) * ;]
that which causes fright, a terror: pl. (as always, exc.Isa, l.with), Luk.21:11 (Hipp., Plat.).†
