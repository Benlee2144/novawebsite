νεφέλη

nephélē — G3507

Transliteration: nephélē

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3507

Derivation: from G3509 (νέφος);

Definition

properly, cloudiness, i.e. (concretely) a cloud

KJV Translation

cloud

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

νεφέλη, ἡ,
(νέφος) cloud, mass of clouds (distinguished from ὁμίχλη, mist or fog, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ν. κυανέη, πορφυρέη, [Refs 8th c.BC+]—Mostly poetry, but found in [Refs 5th c.BC+] [same places], and in late Prose, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__2 metaphorically, νεφέλη δέ μιν ἀμφεκάλυψε κυανέη, of death, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τὸν δ᾽ ἄχεος νεφέλη ἐκάλυψε μέλαινα a cloud of sorrow,[Refs 5th c.BC+]; Κενταύρου φονίᾳ νεφέλᾳ, i.e. with his blood, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; κελαινῶπις ν., of sleep, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II of clouds in urine, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
