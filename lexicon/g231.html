ἁλιεύς

halieús — G231

Transliteration: halieús

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G231

Derivation: from G251 (ἅλς);

Definition

a sailor (as engaged on the salt water), i.e. (by implication) a fisher

KJV Translation

fisher(-man)

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἁλι-εύς
(later written ἁλεεύς[LXX+NT], ὁ, genitive έως, Ionic ῆος, and contraction ἁλιῶς[Refs 5th c.BC+]:—one who has to do with the sea, and so,
__1 fisher, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__2 seaman, sailor, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἐρέτας ἁλιῆας rowers on the sea, [Refs]; as adjective, ἁλιεὺς στρατός [Refs]
__3 a fish, ={βάτραχος}, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
