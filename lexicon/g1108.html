Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1108

γνῶσις

gnōsis — G1108

Transliteration: gnōsis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1108

Derivation: from G1097 (γινώσκω);

Definition

knowing (the act), i.e. (by implication) knowledge

KJV Translation

knowledge, science

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

γνῶσις, εως, ἡ,
seeking to know, inquiry, investigation, especially judicial, τὰς τῶν δικαστηρίων γ. [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 result of investigation, decision, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__II knowing, knowledge, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; opposed to ἀγνωσίη, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to ἄγνοια, [LXX+5th c.BC+]
__II.b higher, esoteric knowledge, [NT]
__II.2 acquaintance with a person, πρός τινα Test. cited in [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II.3 recognizing, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.4 means of knowing, [αἱ αἰσθήσεις] κυριώταται τῶν καθ᾽ ἕκαστα γ. [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__III being known, γνῶσιν ἔχει τι, ={γνωστόν ἐστι}, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III.2 fame, credit, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__IV means of knowing: hence, statement in writing, [Refs 6th c.AD+]
__V ={γνῶμα}, [Refs 5th c.AD+]
