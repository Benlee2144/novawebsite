κακοπάθεια

kakopátheia — G2552

Transliteration: kakopátheia

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2552

Derivation: from a compound of G2256 (ἡμιώριον) and G3806 (πάθος);

Definition

hardship

KJV Translation

suffering affliction

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

κᾰκοπάθ-εια [πᾰ], ἡ,
distress, misery, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of plants or trees, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; strain, stress, on the parts of a machine, [Refs 5th c.BC+] [same place]; ταῖς παρὰ τὴν ἀξίαν νῦν κακοπαθείαις your present unmerited sufferings, [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—later, usually written κᾰκοπαθ-παθία, [NT+3rd c.BC+]; laborious toil, perseverance, [Refs 1st c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

James 5:10
