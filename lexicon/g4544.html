Samos

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Σάμος [ᾰ], ἡ,
“Samos”, the name of several Greek islands:
__1 old name for Κεφαλληνία (which see), [Refs 8th c.BC+]; also called Σάμη,[Refs]; though this, accusative to others, is a town on the island: hence adjective Σαμαῖος, α, ον, [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__2 Σάμος Θρηϊκίη, see at {Σαμοθρᾴκη}.
__3 “Samos”, the large island over against Ephesus, first in [Refs]: hence adjective Σάμιος, α, ον, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἡ Σαμία (i.e. γῆ) [Refs 4th c.BC+]; also Σ. ἀστήρ, clay with medicinal properties, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]:—Σαμιακός, ή, όν, [Refs 5th c.BC+] was an old word signifying a height.)
