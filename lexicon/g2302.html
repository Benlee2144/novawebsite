θέατρον

théatron — G2302

Transliteration: théatron

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2302

Derivation: from G2300 (θεάομαι);

Definition

a place for public show (\

KJV Translation

spectacle, theatre

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

θέᾱτρον,
Ionic dialect θέητρον, τό, (θεάομαι) place for seeing, especiallyfor dramatic representation, theatre, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; as a place of assembly, [NT+5th c.BC+]; θ. κυνηγετικόν, of the Roman amphitheatre, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; εἰς τὸ θ. εἰσφέρειν to bring upon the stage, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τὸ καλὸν τοῦ θ. a good place in the theatre, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 collective for οἱ θεαταί, the spectators, 'the house', [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 ={θέαμα}, spectacle, θ. ἐγενήθημεν τῷ κόσμῳ [NT]
__4 metaphorically, of life, τουτὶ τὸ θ. ὑπεκρίθημεν [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
