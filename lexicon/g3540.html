νόημα

nóēma — G3540

Transliteration: nóēma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3540

Derivation: from G3539 (νοιέω);

Definition

a perception, i.e. purpose, or (by implication) the intellect, disposition, itself

KJV Translation

device, mind, thought

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

νό-ημα, ατος, τό,
Ionic dialect νῶμα [Refs 5th c.BC+] (but νόημα [Refs]: (νοέω):—that which is perceived, perception, thought, τῶν νέες ὠκεῖαι, ὡς εἰ πτερὸν ἠὲ νόημα [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἅμα νοήματι 'in the twinkling of an eye', [Refs 4th c.BC+] lightning survey, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 thought, purpose, idea, design, τοιοῦτον ἐνὶ στήθεσσι νόημα [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__3 in Philos., thought, concept, opposed to sensation, sense-presentation, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; discursive thinking, as the function of διάνοια, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__4 Rhet., thought as expressed in literary form, [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__II understanding, mind, παρέπλαγξεν δὲ νόημα [Refs 8th c.BC+]
