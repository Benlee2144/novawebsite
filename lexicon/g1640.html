ἐλάσσων

elássōn — G1640

Transliteration: elássōn

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1640

Derivation: comparative of the same as G1646 (ἐλάχιστος);

Definition

smaller (in size, quantity, age or quality)

KJV Translation

less, under, worse, younger

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐλάσσ-ων,
Attic dialect ἐλάττων, ον, genitive ονος: superlative ἐλάχιστος (which see): — smaller, less, formed from ἐλαχύς (which see), but serving as comparative to μικρός, δουρηνεκὲς ἢ καὶ ἔλασσον [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τοὔλασσον ἔχειν to have the worse, be worse off, πάντῃ [Refs 6th c.BC+]; ἐλάττω νομίσας τὴν ἀρχὴν ἢ κατὰ τὴν αὑτοῦ φύσιν εἶναι too small for.., [Refs 5th c.BC+]: absolutely, too small, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; below the average in height, [Refs]
__2 with genitive person, worse than, inferior to, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: but with genitive of things, giving way to, subservient to, σιτίων [Refs 5th c.BC+]: absolutely, worse, inferior, τόποι [Refs]
__3 neuter with Preps., περὶ ἐλάσσονος ποιεῖσθαι to consider of less account, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐπ᾽ ἔλαττον (i.e. ἁρμοσθῆναι) [Refs]; δι᾽ ἐλάσσονος at less distance, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
