KJV Translation

Hymenæus

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὑμέναι-ος [ῠ], ὁ,
(Ὑμήν) the wedding or bridal song, sung by the bride's attendants as they led her to the bridegroom's house, [Refs 8th c.BC+], etc.: Aeolic dialect ὐμήνᾰος [Refs 7th c.BC+]: a form ὑμήναιος in [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__2 wedding, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II ={Ὑμήν}, Hymen, the god of marriage, addressed in wedding-songs, frequently in Trag. and Comedy texts (Lyric poetry), Ὑμὴν ὦ Υμέναι᾽ ἄναζ [Refs 5th c.BC+]; Ὑμὴν ὦ Ὑμέναι᾽ Ὑμήν[Refs 5th c.BC+]; Doric dialect Ὑμὰν ὦ Ὑμέναιε [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; hence the two are used as one word, ὑμὴν ὑμέναιον ἀείδων [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
