πυῤῥός

pyrrhós — G4450

Transliteration: pyrrhós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4450

Derivation: from G4442 (πῦρ);

Definition

fire-like, i.e. (specially), flame- colored

KJV Translation

red

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

πυρρός, ά
(Ionic dialect ή), όν, but Trag. and Doric dialect πυρσός, ή (Doric dialect ά), όν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; rare in Prose, [Refs 1st c.AD+]: (πῦρ):— flame-coloured, yellowish-red (πυρρὸν ξανθοῦ τε καὶ φαιοῦ κράσει γίγνεται [Refs 5th c.BC+]. the yolk of an egg, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἡ λευκότης γίγνεται πυρρή [Refs 5th c.BC+] of sediment in urine, [Refs]
__2 especially of persons with red hair, [Refs 6th c.BC+]; of the colour of the first beard, γενειάς A.[same place]; γένυες [Refs 5th c.BC+]; [θρίξ] [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__3 generally, tawny, λέων [NT+5th c.BC+]; τὰ χρώματα τῶν κυνῶν [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__4 of more positive colour, red, χλανίς [LXX+5th c.BC+]; τὸ π. redness, [Refs 5th c.BC+] —_Epic dialect_ _c
