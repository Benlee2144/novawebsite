Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1975

ἐπιπορεύομαι

epiporeúomai — G1975

Transliteration: epiporeúomai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1975

Derivation: from G1909 (ἐπί) and G4198 (πορεύομαι);

Definition

to journey further, i.e. travel on (reach)

KJV Translation

come

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐπιπορ-εύομαι,
travel, ψυχῆς πείρατα οὐκ ἂν ἐξεύροιο πᾶσαν -όμενος ὁδόν [Refs 1st c.AD+]; march, [Refs 2nd c.BC+] traverse, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__2. ={ἐπιπάρειμι} (B) [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; also ἐ. ἐπὶ τὸ πλῆθος come before the assembly, [NT+2nd c.BC+]: metaphorically, go or run through, τῇ διανοίᾳ, τῇ ὄψει, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__3. Astron., reach, τινὶ τῶν κέντρων [Refs 2nd c.AD+]. begins to traverse the Ram, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
__4. ἐ. τὸν ἱστόν, ={ἐποίχομαι} (which see), [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__5. take legal proceedings against a person, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
