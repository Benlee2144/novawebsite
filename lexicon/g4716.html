σταυρός

staurós — G4716

Transliteration: staurós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4716

Derivation: from the base of G2476 (ἵστημι);

Definition

a stake or post (as set upright), i.e. (specially), a pole or cross (as an instrument of capital punishment); figuratively, exposure to death, i.e. self-denial; by implication, the atonement of Christ

KJV Translation

cross

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σταυρός, -oῦ, ὁ
__1. an upright pale or stake (Hom., Hdt., Thuc., al.)
__2. In late writers (Diod., Plut., al.) of the Roman instrument of crucifixion, the Cross: of the Cross on which Christ suffered, Mat.27:32, 40 27:42, Mrk.15:21, 30 15:32, Luk.23:26, Jhn.19:17, 19 19:25, 31, Col.2:14, Heb.12:2; θάνατος σταυροῦ, Php.2:8; τ. αἷμα τοῦ σ., Col.1:20.
