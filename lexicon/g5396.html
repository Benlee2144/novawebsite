φλυαρέω

phlyaréō — G5396

Transliteration: phlyaréō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5396

Derivation: from G5397 (φλύαρος);

Definition

to be a babbler or trifler, i.e. (by implication) to berate idly or mischievously

KJV Translation

prate against

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

φλῠᾱρ-έω,
Ionic dialect φλῠηρέω:— talk nonsense, play the fool, ταῦτα λέγουσι φλυηρέοντες [Refs 5th c.BC+]: with accusative cognate, φάσκοντα.. ἀεροβατεῖν καὶ ἄλλην πολλὴν φλυαρίαν φλυαροῦντα [Refs 5th c.BC+]. with participle, οὐ μὴ φλυαρήσεις ἔχων [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II transitive, prate against, ἡμᾶς [NT]:—passive, φλυαρηθέντες καὶ κατακερτομηθέντες [Refs 1st c.AD+]; to be made a fool of, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
