διάδημα

diádēma — G1238

Transliteration: diádēma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1238

Derivation: from a compound of G1223 (διά) and G1210 (δέω);

Definition

a \

KJV Translation

crown

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

διάδημα, ατος, τό,
(διαδέω) band or fillet: especially band round the τιάρα worn by the Persian king, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; by his successors, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; by kings generally, [LXX+1st c.BC+]
__II Ὀσίριδος δ., ={ἅλιμος}, Ps.[Refs 1st c.AD+]

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

διάδημα, -τος, τό
(διαδέω, to bind round), [in LXX for כֶּתֶר (as Est.1:11), etc. ;]
the band round the τιάρα of a Persian king; a diadem, the badge of royalty: Rev.12:3 13:1 19:12.†
SYN.: στέφανος, the badge of "victory, of valour, of nuptial joy, of festal gladness " (but see M, Th., i, 219; cf. DB, i, 530, 604). (AS)
