χαλκίον

chalkíon — G5473

Transliteration: chalkíon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5473

Derivation: diminutive from G5475 (χαλκός);

Definition

a copper dish

KJV Translation

brazen vessel

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

χαλκίον, τό,
copper vessel, cauldron, kettle, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; χ. θερμαντήριον, ={θερμαντήρ}, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 cymbal, τὸ Δωδωναῖον χ., proverbial of a chatterbox, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__3 bowl of the κότταβος, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__4 copper ticket given to the dicasts, bearing the name of the court in which they were to serve, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__5 piece of copper money, πονηρὰ χαλκία [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II probably diminutive of χάλκη, ={κάλχη}[Refs 4th c.BC+]
