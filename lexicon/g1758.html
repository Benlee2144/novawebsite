ἐνέχω

enéchō — G1758

Transliteration: enéchō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1758

Derivation: from G1722 (ἐν) and G2192 (ἔχω);

Definition

to hold in or upon, i.e. ensnare; by implication, to keep a grudge

KJV Translation

entangle with, have a quarrel against, urge

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἐν-έχω
[in LXX: Gen.49:23 (סָתַם), Eze.14:4, 7, 3Ma.6:10 * ;]
__1. to hold in; pass., to be held, entangled: with dative of thing(s); jig., ζυγῷ δουλείας, Gal.5:1 (cf. MM, Exp., xii); θλίψρεσιν, 2Th.1:4 (cf. ἀσεβείσιν, 3Mac, l.with).
__2. to set oneself against, be urgent against (as Ge, l.with; for con­struction, see Swete, Mk., l.with): Mrk.6:19, Luk.11:53.†
(AS)
