στοιχέω

stoichéō — G4748

Transliteration: stoichéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4748

Derivation: from a derivative of (to range in regular line);

Definition

to march in (military) rank (keep step), i.e. (figuratively) to conform to virtue and piety

KJV Translation

walk (orderly)

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

στοιχέω, -ῶ
(στοῖχος, a row), [in LXX: Ecc.11:6 (כָּשֵׁר) * ;]
to be in rows (of waves, plants, etc., as well as of men), to walk in line (esp. of marching in file to battle; Xen., Cyr., vi, 3, 34, al.). Metaphorical, in late writers, to walk by rule: Act.21:24; with dative, to walk by or in (as a rule of life), Rom.4:12 (cf. MM, xxiii), Gal.5:25 6:16, Php.3:16 (cf. συν-στοιχέω).†
