παραβάτης

parabátēs — G3848

Transliteration: parabátēs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3848

Derivation: from G3845 (παραβαίνω);

Definition

a violator

KJV Translation

breaker, transgress(-or)

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

παρα-βάτης [βᾰ],
poetry and early Attic dialect παραιβάτης [Refs], ου, ὁ, (παραβαίνω I) one who stands beside: properly the warrior or combatant who stands beside the charioteer, ἂν δ᾽ ἔβαν ἐν δίφροισι παραιβάται ἡνίοχοί τε [Refs 8th c.BC+]; feminine παραιβάτις, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__2 light troops (velites) who ran beside the cavalry, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__II (παραβαίνω II.1)transgressor, [Refs 4th c.BC+] (Lyric poetry, in poetry form παρβάτης),[NT+1st c.AD+]
