ἄνεσις

ánesis — G425

Transliteration: ánesis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G425

Derivation: from G447 (ἀνίημι);

Definition

relaxation or (figuratively) relief

KJV Translation

eased, liberty, rest

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἄνεσις,
genitive εως, Ionic dialect ιος, ἡ: (ἀνιημι):— loosening, relaxing, τῶν χορδῶν of the strings, opposed to ἐπίτασις, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; coupled with χάλασις,[Refs 4th c.BC+]; πάγων ἄ., i.e. a thaw, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; of the ebb-tide, [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__2 metaphorically, remission, abatement, κακῶν[Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to θλῖψις, [NT]; λύπης, μοχθηρίας, etc., [Refs 1st c.BC+]; ἄ. φόρων, τελῶν, remission of tribute, taxes, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; of fevers, opposed to παροξυσμός, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__3 relaxation, recreation, opposed to σπουδή, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__4 solution, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__5 ={τὸ τελευταῖον τῆς παρακμῆς} [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II indulgence, licence, ἡδονῶν[Refs 5th c.BC+]; relaxation of custody, [NT]
