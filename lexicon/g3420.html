μνήμη

mnḗmē — G3420

Transliteration: mnḗmē

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3420

Derivation: from G3403 (μιμνήσκω);

Definition

memory

KJV Translation

remembrance

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μνήμ-η,
Doric dialect μνάμα, ἡ, (μνάομαι) remembrance, memory of a person or thing, absolutely or with genitive, κακῶν μ. γίνεται οὐδεμία [Refs 6th c.BC+]; λείπεσθαι ἀθάνατον μ. (i.e. ἑαυτοῦ) [Refs 5th c.BC+]; οἱ ἄνθρωποι πρὸς ἃ ἔπασχον τὴν μ. ἐποιοῦντο made their recollections suit their sufferings, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; μνήμην πεποίηκεν has made [him] remembered, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of the dead, μακαρίας μνήμης, θείας μνήμης, [Refs 6th c.AD+]; κρατίστης μνήμης Wilcken [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; μνήμης ἀρίστης [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
__2 memory as a power of the mind, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; proper to animals, opposed to ἀνάμνησις, of man, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; εἰπεῖν τι μνήμης ἄπο from memory, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; εἰς μ. ἀναληπτέον[Refs 5th c.BC+] all the memories, acts of memory, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; powers of memory, [Refs]
__3 memorial, record, κυπαρίττιναι μνῆμαι εἰς τὸν ἔπειτα χρό

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Peter 1:15

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

μνήμη, -ης, ἡ
(μνάομαι), [in LXX for זֵכֶר, זִכְרוֹן ;]
memory, remembrance, mention: μ. ποιεῖσθαι, with genitive, to remember, 2Pe.1:15 (but in cl., π., μ. π. more frequently = to make mention, and so perh. here, cf. Mayor, in l; and for ex. from π., see Zorell, see word).†
SYN.: μνεία, q.v (AS)
