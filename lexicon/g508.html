ἀνώγεον

anṓgeon — G508

Transliteration: anṓgeon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G508

Derivation: from G507 (ἄνω) and G1093 (γῆ);

Definition

above the ground, i.e. (properly) the second floor of a building; used for a dome or a balcony on the upper story

KJV Translation

upper room

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Included with: ἀνώγαιον
or ἀνώγεον, τό, (ἄνω, γαῖα) anything raised from the ground: the upper floor of a house, used as a granary, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; as a dining-room, [NT]
__2 prison, [Refs]; ἀνάγαιον and ἀνόκαιον are also found in codices,[Refs]
