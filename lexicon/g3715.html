ὄρεξις

órexis — G3715

Transliteration: órexis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3715

Derivation: from G3713 (ὀρέγομαι);

Definition

excitement of the mind, i.e. longing after

KJV Translation

lust

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὄρεξις, εως, ἡ,
(ὀρέγω) general word for all kinds of appetency, conation, including ἐπιθυμία, θυμός, βούλησις, [Refs 4th c.BC+] K.; opposed to φυγή, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; opposed to ἔκκλισις, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__1 with genitive objecti, longing or yearning after a thing, desire for it, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 absolutely, propension, appetency, ὄ. βουλευτική [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ὄ. διανοητική[Refs 4th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Romans 1:27
