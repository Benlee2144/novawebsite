σάρδιος

sárdios — G4556

Transliteration: sárdios

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4556

Derivation: properly, an adjective from an uncertain base;

Definition

sardian (3037 being implied), i.e. (as noun) the gem so called

KJV Translation

sardius

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σάρδιον, τό,
the Sardian stone, [NT+5th c.BC+]; as a seal, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; σάρδια, of female ornaments, [Refs 5th c.BC+]—This stone was of two kinds, the transparent-red or female being our carnelian, the transparent-brown or male our sardine, [Refs 4th c.BC+]:—later λίθος σάρδιος, [Refs 6th c.AD+] in “de An.” 321.10 (plural); σάρδινος λ., [Refs]; σαρδόνιον, [Refs 5th c.AD+]
