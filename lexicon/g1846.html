Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1846

ἐξορύσσω

exorýssō — G1846

Transliteration: exorýssō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1846

Derivation: from G1537 (ἐκ) and G3736 (ὀρύσσω);

Definition

to dig out, i.e. (by extension) to extract (an eye), remove (roofing)

KJV Translation

break up, pluck out

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐξορύσσω,
Attic dialect ἐξορύττω, dig out the earth from a trench, τὸν ἀεὶ ἐξορυσσόμενον χοῦν [Refs 5th c.BC+] — middle, ἐξορύξασθαι χάρακας make oneself a vallum, [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__II dig out of the ground, dig up, τοὺς νεκρούς [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἄγλιθας [Refs 5th c.BC+]; [μορίαν] [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, τοῦ χοὸς τοῦ -ομένου [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; φυτά [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 gouge out, ἐ. αὐτῶν τοὺς ὀφθαλμούς [LXX+5th c.BC+]
__II.3 metaphorically, τὸν ἐξορύσσοντα λόγον τὰ κεκρυμμένα τῶν πραγμάτων [Refs 1st c.AD+]
