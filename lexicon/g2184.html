ἐφήμερος

ephḗmeros — G2184

Transliteration: ephḗmeros

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2184

Derivation: from G1909 (ἐπί) and G2250 (ἡμέρα);

Definition

for a day (\

KJV Translation

daily

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐφήμερ-ος, ον,
Doric dialect ἐπάμ- [Refs 5th c.BC+] (ἐφαμ- [Refs]: (ἡμέρα):—more common form of ἐφημέριος, especially in Prose, living but a day: hence, short-lived, τερπνόν [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 of men, ἐφήμεροι creatures of a day, [Refs 7th c.BC+]
__II for the day, daily, πυρετός [NT+5th c.BC+]: neuter plural as adverb, once a day, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
__III φάρμακον ἐ. killing on the same day, [Refs 1st c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

James 2:15
