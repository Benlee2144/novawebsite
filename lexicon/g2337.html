Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2337

θηλάζω

thēlázō — G2337

Transliteration: thēlázō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2337

Derivation: from (the nipple);

Definition

to suckle, (by implication) to suck

KJV Translation

(give) suck(-ling)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

θηλάζω,
Doric dialect aorist 1 ἐθήλαξα [Refs 3rd c.BC+]: (θηλή):
__I of the mother or nurse, suckle, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: absolutely, give suck, οἱ μαστοί, οἳ οὐκ ἐθήλασαν [NT]:—also in middle, ἐπιμελεῖσθαι, ὅπως μέτριον χρόνον θηλάσονται [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, to be sucked, ὁ δελφὶς.. θηλάζεται ὑπὸ τῶν τέκνων [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II of the young animal, suck, [Refs]; θηλάζων χοῖρος a sucking pig, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; seldom of an infant, [Refs 5th c.AD+]
__II.2 with accusative, λεαίνας μασδὸν ἐθήλαξεν [Refs 4th c.BC+]
