Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3978

πεζεύω

pezeúō — G3978

Transliteration: pezeúō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3978

Derivation: from the same as G3979 (πεζῇ);

Definition

to foot a journey, i.e. travel by land

KJV Translation

go afoot

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

πεζ-εύω,
go or travel on foot, walk, ἐπὶ γαίας πόδα πεζεύων (where πόδα is pleonastic) [Refs 5th c.BC+]; π. περὶ τὴν τροφήν, of certain birds, [Refs]
__2 go or travel by land, opposed to going by sea, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; οἱ πεζεύοντες land-forces, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; π.διὰ τῆς θαλάσσης, of Xerxes passing by his bridge over the Hellespont, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; π. τὴν θάλασσαν pass it like dry land, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; simply, march, pass through, ἀνοδίας [Refs 1st c.AD+]:— passive, ὁ Ἄθως πλείσθω καὶ ὁ Ἑλλήσποντος πεζευέσθω [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; ἡ ἐκ Βρεντεσίου πεζευομένη ὁδός by land, [Refs 1st c.BC+]; πεζεύεται impersonal, ταῖς ἁρμαμάξαις [Refs]
