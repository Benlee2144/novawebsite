🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἄριστον, τό,
morning meal, breakfast, twice in [Refs 8th c.BC+]: later, breakfast was called ἀκράτισμα, and ἄριστον was the midday meal, our luncheon,[Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἄ. αἱρεῖσθαι, ποιεῖσθαι, [Refs 5th c.BC+]. [; contraction from ᾰ (y) ερι-στον, cf. Gothic air, OHG. ēr 'early', Avest. ayar[schwa] 'day'; also ᾱ (y) ερ- in ἦρι, ἠέριο; -στο- from -d-to-, root ed- 'eat'.]
