ἐντυπόω

entypóō — G1795

Transliteration: entypóō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1795

Derivation: from G1722 (ἐν) and a derivative of G5179 (τύπος);

Definition

to enstamp, i.e. engrave

KJV Translation

engrave

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἐν-τυπόω, -ῶ
(τύπος), [in LXX for פִּתּוּחַ, Exo.39:30) A * ;]
to imprint, engrave: pass. ptcp., with dative, 2Co.3:7.†
(AS)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐντῠπ-όω,
carve or mould in or upon, τῷ νομίσματι ἐνετύπωσεν ἀπήνην [Refs 4th c.BC+]; also of a painter, [Refs 1st c.AD+]:—middle, Φειδίαν ἐν μέσῃ τῇ ἀσπίδι τὸ ἑαυτοῦ πρόσωπον ἐντυπώσασθαι [Refs 4th c.BC+]:—passive,[Refs 1st c.AD+]; to be imprinted, of a birth-mark, [Refs 4th c.AD+]; also, to be flattened by pressure, [Refs 2nd c.AD+] is like a piece of carving on the doors, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II metaphorically, τὸ ἰδίωμα τῇ λέξει ἐ. [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
