κάμινος

káminos — G2575

Transliteration: káminos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2575

Derivation: probably from G2545 (καίω);

Definition

a furnace

KJV Translation

furnace

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

κάμῑν-ος [ᾰ], ἡ,
oven, furnace, or kiln, for smelting, baking, burning earthenware and bricks, [Refs 8th c.BC+], etc.: plural, of Hephaestus' forge, [Refs] flue for warming a room, [Refs 2nd c.AD+], compare 10.843; alcove, [LXX]: proverbial of one who ate hot dishes, κάμινος οὐκ ἄνθρωπος [Refs 4th c.BC+]; κάμινον ἔχων ἐν τῷ πνεύμονι, of a drunkard, [Refs]
