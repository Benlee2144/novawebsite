εὐτραπελία

eutrapelía — G2160

Transliteration: eutrapelía

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2160

Derivation: from a compound of G2095 (εὖ) and a derivative of the base of G5157 (τροπή) (meaning well-turned, i.e. ready at repartee, jocose);

Definition

witticism, i.e. (in a vulgar sense) ribaldry

KJV Translation

jesting

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

εὐτρᾰπελ-ία, ἡ,
ready wit, liveliness, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: plural, pleasantries, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ἡ περὶ τὰς παιδιὰς καὶ τὰς ὁμιλίας εὐ. [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__2 rarely in bad sense, ={βωμολοχία}, [NT]

Appears In (1 verse)

Ephesians 5:4
