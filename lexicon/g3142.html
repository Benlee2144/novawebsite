μαρτύριον

martýrion — G3142

Transliteration: martýrion

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3142

Derivation: neuter of a presumed derivative of G3144 (μάρτυς);

Definition

something evidential, i.e. (genitive case) evidence given or (specially), the Decalogue (in the sacred Tabernacle)

KJV Translation

to be testified, testimony, witness

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μαρτύριον [ῠ], τό,
testimony, proof, πρῶτον καὶ μέγιστον μ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; μαρτύριον δέ.., followed by γάρ, here is a proof, namely.., [Refs], etc.; μέγα τόδε μ.,.. γάρ [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II τὰ μ. the tables of the Decalogue, [LXX]
__III shrine of a martyr, [Refs 6th c.AD+]
