Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3765

οὐκέτι

oukéti — G3765

Transliteration: oukéti

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3765

Derivation: from G3756 (οὐ) and G2089 (ἔτι);

Definition

not yet, no longer

KJV Translation

after that (not), (not) any more, henceforth (hereafter) not, no longer (more), not as yet (now), now no more (not), yet (not)

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

οὐκέτι
neg. adv. of time, [in LXX chiefly for עוֹד with neg., לֹא, etc. ;]
no longer, no more: Mat.19:6, Mrk.10:8, Luk.15:19, Jhn.4:42, Rom.6:9, Gal.3:25 Eph.2:19 Heb.10:18, al.; with neg. (to strengthen the negation), Mat.22:46 Mrk.5:3, Act.8:39, al
(AS)

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

οὐκέτι
or οὐκ ἔτι, adverb no more, no longer, no further: and generally, not now, opposed to οὔπω (not yet), frequently in [Refs 8th c.BC+], and Attic dialect; οὐκέτι πάμπαν [Refs 8th c.BC+]; with a word between, οὐ πάμπαν ἔτι [Refs 5th c.BC+]
