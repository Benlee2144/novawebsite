σπλαγχνίζομαι

splanchnízomai — G4697

Transliteration: splanchnízomai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4697

Derivation: middle voice from G4698 (σπλάγχνον);

Definition

to have the bowels yearn, i.e. (figuratively) feel sympathy, to pity

KJV Translation

have (be moved with) compassion

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σπλαγχν-ίζω,
= σπλαγχνεύω, [LXX]:—passive, [Refs 4th c.BC+].
__II middle (with aorist passive -ίσθην), feel pity, compassion, or mercy, [LXX+NT]; οὐκ ἐσπλαγχνίσθη had no mercy, [Refs 6th c.AD+]
