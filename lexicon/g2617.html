Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2617

καταισχύνω

kataischýnō — G2617

Transliteration: kataischýnō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2617

Derivation: from G2596 (κατά) and G153 (αἰσχύνομαι);

Definition

to shame down, i.e. disgrace or (by implication) put to the blush

KJV Translation

confound, dishonour, (be a-, make a-)shame(-d)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

καταισχύν-ω,
future -αισχῠνῶ[Refs 4th c.BC+]:—dishonour, put to shame, μή τι καταισχύνειν πατέρων γένος [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τὴν σὴν οὐ κ. φύσιν I put not thy nature to shame, i.e. show myself not unworthy of thee, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 dishonour a woman, ἀλλοτρίας γυναῖκας [Refs 5th c.BC+]; also of a male, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__3 ὁ μέλλων Χρόνος ἐμὸν καταίσχυνε.. Χρέος covered me with dishonour in that my debt remained unpaid, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__4 ={καταχέζειν},{Χαίτην} [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II middle, feel shame before, θεούς [Refs 5th c.BC+]: —aorist passive, καταισχυνθέντες τὴν ἀρετὴν αὐτῶν [Refs 5th c.BC+]: with infinitive, to be ashamed to.., ἰητρεύειν [Refs 5th c.BC+]; καταισχυνθῆναι.. ὅπως μὴ δόξει.. to be ashamed of being thought.., [Refs 5th c.BC+]
