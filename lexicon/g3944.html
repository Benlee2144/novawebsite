παροίχομαι

paroíchomai — G3944

Transliteration: paroíchomai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3944

Derivation: from G3844 (παρά) and (to depart);

Definition

to escape along, i.e. be gone

KJV Translation

past

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

παρ-οίχομαι
to have passed by; of time, to be gone by: ἐν τ. παρῳχημέναις γενεαῖς, Act.14:16.†
(AS)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

παροίχομαι,
future -οιχήσομαι: perfect παρῴχηκα, Ionic dialect παροίχωκα, and in later writers παρῴχημαι, [NT+5th c.BC+], but with variant{παροιχομένων}:—to have passed by, παρῴχετο γηθόσυνος κῆρ. he passed on, went on his way, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__2 of Time, to be past παροίχωκεν (variant{παρῴχηκεν}) δὲ πλέων νύξ [Refs]; ἡ παροιχομένη νύξ the by-gone night, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; παροιχόμενοι ἀνέρες men of by-gone times, [Refs 5th c.BC+]the past, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; also, the aforesaid, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τοὔστρακον παροίχεται the danger of ostracism has gone by, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3Grammars, ὁ παρῳχημένος [χρόνος] past tense, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; παρῳχημέναι φωναί forms of past tenses, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__IIto be gone, dead, δείματι with fright, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ὅσον παροίχῃ how art thou fallen, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__IIIc.genitive, shrink from, νείκους τοῦδε [Refs 4th c.BC+]; later, neglect, τῶν πραγμάτων [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
