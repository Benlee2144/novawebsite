Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3594

ὁδηγέω

hodēgéō — G3594

Transliteration: hodēgéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3594

Derivation: from G3595 (ὁδηγός);

Definition

to show the way (literally or figuratively (teach))

KJV Translation

guide, lead

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὁδηγ-έω,
lead one upon his way, guide, with accusative person, [NT+5th c.BC+]:—passive, [Refs 1st c.AD+], Vett. Val.359.30:— also ὁδηγ-ετέω, [Refs 4th c.AD+]

Appears In (5 verses)

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ὁδηγέω, -ῶ,
(ὁδηγός) [in LXX chiefly for נוּחַ, also for דֶּרֶךְ hi., הָלַךְ hi., etc. ;]
to lead on one's way, to guide: with accusative of person(s), Mat.15:14, Luk.6:39; id. before ἐπί, Rev.7:17. Metaphorical, to guide, instruct, teach: Act.8:31; before εἰς τ. ἀλήθειαν (ἐν τ. ἀ., WH, mg.), Jhn.16:13 (cf. Psa.25:5).†
