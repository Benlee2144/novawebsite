λῃστής

lēistḗs — G3027

Transliteration: lēistḗs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3027

Derivation: from (to plunder);

Definition

a brigand

KJV Translation

robber, thief

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

λῃστ-ής, οῦ, ὁ,
Ionic dialect ληϊστής, Doric dialect λᾳστής, (ληΐς, ληΐζομαι) robber, pirate, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to κλέπτης, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; especially by sea, buccaneer, later πειρατής, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ληϊστὴς κατεστήκεε Καρχηδονίων he began a course of piracies upon them, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of irregular troops, [Refs]
__II metaphorically, λ. ἐναργὴς τῆς ἐμῆς τυραννίδος [Refs 5th c.BC+]; λῃστὰ λογισμοῦ, of love, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
