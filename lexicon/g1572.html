Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1572

ἐκκαίω

ekkaíō — G1572

Transliteration: ekkaíō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1572

Derivation: from G1537 (ἐκ) and G2545 (καίω);

Definition

to inflame deeply

KJV Translation

burn

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐκκαίω,
Attic dialect ἐκκάω, future -καύσω: aorist I ἐξέκαυσα [Refs 5th c.BC+], but participle ἐκκέαντες [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—burn out, τοὺς ὀφθαλμούς τινος [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, ἐκκάεσθαι τοὺς ὀφθαλμούς to have one's eyes burnt out, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II light up, kindle, τὰ πυρά [Refs 5th c.BC+]; provoke to anger, ἔκ με κάεις [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; inflame with curiosity, excite, τινά [Refs 1st c.AD+]:—passive, to be kindled, burn up, τὸ πῦρ ἐκκάεται [LXX+5th c.BC+]
__II.2 stimulate, τὴν βλάστησιν [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__III scorch, ἐκκαίων ὁ ἥλιος [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of thirst, parch, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Romans 1:27
