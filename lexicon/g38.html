ἁγιασμός

hagiasmós — G38

Transliteration: hagiasmós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G38

Derivation: from G37 (ἁγιάζω);

Definition

properly, purification, i.e. (the state) purity; concretely (by Hebraism) a purifier

KJV Translation

holiness, sanctification

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἁγι-ασμός, ον, ὁ,
consecration, sanctification, [LXX+NT]

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἁγιασμός, -οῦ, ὁ
(ἁγιάζω), [in LXX: Eze.45:4 (מִקְדָּשׁ), \2, etc. ;]
as an active verbal noun in -μός, it signifies properly the process τὸ ἁγιάζειν, rather than the resultant state, ἁγιωσύνη, hence,
__1. consecration;
__2. sanctification: so strictly in Rom.6:19,22 (but see Meyer), 1Co.1:30, 1Th.4:3,7, 2Th.2:13, Heb.12:14, 1Pe.1:2. Elsewhere it perhaps (Ellic.; but see Milligan, Th., 48) inclines to the resultant state: 1Th.4:4, 1Ti.2:15 (Cremer, 55, 602).
† (AS)
