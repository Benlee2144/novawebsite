Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1876

ἐπάναγκες

epánankes — G1876

Transliteration: epánankes

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1876

Derivation: neuter of a presumed compound of G1909 (ἐπί) and G318 (ἀνάγκη);

Definition

(adverbially) on necessity, i.e. necessarily

KJV Translation

necessary

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Included with: ἐπᾰνάγκ-ης,
used only in neuter:
__1 ἐπάναγκές [ἐστι] it is compulsory, necessary, with infinitive, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; μηδὲν ἐ. ἔστω let there be no compulsion,[Refs]
__2 as adverb, on compulsion, ἐ. κομῶντες wearing long hair by fixed custom, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐ. λέγειν, ἐντίθεσθαι, [NT+4th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Acts 15:28
