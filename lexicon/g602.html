ἀποκάλυψις

apokálypsis — G602

Transliteration: apokálypsis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G602

Derivation: from G601 (ἀποκαλύπτω);

Definition

disclosure

KJV Translation

appearing, coming, lighten, manifestation, be revealed, revelation

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀπο-κάλυψις [κᾰ], εως, ἡ,
uncovering, of the head, [Refs 1st c.BC+]; disclosing, of hidden springs, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; revelation, especially of divine mysteries, [NT]; of persons, manifestation, [NT]; title of the Apocalypse.

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἀπο-κάλυψις, -εως, ἡ
(ἀποκαλύπτω), [in LXX: 1Ki.20:30 (עֶרְוָה), \2 * ;]
an uncovering, laying bare (Plut.). Metaphorical, a reveal­ing, revelation: a disclosure of divine truth, or a manifestion from God: Luk.2:32 Rom.2:5 8:19 16:25, 1Co.1:7 14:6 14:26, 2Co.12:1 12:7, Gal.1:12 2:2, Eph.1:17 3:3 2Th.1:7 1Pe.1:7 1:13 4:13, Rev.1:1.†
SYN.: ἐπιφάνεια, παρουσία, φανέρωσις (see Tr., Syn., xciv; Lft., Notes, 102, 178; Westc, Eph., 178 f.; M, Th., 145 ff.; Cremer, 343) (AS)
