φαρμακεία

pharmakeía — G5331

Transliteration: pharmakeía

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5331

Derivation: from G5332 (φαρμακεύς);

Definition

medication (\

KJV Translation

sorcery, witchcraft

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

φαρμᾰκ-είᾱ, ἡ,
use of drugs, especially of purgatives, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; αἱ ἄνω φ., i. e. emetics, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of abortifacients, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]: generally, the use of any kind of drugs, potions, or spells, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 poisoning or witchcraft, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; αἱ περὶ τὰς φαρμακείας, ={αἱ φαρμακίδες}, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II metaphorically, remedy, παιδιὰς προσάγειν φαρμακείας χάριν [Refs]
