γνωστός

gnōstós — G1110

Transliteration: gnōstós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1110

Derivation: from G1097 (γινώσκω);

Definition

well-known

KJV Translation

acquaintance, (which may be) known, notable

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

γνωστός, ή, όν,
collateral form of γνωτός (which see), known, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; γνωστόν, τό, common knowledge, τινός [Refs 4th c.AD+] adverb -τῶς clearly, [LXX]
__2 knowable, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; γνωστὰ σαρκός bodily symptoms (of anger), [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__II plural, as substantive, ={γνώριμοι}, notables, [LXX+2nd c.AD+]; acquaintance, friend, [NT]
__III active, knowing, uncertain in [LXX+1st c.AD+]
