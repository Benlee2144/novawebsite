ὑψηλός

hypsēlós — G5308

Transliteration: hypsēlós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5308

Derivation: from G5311 (ὕψος);

Definition

lofty (in place or character)

KJV Translation

high(-er, -ly) (esteemed)

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὑψηλός, ή, όν
(also -ός, όν Demetr.Troezen. 1 Diels): comparative and superlative -ότερος, -ότατος, and irregular -έστατος[Refs 2nd c.AD+]: (ὕψι, ὕψος): —high, lofty, θάλαμος [Refs 8th c.BC+]; of a highland country, χώρη ὀρεινὴ.. καὶ ὑψηλή [Refs 5th c.BC+]; and ὑψηλά alone, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐφ᾽ ὑψηλοῦ εἶναι, καθῆσθαι, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐποικοδομήσαντες ὑψηλότερον [τὸ τεῖχος] [Refs 5th c.BC+]. adverb, -λῶς καθήμενος [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II metaphorically, high, lofty, stately, proud, ὄλβος, ἀρεταί, κλέος, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὑψηλὰ κομπεῖν talk high and boastfully, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 of persons, opposed to δυσδαίμων, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
