τρύπημα

trýpēma — G5169

Transliteration: trýpēma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5169

Derivation: from a derivative of the base of G5168 (τρυμαλιά);

Definition

an aperture, i.e. a needle's eye

KJV Translation

eye

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

τρῡπ-ημα, ατος, τό,
that which is bored, a hole, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; in the ψῆφος, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; τ. νεώς, i. e. one of the holes through which the oars worked, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; in the flute, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; in a gate-fastening, the hole for the βάλανος, Aen. Tact. 18.3; ῥαφίδος (compare τρυμαλιά) [NT+5th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Matthew 19:24
