Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1107

γνωρίζω

gnōrízō — G1107

Transliteration: gnōrízō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1107

Derivation: from a derivative of G1097 (γινώσκω);

Definition

to make known; subjectively, to know

KJV Translation

certify, declare, make known, give to understand, do to wit, wot

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

γνωρ-ίζω,
future Attic dialect-ῐῶ: perfect ἐγνώρικα [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—make known, point out, [NT+4th c.BC+]:—in this sense mostly passive, become known, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 with accusative person, make known, τινά τινι [Refs 1st c.AD+]; commend, τινὰ τῇ βουλῇ ἰσχυρῶς [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__3 certify a person's identity, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II gain knowledge of, become acquainted with, discover, with participle, τοὔργον ὡς οὐ γνωριοῖμί σου τόδε δόλῳ προσέρπον [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; γ. περί τι or περί τινος [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II.2 become acquainted with, τινά [Refs 5th c.BC+] — passive, ἐγνωρισμένοι αὐτῷ being made acquainted with him, [Refs]; πρός τινος [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
