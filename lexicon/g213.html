ἀλαζών

alazṓn — G213

Transliteration: alazṓn

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G213

Derivation: from (vagrancy);

Definition

braggart

KJV Translation

boaster

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀλαζ-ών [ᾰλ], όνος, ὁ, ἡ,
(ἄλη) properly wanderer about country, vagrant, [Refs 7th c.BC+]
__II charlatan, quack, especially of Sophists, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 braggart, boaster, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; title of play by [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II.3 adjective, boastful, pretentious, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: comparative -έστερος[Refs] most shameless, [Refs 5th c.BC+]. adverb superlative -έστατα, δρω-ν[Refs 2nd c.AD+]
