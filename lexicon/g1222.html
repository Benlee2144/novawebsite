δήπου

dḗpou — G1222

Transliteration: dḗpou

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1222

Derivation: from G1211 (δή) and G4225 (πού);

Definition

a particle of asseveration; indeed doubtless

KJV Translation

verily

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

δή-που (WH, δή που),
indef. adv.,
mostly in sense of surely, of course, we know: Heb.2:16 T (WH, δή που).†
(AS)

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

δήπου,
indefinite adverb (better written δή που) perhaps, it may be, ᾧ δή που ἀδελφεὸν ἔκτανε [Refs 8th c.BC+]: in Trag. and Attic dialect usually doubtless, I presume, οὐ δήπου τλητόν [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἴστε γὰρ δή που, μέμνησθε γὰρ δή που, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II as interrogative implying an affirmative answer, τὴν αἰχμάλωτον κάτοισθα δή πο; i.e. I presume you know, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; οὐ δή πο; surely it is not so? implying a negative answer, as [Refs 5th c.BC+]
