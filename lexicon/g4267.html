Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4267

προγινώσκω

proginṓskō — G4267

Transliteration: proginṓskō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4267

Derivation: from G4253 (πρό) and G1097 (γινώσκω);

Definition

to know beforehand, i.e. foresee

KJV Translation

foreknow (ordain), know (before)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

προγιγνώσκω,
Ionic dialect and later προγεωργ-γῑνώσκω: future -γνώσομαι: Epic dialect aorist infinitive προγνώμεναι [Refs]:—know, perceive, learn, or understand beforehand, τι [same place]; τὰ στοιχεῖα [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—in Medicine texts, know before being told, declare unaided, τά τε παρεόντα καὶ τὰ προγεγονότα καὶ τὰ μέλλοντα ἔσεσθαι [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 prognosticate, π. χειμῶνα αἱ μέλιτται [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__3 foreknow, λαόν [NT]
__4 with genitive, π. τῶν θεῶν learn things in advance of.., [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II judge beforehand, ἐς τὸ μέλλον καλὸν π. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; provide, τι [Refs 5th c.BC+] —passive, παρὰ τῷ διαιτητῇ προεγνωσμένος ἀδικεῖν judged beforehand to have done wrong, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
