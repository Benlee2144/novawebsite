Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Derivation: apparently a primary word;

Definition

KJV Translation

honey

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μέλῐ, τό,
genitive ῐτος, etc.; dative μέλι [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—honey, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; said to be made from the palm (φοῖνιξ), [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 in comparisons, of anything sweet, especially of eloquence, μέλιτος γλυκίων ῥέεν αὐδή [Refs 8th c.BC+], of those who refuse to take 'the rough with the smooth', [Refs 7th c.BC+]
__II sweet gum collected from certain trees, manna, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; μ. ἄγριον, μαινόμενον, [Refs 1st c.BC+]. (Cf. Gothic milip, Latin mel.)
