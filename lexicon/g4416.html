πρωτότοκος

prōtótokos — G4416

Transliteration: prōtótokos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4416

Derivation: from G4413 (πρῶτος) and the alternate of G5088 (τίκτω);

Definition

first-born (usually as noun, literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

firstbegotten(-born)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

πρωτοτόκ-ος
(paroxytone), Doric dialect πρᾱτοτόκος, ον, bearing or having borne her first-born, μήτηρ π., of a heifer, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ὗς, ταὧς, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of women, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II proparoxytone πρωτότοκος, ον, passive,first-born, [LXX+NT+4th c.AD+]; π. ἐγὼ ἢ σύ [LXX]
__II.2 metaphorically, π. πάσης κτίσεως [NT]; of Homer, opposed to Nicander, [Refs]
