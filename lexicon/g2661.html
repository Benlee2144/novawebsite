Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2661

καταξιόω

kataxióō — G2661

Transliteration: kataxióō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2661

Derivation: from G2596 (κατά) and G515 (ἀξιόω);

Definition

to deem entirely deserving

KJV Translation

(ac-)count worthy

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

καταξι-όω,
deem worthy, with accusative et infinitive, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; hold in honour, [Refs 2nd c.BC+] with genitive _of things_, deem worthy of a thing, [Refs 1st c.BC+]:—middle, οὔτε νιν.. Δίκη προσεῖδε καὶ κατηξιώσατο did not regard and hold in high esteem, [Refs 4th c.BC+] —passive, to be held worthy, [Refs 2nd c.BC+].
__II command, bid, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σύ τοι κατηξίωσας thou didst decree it so, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III deign, vouchsafe, with infinitive, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__IV in bad sense, τῶν ἐν μέρους εἴδει πεφυκότων μηδενὶ καταξιώσωμεν let us not degrade it by likening it to.., [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__V in argument, claim, maintain, [Refs 1st c.BC+] —middle, with infinitive, [Refs]
