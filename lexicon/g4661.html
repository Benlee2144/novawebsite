Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4661

σκῦλον

skŷlon — G4661

Transliteration: skŷlon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4661

Derivation: neuter from G4660 (σκύλλω);

Definition

something stripped (as a hide), i.e. booty

KJV Translation

spoil

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σκῦλον, τό,
mostly in plural σκῦλα, arms stripped off a slain enemy, spoils, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σκῦλα γράφειν to write one's name on arms gained as spoils, which were then dedicated to a deity, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: less frequently in singular, booty, spoil, prey, σκῦλον οἰωνοῖσιν [Refs 5th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Luke 11:22
