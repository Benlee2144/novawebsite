ἄπειμι

ápeimi — G548

Transliteration: ápeimi

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G548

Derivation: from G575 (ἀπό) and G1510 (εἰμί);

Definition

to be away

KJV Translation

be absent

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἄπειμι,
(εἰμί
__A sum), imperfect ἀπῆν (later ἀπήμην[Refs 3rd c.AD+], 2nd pers.singular ἀπῆσθα[Refs 5th c.BC+]; Epic dialect ἀπέην[Refs 8th c.BC+]: future ἀπέσομαι[Refs 5th c.BC+]; Epic dialect ἀπέσσομαι[Refs 8th c.BC+]:—to be away or far from, with genitive, ὁππότε πάτρης ἧς ἀπέῃσιν ἀνήρ [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__A.2 absolutely, to be away or absent, and of things, to be wanting, οἵ τ᾽ ὄντες οἵ τ᾽ ἀπόντες, i.e. all that are, every one, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of the dead, variant in [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τοῦ θεοῦ ἀπεόντος the god not being counted, [Refs 5th c.BC+]imperfect, ἀπῆσαν and ἀπῇσαν are frequently confused in codices, as in [Refs 5th c.BC+]
