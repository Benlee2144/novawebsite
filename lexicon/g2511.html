Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2511

καθαρίζω

katharízō — G2511

Transliteration: katharízō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2511

Derivation: from G2513 (καθαρός);

Definition

to cleanse (literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

(make) clean(-se), purge, purify

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

κᾰθαρ-ίζω,
future -ιῶ[NT]:—cleanse, θυσιαστήριον [LXX+NT]; sift grain, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]; prune away, περισσὰ βλαστήματα [Refs 1st c.AD+]; clear ground of weeds, etc., [Refs 4th c.AD+]; keep a precinct clear, ἀπό τινων [Refs 1st c.BC+] —in middle, future -ιοῦμαι, of the menses, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II of persons, purify, ἀπὸ ἁμαρτίας [LXX+NT]; cleanse from leprosy, [NT]passive, -ιζέστω ἀπὸ γυναικός κτλ. [Refs]

καθαρίζω

__1. In physical sense: with accusative of thing(s), figuratively, Mat.23:25-26, Luk.11:39; of disease (leprosy), with accusative of person(s), Mat.8:2-3 10:8 11:5, Mrk.1:40-42 Luk.4:27 5:12-13 7:22 17:14, 17; ἡ λέπρα ἐκαθερίσθη (on the spelling see supr.), Mat.8:3.
