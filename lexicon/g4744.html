Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4744

στίλβω

stílbō — G4744

Transliteration: stílbō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4744

Derivation: apparently a primary verb;

Definition

to gleam, i.e. flash intensely

KJV Translation

shining

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

στίλβ-ω,
chiefly present and imperfect: aorist ἔστιλψα [Refs 2nd c.AD+]:—glitter, gleam, of polished or bright surfaces, χιτῶνας.. ἦκα στίλβοντας ἐλαίῳ [Refs 8th c.BC+]; λαμπραὶ δ᾽ ἀκτῖνες ἀπ᾽ αὐτοῦ αἰγλῆεν στίλβουσι beam from him, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σ. ὅπλοις [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἰδὼν στίλβοντα τὰ λάβδα, i.e. the λ upon the Spartan shields, [NT+5th c.BC+]; of sleek horses, σ. ὥστε κύκνου πτερόν [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of brilliant complexion, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; of water in motion, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of the white gleam on the eye, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of fixed stars, opposed to planets (exc. Mercury, see at {στίλβων}), twinkle, [Refs 4th c.BC+] flash lightning, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 metaphorically, shine, be bright, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II transitive,= στιλπνόω, στίλβει πρόσωπον [Refs 1st c.AD+]
