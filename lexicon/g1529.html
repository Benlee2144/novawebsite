εἴσοδος

eísodos — G1529

Transliteration: eísodos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1529

Derivation: from G1519 (εἰς) and G3598 (ὁδός);

Definition

an entrance (literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

coming, enter(-ing) in (to)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

εἴσοδος
or ἔσοδος, ἡ, entrance:
__I place of entrance, entry, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἐσόδους Φοίβου the entrance to his temple, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of a mountain-pass, ἡ διὰ Τρηχῖνος ἔ. ἐς τὴν Ἑλλάδα [Refs 5th c.BC+]; in a theatre, entrance for the Chorus, [Refs 5th c.BC+], see Scholia; entrance-door of a court of justice, [Refs 4th c.BC+] paths to glory, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II entering, entrance, εἴ. παρασχεῖν [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 entrance into the lists to contend in the games, ἱππείαν ἔ.(compare εἰσέρχομαι II) [Refs 5th c.BC+]; also ἡ εἴ. τῆς δίκης εἰς τὸ δικαστήριον the introduction of it, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.3 right or privilege of entrance, ἔσοδον εἶναι παρὰ βασιλέα ἄνευ ἀγγέλου [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.4 visit, κακῶν γυναικῶν εἴσοδοι [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of a doctor, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II.5 study, investigation, Vett. Val.259.7; ἀκροθιγεῖς τὰς εἰσόδους ποιήσασθαι [Refs]; also, method, [Refs]
__III that which comes in, revenue, opposed to ἔξοδος, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
