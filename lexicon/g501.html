ἀντλέω

antléō — G501

Transliteration: antléō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G501

Derivation: from (the hold of a ship);

Definition

to bale up (properly, bilge water), i.e. dip water (with a bucket, pitcher, etc.)

KJV Translation

draw (out)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀντλ-έω,
(ἄντλος) bale out bilge-water, bale the ship, [Refs 7th c.BC+]
__2 generally, draw water, ἀντλέει καὶ ἐγχέει[NT+5th c.BC+]; οἷον ἐκ κρήνης ἐπ᾽ ὀχετοὺς ἀ. draw as from a well, and pour into.., [Refs 5th c.BC+] draw water in a sieve, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; but ἐκ πίθω ἀ. of one who has abundance in store, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__II metaphorically, drain dry, i.e.,
__II.1 use the utmost, make the most of, τὰν ἔμπρακτον ἄντλει μαχανάν[Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 of toil, suffering, etc., drain to the dregs, τὴν παροῦσαν ἀντλήσω τύχην[Refs 5th c.BC+]; δέκα ἀντλήσας ἔτη variant [Refs]
__II.3 squander, πατρῴαν κτῆσιν ἀντλεῖν[Refs 5th c.BC+]
