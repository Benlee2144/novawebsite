Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1114

γόης

góēs — G1114

Transliteration: góēs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1114

Derivation: from (to wail);

Definition

properly, a wizard (as muttering spells), i.e. (by implication) an imposter

KJV Translation

seducer

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

γόης, ητος, ὁ,
sorcerer, wizard, Phoronis 2, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; probably falsa lectio for{βοῇσι} [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 juggler, cheat, δεινὸς γ. καὶ φαρμακεὺς καὶ σοφιστής [Refs 5th c.BC+]: comparative γοητότερος [Refs 3rd c.AD+]. (Cf. Lithuanian žavēti 'incantare'.)

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Timothy 3:13

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

γόης, -ητος, ὁ (γοάω, to wail),
__1. a wailer.
__2. a wizard.
__3. an impostor (cf. γοητεία, trickery, \2): 2Ti.3:13.
