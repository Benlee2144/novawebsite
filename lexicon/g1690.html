ἐμβριμάομαι

embrimáomai — G1690

Transliteration: embrimáomai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1690

Derivation: from G1722 (ἐν) and (to snort with anger);

Definition

to have indignation on, i.e. (transitively) to blame, (intransitively) to sigh with chagrin, (specially) to sternly enjoin

KJV Translation

straitly charge, groan, murmur against

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐμβρῑμ-άομαι
(active only in [Refs 5th c.AD+], with aorist middle et passive, snort in, ἵππους ἐν ἀμπυκτῆρσιν ἐμβριμωμένας, of horses, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 of persons, to be deeply moved, τῷ πνεύματι, ἐν ἑαυτῷ, [NT]
__II admonish urgently, rebuke, [NT+5th c.BC+]
