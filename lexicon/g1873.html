Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1873

ἐπακούω

epakoúō — G1873

Transliteration: epakoúō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1873

Derivation: from G1909 (ἐπί) and G191 (ἀκούω);

Definition

to hearken (favorably) to

KJV Translation

hear

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐπᾰκού-ω,
future ἐπακούσομαι[LXX], later ἐπακούσω [Refs]:—hear, with accusative of things, ὂς πάντ᾽ ἐφορᾷς καὶ πάντ᾽ ἐπακούεις, of the Sun, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; proverbial, ὁπποῖόν κ᾽ εἴπῃσθα ἔπος, τοῖόν κ᾽ ἐπακούσαις as thou speakest, so wilt thou be answered, [Refs 8th c.BC+]: with accusative of things et genitive person, ἔπος ἐμέθεν [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__2 overhear, μή τις τῶν ἀμυήτων ἐπακούσῃ [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 hear about, hear tell of, μόχθων [Refs 5th c.BC+]; with participle, οἷον γᾶς Ἀσίας οὐκ ἐπακούω.. βλαστὸν φύτευμα [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__4 give ear, listen, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐ. μοι 'pray attend', [Refs]: especially of giving ear to one who prays, of God, [LXX+2nd c.BC+]; or to advice, commands, etc., i.e. obey, βουλῆς [LXX+8th c.BC+

Appears In (1 verse)

2 Corinthians 6:2
