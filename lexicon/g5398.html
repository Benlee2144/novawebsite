φοβερός

phoberós — G5398

Transliteration: phoberós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5398

Derivation: from G5401 (φόβος);

Definition

frightful, i.e. (objectively) formidable

KJV Translation

fearful, terrible

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

φοβερός, ά, όν,
(φόβος) fearful, whether active or passive:
__I active, causing fear, terrible, χρηστήρια φ. [Refs 5th c.BC+], etc.; ὅμιλος πλήθει -ώτατος formidable only from numbers, [Refs 5th c.BC+] things which are fearful to the multitude, [Refs 5th c.BC+]infinitive, φ. ἰδεῖν, φ. προσιδέσθαι, fearful to behold, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__I.2 regarded with fear, especially with respect to consequences, οὔτε ὅρκος φ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἵππος φ. μὴ ἀνήκεστόν τι ποιήσῃ a horse that makes one fear he will do some mischief, [Refs 5th c.BC+]terror, danger, [Refs]; τῶν φοβερῶν ὄντων τῇ πόλει γενέσθαι the things which were dreaded as like ly to happen.., [Refs]; φοβερόν [ἐστι] μὴ.. there is reason to dread that.., [Refs]; ἀγγέλλεσθαι ἐπὶ τὸ φοβερώτατον to be fearfully exaggerated, [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__I.3 Rhet., of style, impressive, awe-inspiring, τὸ κάλλος τὸ Θουκυδίδου φ. [Refs 4th c.BC+
