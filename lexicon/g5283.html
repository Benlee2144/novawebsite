ὑπόνοια

hypónoia — G5283

Transliteration: hypónoia

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5283

Derivation: from G5282 (ὑπονοέω);

Definition

suspicion

KJV Translation

surmising

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὑπόνοια, ἡ,
(ὑπονοέω) suspicion, conjecture, guess, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὑπόνοιαι τῶν μελλόντων notions formed of future events, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; in bad sense, ὑπόνοιαι πλασταί [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 suggestion, [Refs 1st c.BC+]; imputation, [Refs]
__II the real meaning which lies at the bottom of a thing, deeper sense, τὰς ὑ. οὐκ ἐπίστανται [Refs 5th c.BC+]; especially covert meaning (such as is conveyed by myths and allegories), ὁ.. νέος οὐχ οἷός τε κρίνειν ὅτι τε ὑ. καὶ ὃ μή [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to αἰσχρολογία, [Refs 4th c.BC+]by insinuation, covertly, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
