θεῖον

theîon — G2303

Transliteration: theîon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2303

Derivation: probably neuter of G2304 (θεῖος) (in its original sense of flashing);

Definition

sulphur

KJV Translation

brimstone

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

θεῖον,

__AEpic dialect θέειον (in [Refs 8th c.BC+] θήϊον), τό, brimstone, used to fumigate and purify, δέπας.. ἐκάθηρε θεείῳ [Refs 8th c.BC+]; δεινὴ δὲ θεείου γίγνεται ὀδμή, from a thunderbolt, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἐν δὲ θεείου πλῆτο, of a ship struck by lightning, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἐμβαλόντες πῦρ ξὺν θ. [LXX+5th c.BC+]; as a natural product, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to πεπυρωμένον, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; compare θεάφιον, θέαφος. (Perh. cognate with θύω, θυμιάω, Latin suffire.)
