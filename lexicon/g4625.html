Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4625

σκάνδαλον

skándalon — G4625

Transliteration: skándalon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4625

Derivation: (\

Definition

a trap-stick (bent sapling), i.e. snare (figuratively, cause of displeasure or sin)

KJV Translation

occasion to fall (of stumbling), offence, thing that offends, stumblingblock

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Included with: σκανδαλ-ιστής, οῦ, ὁ,
probably acrobat who performed on a trapeze (σκάνδαλον), [Refs 2nd c.AD+], trap or snare laid for an enemy, [LXX+NT]; probably laid for animals, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]: metaphorically, stumbling-block, offence, scandal, [NT+6th c.AD+]
__II see at {σκανδαλιστής}.
