αὐθάδης

authádēs — G829

Transliteration: authádēs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G829

Derivation: from G846 (αὐτός) and the base of G2237 (ἡδονή);

Definition

self-pleasing, i.e. arrogant

KJV Translation

self-willed

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

αὐθάδ-ης, ες,
self-willed, stubborn, ἦσάν τε αὐθαδέστεροι[Refs 5th c.BC+]; surly, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of a dog, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 metaphorically of things, remorseless, σφηνὸς γνάθος αὐ. [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__3 adverb -δως[Refs 5th c.BC+]: comparative -έστερον[Refs 5th c.BC+]; compare αὐτώδης. (From αὐτο-ἁδης,[Refs 4th c.BC+]
