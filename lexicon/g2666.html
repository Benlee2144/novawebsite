καταπίνω

katapínō — G2666

Transliteration: katapínō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2666

Derivation: from G2596 (κατά) and G4095 (πίνω);

Definition

to drink down, i.e. gulp entire (literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

devour, drown, swallow (up)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

καταπίνω [ῑ],
future -πίομαι [Refs 6th c.BC+], later -πιοῖμαι [Refs 1st c.AD+]: aorist [Refs 8th c.BC+] R.: perfect -πέπωκα [Refs 6th c.BC+]:—gulp, swallow down, both of liquids and solids [Refs 8th c.BC+]; λίθους [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of the sea, [LXX+6th c.BC+]:—passive, τὸ -ποθὲν ὕδωρ (i.e. by the earth) [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of rivers that disappear underground, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of cities swallowed by an earthquake, [Refs 2nd c.BC+].
__2 absolutely, swallow, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II metaphorically, τὸν ἡμίοπον ὁ μέγας [αὐλὸς] κ. [Refs 4th c.BC+]:—passive, to be absorbed, of knots in wood, [Refs 4th c.BC+].
__II.b κ. Εὐριπίδην
