ἀποστάσιον

apostásion — G647

Transliteration: apostásion

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G647

Derivation: neuter of a (presumed) adjective from a derivative of G868 (ἀφίστημι);

Definition

properly, something separative, i.e. (specially) divorce

KJV Translation

(writing of) divorcement

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Included with: ἀπο-στᾰσίου δίκη
action against a freedman for having forsaken his προστάτης and chosen another, ἀ. ὀφλεῖν[Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II ἀποστασίου βιβλίον writing or bill of divorce, [LXX+NT]
__III ἀποστασίου συγγραφή deed of cession, conveyance, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; ἀντίγραφονίου[Refs 2nd c.AD+]; with συγγραφή omitted, especially in phrase πρᾶσις καὶ ἀποστασίου[Refs 1st c.AD+]
