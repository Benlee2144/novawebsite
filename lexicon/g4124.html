πλεονεξία

pleonexía — G4124

Transliteration: pleonexía

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4124

Derivation: from G4123 (πλεονέκτης);

Definition

avarice, i.e. (by implication) fraudulency, extortion

KJV Translation

covetous(-ness) practices, greediness

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

πλεονεξία,
Ionic dialect -ιη, ἡ, greediness, assumption, arrogance, τῶν Σπαρτιητέων ἡ π. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; π. συγγενική wrong done to one's kin, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
__2 assumption, αἱ ἐν τῷ πυνθάνεσθαι π. [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II advantage, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; αἱ π. αἱ ἴδιαι, αἱ δημόσιαι, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐπὶ πλεονεξίᾳ with a view to one's own advantage, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; μετὰ πλεονεξίας τινὸς ἀγωνίζεσθαι πρὸς [τὰ θηρία] [Refs]excellences, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
__II.2 a larger share of a thing, τῶν πολιτικῶν δικαίων [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II.3 gain derived from a thing, τὴν ἐπὶ τῶν ἰδίων δικῶν πλεονεξίαν [Refs 4th c.BC+]undue gains, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III excess, opposed to ἔνδεια, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
