Πύθων

Pýthōn — G4436

Transliteration: Pýthōn

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4436

Derivation: from (the name of the region where Delphi, the seat of the famous oracle, was located);

Definition

a Python, i.e. (by analogy, with the supposed diviner there) inspiration (soothsaying)

KJV Translation

divination

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

Πύθων [ῡ], ωνος, ὁ,
(compare Πυθώ) the serpent “Python”, slain by Apollo, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II παιδίσκη ἔχουσα πνεῦμα Πύθωνα a spirit of divination, [NT]
__II.2 plural Πύθωνες, ventriloquists, [Refs 1st c.AD+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Acts 16:16

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

πύθων, -ωνος, ὁ
__1. in cl., Python, a serpent slain by Apollo, who is hence surnamed the Pythian.
__2. In Plut. (ii, 414 E), a name given to ventriloquist soothsayers (ἐγγαστρίμυθοι; cf. Lev.19:31 20:6, 27, \2), and perhaps in this sense πνεῦμα πύθωνα, a python-spirit: Act.16:16.†
