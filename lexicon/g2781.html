κηρίον

kēríon — G2781

Transliteration: kēríon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2781

Derivation: diminutive from (wax);

Definition

a cell for honey, i.e. (collectively) the comb

KJV Translation

(honey-)comb

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

κηρίον, τό, κηρός
) honeycomb, mostly in plural, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; κ. καὶ λίβανον, as offerings, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τὸ κ. τοῦ μέλιτος [LXX]; used in Medicine, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; παιδίον κηρίῳ βεβυσμένον having its mouth stopped with a piece of honeycomb, [Refs 5th c.BC+], honey, [Refs 6th c.BC+]
