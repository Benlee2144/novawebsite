παρεμβολή

parembolḗ — G3925

Transliteration: parembolḗ

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3925

Derivation: from a compound of G3844 (παρά) and G1685 (ἐμβάλλω);

Definition

a throwing in beside (juxtaposition), i.e. (specially), battle-array, encampment or barracks (tower Antonia)

KJV Translation

army, camp, castle

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

παρεμ-βολή, ἡ,
insertion, interpolation, ἑτέρων πραγμάτων [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 Grammars, parenthesis, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II drawing up in battle-order, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; in Tactics, insertion of men in the ranks (distinct from παρένταξις and παρεμπλοκή), [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__II.b company of soldiers, etc., [LXX]; host, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
__II.2 encampment, [LXX+4th c.BC+]: generally, soldiers' quarters, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; barracks, [NT+2nd c.AD+]; name of an ἄμφοδον, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
__III ={παρεξειρεσία} (which see), [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
__IV in boxing and wrestling, π. βάλλειν trip an adversary by a twist of the leg, [Refs 1st c.AD+]

παρ-εμ-βολή, -ῆς, ἡ
(παρεμβάλλω), [in LXX chiefly for מַחֲנֶה ;]
__1. an insertion, interpolation (Æschin.)
__2. In the Macedonian dialect (Rutherford, NPhr., 473), as a military term;
__(a) an army in battle array: Heb.11:34 (cf. Exo.14:19-20, Jdg.4:16, al.; frequently in Polyb.);
__(b) a camp (Exo.29:14, al.) : Heb.13:11, 13 Rev.20:9;
__(with) barracks, soldiers' quarters: Act.21:34, 37 22:24 23:10, 16 23:32 28:16 (WH, txt., R, om.).†
παρ-εμ-βάλλω,
[in LXX frequently and chiefly for מַחֲנֶה ;]
__1. to put in beside or between, interpose.
__2. In late writers (Polyb., al., LXX) as technical military term;
__(a) of soldiers, to draw up in line (frequently in \2, al.);
__(b) of siege works, to cast up: with accusative and dative, χάρακά σοι, Luk.19:43.†
