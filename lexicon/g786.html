ἄσπονδος

áspondos — G786

Transliteration: áspondos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G786

Derivation: from G1 (Α) (as a negative particle) and a derivative of G4689 (σπένδω);

Definition

literally, without libation (which usually accompanied a treaty), i.e. (by implication) truceless

KJV Translation

implacable, truce-breaker

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἄσπονδ-ος, ον,
without σπονδή or drink-offering: hence,
__I of a god, to whom no drink-offering is poured, ἄ. θεός, i.e. death, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II without a regular truce (ratified by σπονδαί), ἀνοκωχή[Refs 5th c.BC+]; of persons, without making a truce, ἄ. ἀπιέναι[Refs]; ἀσπόνδους τοὺς νεκροὺς ἀνελέσθαι take up their dead without leave asked, [Refs]; τὸ εὐπρεπὲς ἄσπονδον the specious plea of neutrality, [Refs]
__II.2 admitting of no truce, implacable, ἄσπονδόν τ᾽ Ἄρη (ἀράν codices) [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of persons, implacable, [NT]. adverb -δως, ἔχειν[Refs 1st c.AD+]
