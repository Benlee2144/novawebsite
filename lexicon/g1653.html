ἐλεέω

eleéō — G1653

Transliteration: eleéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1653

Derivation: from G1656 (ἔλεος);

Definition

to compassionate (by word or deed, specially, by divine grace)

KJV Translation

have compassion (pity on), have (obtain, receive, shew) mercy (on)

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἐλεέω
(in Rom.9:16, Jude 22, -άω, which see), -ῶ (ἔλεος), [in LXX (Hex, Pss, Pr) chiefly for חָנַן, also frequently in Proph. for רָחַם, etc. ;]
to have pity or mercy on, to show mercy: absol., Rom.9:16 12:8; with accusative, Mat.9:27 15:22 17:15 18:33 20:30-31, Mrk.5:19 10:47-48, Luk.16:24 17:13 18:38-39 Rom.9:15, 18 11:32, Php.2:27, Jude 22. Pass., to have pity or mercy shown one (EV, obtain mercy): Mat.5:7, Rom.11:30-31, 1Co.7:25, 2Co.4:1, 1Ti.1:13 1:16, 1Pe.2:10.†
