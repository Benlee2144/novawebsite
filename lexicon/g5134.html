τραῦμα

traûma — G5134

Transliteration: traûma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5134

Derivation: from the base of (to wound;

Definition

akin to the base of G2352 (θραύω), G5147 (τρίβος), G5149 (τρίζω), etc.); a wound

KJV Translation

wound

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

τραῦμα, ατος, τό,
Ionic dialect τρῶμα [Refs 5th c.BC+]; Doric dialect also τρῶμα, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]:— wound, hurt, ἀποθνῄσκειν ἐκ τῶν τ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; φέρειν, ποιεῖν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II of things, hurt, damage, as of ships, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III in war, heavy blow, defeat, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__IV ἡ τοῦ τ. γραφή an indictment for wounding (with intent to murder), [Refs 5th c.BC+]
