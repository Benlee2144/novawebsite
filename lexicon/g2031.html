🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἔπος,
older ϝέπος [Refs], etc., εος, τό (Sanskrit vácas 'word', 'hymn', compare εἶπον):
__I word, παύρῳ ἔπει in short utterance, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: generally, that which is uttered in words, speech, tale, ἔπος ἐρέειν [Refs 8th c.BC+]; joined with μῦθος, [Refs 8th c.BC+] —Special uses,
__I.1 song or lay accompanied by music, [Refs]
__I.2 pledged word, promise, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τελέσαι ἔπος fulfil, keep one's word,[Refs 4th c.BC+]
__I.3 word in season, counsel, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__I.4 word of a deity, oracle, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__I.5 saying, proverb, τὸ παλαιὸν ἔπος [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__I.6 word, opposed to deed, ἔπε᾽ ἀκράαντα words of none effect, opposed to ἔτυμα, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; opposed to ἔργον, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; opposed to βίη, [Refs 8th c.BC+
