βαπτίζω

baptízō — G907

Transliteration: baptízō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G907

Derivation: from a derivative of G911 (βάπτω);

Definition

to immerse, submerge; to make whelmed (i.e. fully wet); used only (in the New Testament) of ceremonial ablution, especially (technically) of the ordinance of Christian baptism

KJV Translation

Baptist, baptize, wash

⚠ Our Analysis Differs

Βαπτίζω means “to immerse, to dip, to submerge.” It was never translated — it was transliterated. The KJV translators left it as “baptize” instead of translating it as “immerse” to avoid contradicting the Church of England’s practice of sprinkling. See our study.
