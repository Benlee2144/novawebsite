Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1092

γεωργός

geōrgós — G1092

Transliteration: geōrgós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1092

Derivation: from G1093 (γῆ) and the base of G2041 (ἔργον);

Definition

a land-worker, i.e. farmer

KJV Translation

husbandman

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

γεωργ-ός, όν,
(γῆ, ἔργον) tilling the ground, βοίδιον [Refs 5th c.BC+]; fertilizing, Νεῖλος [Refs 4th c.AD+] —as substantive, γεωργός, ὁ, husbandman, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; οἱ γ., opposed to οἱ μισθαρνοῦντες, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; but γ., opposed to ὁ δεσπότης τοῦ χωρίου, [Refs]; so of vine-dressers, gardeners, etc., [Refs 5th c.BC+]; γ. ὄχλος the peasantry, [Refs 1st c.BC+]; γ. βίος probably in [Refs 5th c.BC+]
