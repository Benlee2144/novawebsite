ἐπιθυμέω

epithyméō — G1937

Transliteration: epithyméō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1937

Derivation: from G1909 (ἐπί) and G2372 (θυμός);

Definition

to set the heart upon, i.e. long for (rightfully or otherwise)

KJV Translation

covet, desire, would fain, lust (after)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐπιθῡμ-έω,
set one's heart upon a thing, long for, covet, desire, with genitive of things, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of political attachments, τῶν ἡμετέρων πολεμίων 5th-6th c.BC: Andocides Orator 4.28; ὀλιγαρχίας 5th c.BC: Lysias Orator 20.3: with infinitive, desire to do, πλῶσαι 5th c.BC: Herodotus Historicus 1.24; ἀπικνέεσθαι[Refs 5th c.BC+]: absolutely, desire, covet, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τὸ ἐπιθυμοῦν τοῦ πλοῦ, ={ἐπιθυμία}, eagerness for it, [Refs]:—passive, to be desired, τὰ ἐπιθυμούμενα 5th-6th c.BC: Plato Philosophus “Philebus” 35d.
