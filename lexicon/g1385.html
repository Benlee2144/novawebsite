δοκός

dokós — G1385

Transliteration: dokós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1385

Derivation: from G1209 (δέχομαι) (through the idea of holding up);

Definition

a stick of timber

KJV Translation

beam

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

δοκός, ἡ,
later also ὁ, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]: (δέχομαι):— bearing-beam, main beam, especially in the roof or floor of a house, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; any balk or beam, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; bar of a gate or door, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: also, in plural, firewood, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]one who has 'swallowed a poker', [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ἐν δοκοῖσι is probably falsa lectio for{ἐνδόκοισι} in [Refs 7th c.BC+]
__II a kind of meteor, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
