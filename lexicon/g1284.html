Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1284

διαῤῥήσσω

diarrhḗssō — G1284

Transliteration: diarrhḗssō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1284

Derivation: from G1223 (διά) and G4486 (ῥήγνυμι);

Definition

to tear asunder

KJV Translation

break, rend

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

διαρρήγνῡμι,
break through, [Refs 8th c.BC+] only in middle, διά τε ῥήξασθαι ἐπάλξεις [Refs 8th c.BC+]; διαρρήξασα χαλινόν having broken the bridle asunder, [Refs 6th c.BC+]; μόγις ἂν.. διαρρήξειας [τὴν κεφαλήν] [Refs 5th c.BC+]; πλευρὰν διαρρήξαντα.. φασγάνῳ having cloven it, [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, burst, as with eating, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; διαρραγείης, as a curse, 'split you!' [Refs 5th c.BC+]perfect διέρρωγα to be broken or torn, διερρωγυιῶν τῶν χορδῶν [Refs 5th c.BC+]: later perfect participle passive διερρηγμένος [Refs 4th c.AD+]
