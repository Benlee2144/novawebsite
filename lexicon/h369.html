אַיִן

ʼayin — H369

Transliteration: ʼayin

Pronunciation:

Language: Hebrew

Strong’s: H369

Derivation: as if from a primitive root meaning to be nothing or not exist;

Definition

a non-entity; generally used as a negative particle

KJV Translation

else, except, fail, (father-) less, be gone, in(-curable), neither, never, no (where), none, nor, (any, thing), not, nothing, to nought, past, un(-searchable), well-nigh, without. Compare H370 (אַיִן).

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

1) nothing, not, nought n
1a) nothing, nought neg
1b) not
1c) to have not (of possession) adv
1d) without w/prep
1e) for lack of
