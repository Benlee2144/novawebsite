πτόησις

Transliteration: ptóēsis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4423

Derivation: from G4422 (πτοέω);

Definition

alarm

KJV Translation

amazement

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

πτό-ησις,
or πτοίησις (so in [Refs 5th c.BC+], εως, ἡ, vehement emotion or excitement, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἡ τοῦ σώματος π. [NT+4th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

1 Peter 3:6

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

πτόησις, -εως, ἡ
(πτοέω) [in LXX: Pro.3:25 (פַּחַד), \2 א1, \2 R * ;]
a fluttering, excitement, caused by any emotion, but esp. by fear, hence, terror: φοβεῖσθαι πτόησιν, to be afraid with (cogn. accusative) or of any terror (see ICC, in l): 1Pe.3:6.†
