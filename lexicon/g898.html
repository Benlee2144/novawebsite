Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G898

βαθμός

bathmós — G898

Transliteration: bathmós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G898

Derivation: from the same as G899 (βάθος);

Definition

a step, i.e. (figuratively) grade (of dignity)

KJV Translation

degree

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

βαθμ-ός
or βασμός, ὁ, (βαίνω) step, threshold, [LXX+5th c.BC+]; degree on the dial, [LXX]; fifteen degrees of the zodiac, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; interval in a musical scale, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
__2 rung of a ladder, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__3 base or plinth of a tower, [Refs]
__II metaphorically, step, degree in rank (οἱ β. κλίμακος προκοπὴν σημαίνουσι [NT+2nd c.AD+]; simply, degree, τολμημάτων βαθμοί [Refs 1st c.AD+]; step in an argument, [Refs 6th c.AD+]; of a genealogy, ἀπωτέρω δυοῖν β. two steps farther back, i.e. farther back than one's grandfather, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__III tax paid on stairs, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]

1 Timothy 3:13
