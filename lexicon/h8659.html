תַּרְשִׁישׁ

Tarshîysh — H8659

Transliteration: Tarshîysh

Pronunciation:

Language: Hebrew

Strong’s: H8659

Derivation: probably the same as H8658 (תַּרְשִׁישׁ) (as the region of the stone, or the reverse);

Definition

Tarshish, a place on the Mediterranean, hence, the ephithet of a merchant vessel (as if for or from that port); also the name of a Persian and of an Israelite

KJV Translation

Tarshish, Tharshish.

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

A man of the tribe of Benjamin living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at 1Ch.7.10;
son of: Bilhan (H1092H);
brother of: Jeush (H3266H), Benjamin (H1144H), Ehud (H0164H), Chenaanah (H3668H), Zethan (H2133) and Ahishahar (H0300)
§ Tarshish or Tharshish = "yellow jasper"
1) son of Javan
2) a Benjamite, son of Bilhan
3) one of the wise men close to king Ahasuerus of Persia
4) a city of the Phoenicians in a distant part of the Mediterranean Sea to which the prophet Jonah was trying to flee
1a) perhaps in Cyprus or Spain
5) a city somewhere near and accessible to the Red Sea to which ships constructed at Ezion-geber on the Elanitic Gulf on the Red Sea were to sail
