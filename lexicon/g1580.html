Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1580

ἐκκομίζω

ekkomízō — G1580

Transliteration: ekkomízō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1580

Derivation: from G1537 (ἐκ) and G2865 (κομίζω);

Definition

to bear forth (to burial)

KJV Translation

carry out

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐκκομ-ίζω,
carry or bring out, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; especially to a place of safety, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐκκομίζειν τινὰ ἐκ τοῦ μέλλοντος γίνεσθαι πρήγματος to keep him out of trouble, [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—middle, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐσεκομίσαντο καὶ ἐξεκομίσαντο ἃ ἐβούλοντο, of those relieved from a state of siege, [Refs]: absolutely, remove, ἐς τοὺς Λοκρούς [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 especially carry out a corpse, bury, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
__3 ἐ. σῖτον, of a horse, throw the provender out of the manger, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__4 carry home, ἄνδρας [Refs]
__II endure to the end, τὸ πεπρωμένον [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III middle, receive what is due, λόγους, ὀψώνια, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
