Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2044

ἐρεύγομαι

ereúgomai — G2044

Transliteration: ereúgomai

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2044

Derivation: of uncertain affinity;

Definition

to belch, i.e. (figuratively) to speak out

KJV Translation

utter

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐρεύγομαι,

__B aorist 2 active ἤρῠγον, bellow, roar, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τόν γ᾽ ἐρυγόντα λίπε..θυμός [Refs] roared to the full depth of his throat or voice, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; of the sea, ἀμφὶ δέ τ᾽ ἄκραι ἠϊόνες βοόωσιν ἐρευγομένης ἁλὸς ἔξω the headlands echo to the roar of the sea, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἐρεύγεται ἤπειρόνδε [LXX]

Appears In (1 verse)

Matthew 13:35

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἐρεύγομαι
[in LXX chiefly for שָׁאַג, Hos.11:10, Amo.3:4, 8 al.; also for נָבַע, Psa.19:2, etc. ;]
__1. to spit or spue out.
__2. Prop., of oxen (Hom.), to bellow, roar; whence, as in LXX, to speak aloud, utter: Mat.13:35 (LXX). (For other examples of softened force of words in late Gk., cf. σκύλλω, τρώγω, χορτάζω.) †
