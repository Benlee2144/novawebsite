ἀναίρεσις

anaíresis — G336

Transliteration: anaíresis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G336

Derivation: from G337 (ἀναιρέω);

Definition

(the act of) killing

KJV Translation

death

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀναίρ-εσις, εως, ἡ,
taking up or away, especially of dead bodies for burial, ὀστέων[Refs 5th c.BC+]; in a sea-fight, νεκρῶν ἢ ναυαγίων ἀ.[Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 taking up, ἀ. καὶ θέσις ὅπλων[Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 undertaking, ἔργων[Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II destruction, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 slaying, putting to death, [LXX+1st c.AD+]; banishment, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.3 repeal, δογμάτων[Refs 1st c.AD+]; quashing of indictment, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II.4 direct confutation of arguments, opposed to διαίρεσις (confutation by drawing a distinction), [Refs 4th c.BC+]; destruction (by argument), τινός[Refs 1st c.BC+]
__II.5 Astrology texts, ={ἀκτινοβολία}, Thrasyll. cited in [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
