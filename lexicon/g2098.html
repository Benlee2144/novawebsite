Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2098

εὐαγγέλιον

euangélion — G2098

Transliteration: euangélion

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2098

Derivation: from the same as G2097 (εὐαγγελίζω);

Definition

a good message, i.e. the gospel

KJV Translation

gospel

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

εὐαγγέλ-ιον, τό,
reward of good tidings, given to the messenger, εὐαγγέλιον δέ μοι ἔστω [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἀπολήψῃ τὸ εὐ. [Refs 1st c.AD+] to make a thank-offering for good-tidings, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐβουθύτει ὡς εὐ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; εὐ. στεφανοῦν, ἀναδῆσαί τινα, to crown one for good news brought, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II good tidings, good news, in plural, [LXX+1st c.BC+]
__II.2 in Christian sense, the gospel, [NT]
