ἀτιμία

atimía — G819

Transliteration: atimía

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G819

Derivation: from G820 (ἄτιμος);

Definition

infamy, i.e. (subjectively) comparative indignity, (objectively) disgrace

KJV Translation

dishonour, reproach, shame, vile

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀτῑμ-ία,
Ionic dialect -ιη, ἡ, dishonour, disgrace, ἀτιμίῃσιν ἰάλλειν[Refs 8th c.BC+]; θεῶν ἀ. dishonour done to the gods, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; indignities, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 deprivation of privileges, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; especially of civic rights, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; coupled with θάνατος and φυγή, [Refs]
__II of things, ἐσθημάτων ἀ., i.e. sorry garb, [Refs 5th c.BC+]. [Epic dialect ἀτιμῑη[Refs 8th c.BC+]
