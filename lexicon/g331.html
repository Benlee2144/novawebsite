ἀνάθεμα

anáthema — G331

Transliteration: anáthema

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G331

Derivation: from G394 (ἀνατίθεμαι);

Definition

a (religious) ban or (concretely) excommunicated (thing or person)

KJV Translation

accused, anathema, curse, X great

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἀνάθεμα,
poetry ἄνθεμα, ατος, τό, (ἀνατίθημι) properly, like{ἀνάθημα}, anything dedicated, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__2 anything devoted to evil, an accursed thing, [LXX]; of persons. [NT]
__II curse, [Refs 1st c.AD+]

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

ἀνάθεμα, -τος, τό
(ἀνατίθημι),
Hellenistic for Attic ἀνάθημα (Bl., § 27, 2);
__1. prop. = τὸ ἀνατιθέμένον, that which is laid by to be kept, a votive offering (as ἀνάθημα in \2, Luk.21:5—where LT read -θεμα, see M, Pr., 46).
__2. [As equiv. in LXX for חֵרֶם,] devoted, a thing devoted to God (see Driver, De., 98 f., and cf. Lev.27:28-29), hence;
__(a) of the sentence pronounced (Deu.13:15), a curse: Act.23:14;
__(b) of the object on which the curse is laid, accursed (Deu.7:26): Rom.9:3, 1Co.12:3 16:22, Gal.1:8-9 (see ICC on Ro.; Lft., Ga., ll. with; Cremer, 547; Tr., Syn., § v; MM, VGT, see word).†
