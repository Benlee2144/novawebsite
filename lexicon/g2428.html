ἱκετηρία

hiketēría — G2428

Transliteration: hiketēría

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2428

Derivation: from a derivative of the base of G2425 (ἱκανός) (through the idea of approaching for a favor);

Definition

intreaty

KJV Translation

supplication

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἱκετ-ήριος,
poetry ἱκτήριος, α, ον, as adjective in the latter form only, of or fit for suppliants, ἱ. θησαυρός, of hair offered to a god, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; φωτῶν ἱκτήρια, ={φῶτας ἱκτηρίους}, [Refs]
__II ἱκετηρία, poetry ἱκτηρία, Ionic dialect ἱκετηρίη (i.e. ῥάβδος), ἡ, olive-branch which the suppliant held in his hand as a symbol of his condition, λευκοστεφεῖς ἱκτηρίας [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ἱκετηρίην λαβεῖν, φέρειν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; especially of petitions laid before the Athenian people, ἱ. θεῖναι And.[same place], [Refs 4th c.BC+], which is a later use,[Refs]; ὑπὲρ θυγατρὸς ἱ. τιθεμένη [Refs 3rd c.AD+]; ἱ. ἔθηκεν παρ᾽ ὑμῖν,= ἱκέτευσεν ὑμᾶς, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; later ἱ. πέμπειν, προβάλλεσθαι, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; ἱκετηρίας προσενέγκας, ἱκετηρίαν προσάγειν, [NT+4th c.AD+], where the suppliant represents herself as the olive-branch, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 ={ἱκεσία}, variant in [Refs 5th c.BC+]ad Ath.275c, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
