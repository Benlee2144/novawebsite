Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4347

προσκολλάω

proskolláō — G4347

Transliteration: proskolláō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4347

Derivation: from G4314 (πρός) and G2853 (κολλάω);

Definition

to glue to, i.e. (figuratively) to adhere

KJV Translation

cleave, join (self)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

προσκολλ-άω,
glue on or to, τι πρός τι [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, generally, to be stuck to, stick or cleave to, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὑπὸ τοῦ αἵματος προσκολληθῆναι τὴν ῥομφαίαν αὐτοῦ τῇ δεξιᾷ [Refs 1st c.AD+]; of a snail, τοῖς θαμνίσκοις π. [Refs 1st c.AD+]; of a husband, π. τῇ γυναικί [LXX+NT+1st c.AD+]
__II intransitive of style, to be compact, [Refs 1st c.BC+]
