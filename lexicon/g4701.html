σπορά

sporá — G4701

Transliteration: sporá

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4701

Derivation: from G4687 (σπείρω);

Definition

a sowing, i.e. (by implication) parentage

KJV Translation

seed

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σπορ-ά, ἡ,
(σπείρω) sowing of seed, σπερμάτων [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__b of children, σπορᾶς γε μὴν ἐκ τῆσδε from this origin, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; τοιοῦτος ὢν τοιῷδ᾽ ὀνειδίζεις σπορά; his origin, birth.. ? [Refs 5th c.BC+]; procreation, παίδων, γένους, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τὴν Ῥωμύλου σ. begetting, [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__2 seed-time, sowing-time, ἀπὸ τῆς σ. [Refs 4th c.BC+]; δεκέτεσιν σποραῖσιν in the tenth seed-time, i.e. year, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II seed, [NT]; field sown, ξηρὰ σ. dry land, uncertain reading in [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σ. δράκοντος ground sown with the dragon's teeth, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.b of persons, seed, offspring, [Refs 4th c.BC+]: plural, young ones, uncertain in [Refs 5th c.BC+]. the female race, [Refs]; θήλεια σ. [Refs]
