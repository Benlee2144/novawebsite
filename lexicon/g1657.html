ἐλευθερία

eleuthería — G1657

Transliteration: eleuthería

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1657

Derivation: from G1658 (ἐλεύθερος);

Definition

freedom (legitimate or licentious, chiefly moral or ceremonial)

KJV Translation

liberty

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐλευθ-ερία,
Ionic dialect -ιη, ἡ, freedom, liberty, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; δι᾽ ἐλευθερίας μόλις ἐξῆλθες, i.e. μόλις ἠλευθερώθης, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; freedom from a thing, ἀπὸ πασῶν ἀρχῶν [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__b manumission, ἡ εἰκοστὴ τῶν ἐ.,= Latin vicesima manumissionum, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 licence, ἀκολασία καὶ ἐ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of Diogenes, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
__3 later, ={ἐλευθεριότης}, [Refs]
__4 name of a dance, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
