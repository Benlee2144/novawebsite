χωλός

chōlós — G5560

Transliteration: chōlós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5560

Derivation: apparently a primary word;

Definition

\

KJV Translation

cripple, halt, lame

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

χωλός, ή, όν,
lame in the feet, halting, limping, with accusative, χωλὸς δ᾽ ἕτερον πόδα [Refs 8th c.BC+]; also with dative, σκέλει χωλός [Refs 1st c.AD+]: later also of the hand, like{κυλλός}, χωλὸς τὴν χεῖρα [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II metaphorically, defective, φύσις [Refs 5th c.BC+]; one-sided, [Refs]; βασιλεία Oracle texts cited in [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 of metre, especially of the χωλίαμβος (which see), halting, μέτρον [Refs 4th c.BC+]; also of a trochaic metre, [Refs 3rd c.AD+]
__II.3 ἀείδειν χωλά, of a 'lame tale', [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
