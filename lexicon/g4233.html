Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4233

πράκτωρ

práktōr — G4233

Transliteration: práktōr

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4233

Derivation: from a derivative of G4238 (πράσσω);

Definition

a practiser, i.e. (specially), an official collector

KJV Translation

officer

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

πράκ-τωρ, ορος, ὁ,
={πρακτήρ}, one who does or executes, accomplisher, Ζεὺς ὅτου π. φανῇ [Refs 5th c.BC+]; with feminine substantive, Κύπρις.. τῶνδ᾽ ἐφάνη π. [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II official who executes a judgment for debt, especially public debt, bailiff, [NT+5th c.BC+]; τῶν ξενικῶν [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
__II.2 collector of taxes, π. βαλανήου “Ostr.” in Wilcken Grundzüge [Refs 1st c.AD+]; π. ἀργυρικῶν [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; π. σιτικῶν [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II.3 in Poets, one who exacts punishment, avenger, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: as adjective, with a feminine substantive, avenging, σὺν δορὶ καὶ χερὶ πράκτορι [Refs 4th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Luke 12:58

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

πράκτωρ, -ορος, ὁ
(πράσσω), [in LXX: Isa.3:12 (נָגַשׁ) * ;]
__1. (poët.) one who does or accomplishes.
__2. In Athens, one who exacts payment, a collector; hence, generally (frequently in π., see Deiss., BS, 154), a court officer: Luk.12:58.†
