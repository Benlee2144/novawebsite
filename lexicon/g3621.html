Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3621

οἰκονομέω

oikonoméō — G3621

Transliteration: oikonoméō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3621

Derivation: from G3623 (οἰκονόμος);

Definition

to manage (a house, i.e. an estate)

KJV Translation

be steward

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

οἰκονομ-έω,
manage as a house-steward, order, regulate, θαλάμους πατρός [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ταῦτα (i.e. meats) [Refs 2nd c.AD+]:—middle, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__2 dispense, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; disburse, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]
__3 treat a substance with another, πυρίτην ὀξάλμῃ [Refs 5th c.BC+]: metaphorically, of a poet, εἰ τὰ ἄλλα μὴ εὖ οἰ. treat, handle, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; so (in middle) of an artist, οἰ. τὴν ὕλην [Refs 2nd c.AD+]:—passive, τὰ σκέμματα.. ᾠκονομήσθω [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__4 of public officers, administer, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]:—passive, πολιτεία ἀρίστη ἡ ὑπὸ τῶν ἀρίστων -ουμένη [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II intransitive, to be a house-steward, [NT]
