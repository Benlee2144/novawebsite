Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5176

τρώγω

trṓgō — G5176

Transliteration: trṓgō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5176

Derivation: probably strengthened from a collateral form of the base of G5134 (τραῦμα) and G5147 (τρίβος) through the idea of corrosion or wear;

Definition

or perhaps rather of a base of G5167 (τρυγών) and G5149 (τρίζω) through the idea of a craunching sound; to gnaw or chew, i.e. (generally) to eat

KJV Translation

eat

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

τρώγω
__1. prop., of animals, to gnaw, munch, crunch (Hom., al.).
__2. Of men, to eat raw food, as vegetables, nuts, etc. (Hdt., al.).
__3. In late vernacular, simply to eat (= ἐσθίω): Mat.24:38, Jhn.6:54, 56-58 13:18 (LXX ἐσθίων) (see Kennedy, Sources, 82, 155; MM, xxv).†
