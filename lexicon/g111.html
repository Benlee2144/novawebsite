ἀθέμιτος

athémitos — G111

Transliteration: athémitos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G111

Derivation: from G1 (Α) (as a negative particle) and a derivative of (statute;

Definition

from the base of G5087 (τίθημι)); illegal; by implication, flagitious

KJV Translation

abominable, unlawful thing

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

or ἀθέμιτος, ον, (the former in Poetry, the latter more correct in Prose) = {ἀθεμίστιος}, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; of the Cyclopes, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἀθεμιστότεροι[Refs 5th c.BC+]adverb -ίστως Phaennis cited in [Refs 2nd c.AD+] -ίτως [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II of things, unlawful, frequently in neuter, ἀθέμιτα ἔρδειν[NT+5th c.BC+]
