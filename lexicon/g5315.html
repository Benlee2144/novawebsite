Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5315

φάγω

phágō — G5315

Transliteration: phágō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5315

Derivation: a primary verb (used as an alternate of G2068 (ἐσθίω) in certain tenses);

Definition

to eat (literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

eat, meat

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

φᾰγεῖν,
infinitive of ἔφαγον, with no present in use (except in late Gr., φαγεῖ [Refs]; φαγέοις in editions of [Refs 1st c.AD+], used as aorist 2 of ἐσθί; later 1st pers. plural ἐφάγαμεν [LXX]future is φάγομαι,[LXX+NT]; 2nd pers. singular φάγεσαι [LXX+NT]; future φαγήσω is see uncertain in [Refs 4th c.AD+]:—eat, devour, both of men and beasts, frequently in [Refs 8th c.BC+]: with genitive, eat of a thing, [LXX+8th c.BC+]
__II eat up, devour, squander, [Refs 8th c.BC+] and Sanskrit bhájati 'apportion, (middle) enjoy'.)
