μάκελλον

mákellon — G3111

Transliteration: mákellon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3111

Derivation: of Latin origin (macellum);

Definition

a butcher's stall, meat market or provision-shop

KJV Translation

shambles

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μάκελλον, τό,
enclosure, [Refs 4th c.BC+]:— also μάκελλος, ὁ, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; μάκελος, ὁ, and plural μακέλα, μάκελλα, [Refs 5th c.AD+]
__II μάκελλος, ὁ, = Latin macellum, market, [NT+1st c.BC+]:—hence μακελλάριος, ὁ, = Latin macellarius, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; = laniator, lanio, [Refs]: neuter μακελλάριον, τό, [Refs 6th c.AD+].
