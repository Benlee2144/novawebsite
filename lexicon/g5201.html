Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5201

ὑδρία

hydría — G5201

Transliteration: hydría

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5201

Derivation: from G5204 (ὕδωρ);

Definition

a water-jar, i.e. receptacle for family supply

KJV Translation

water-pot

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὑδρ-ία, ἡ,
(ὕδωρ) water-pot, pitcher, [NT+5th c.BC+]; ὑδρίης πέρι δῆρις (compare ἀμφορίτης) [Refs 3rd c.BC+] to break the pitcher at the door, 'there's many a slip 'twixt cup and lip', [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II vessel of any kind, e. g. wine-pot, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; a pot of money, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; ὑ. χαλκῆ [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ὑ. χρυσῆ, ἀργυρᾶ, [Refs]; ὑδρίαι ἄρτων πέντε bread-pans, [Refs 6th c.AD+]
__II.2 balloting urn, especially in lawcourts, etc., [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.3 cinerary urn, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II.4 water-clock, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
