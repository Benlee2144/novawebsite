Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4660

σκύλλω

skýllō — G4660

Transliteration: skýllō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4660

Derivation: apparently a primary verb;

Definition

to flay, i.e. (figuratively) to harass

KJV Translation

trouble(self)

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

σκύλλω
__1. in cl., to skin, flay, rend (Æsch., Anthol.).
__2. In Hellenistic writers, to vex, trouble, annoy: with accusative of person(s), Mrk.5:35, Luk.8:49; pass., ἐσκυλμένοι, distressed, Mat.9:36; mid., to trouble oneself, μὴ σκύλλου, Luk.7:6 (frequently in π.; see MM, i, ii, and cf. M, Pr., 89; Abbott, Essays, 87; Kennedy, Sources, 82).†
