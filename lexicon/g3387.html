μήτις

mḗtis — G3387

Transliteration: mḗtis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3387

Derivation: from G3361 (μή) and G5100 (τὶς);

Definition

whether any

KJV Translation

any (sometimes unexpressed except by the simple interrogative form of the sentence)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μήτῐς
or μή τις, ὁ, ἡ, neuter μήτῐ, genitive μήτῐνος: (τὶς) old Gr. and Cretan dialect for μηδείς, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; Cretan dialect dative singular μηδιμί [Refs] —hence
__1 μήτι or μή τι, adverb, with imperative, and infinitive used imperatively, [Refs 8th c.BC+]: with optative to express a wish, ὄλοιντο μή τι πάντες [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 after Verbs of fear or doubt, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__3 in direct questions, μή τί σοι δοκῶ ταρβεῖ; do I.. ? (i.e. I do not), [NT+4th c.BC+]
__4 μή τί γε let alone, much less, οὐδὲ στρατιώτης οὗτός γε οὐδενός ἐστιν ἄξιος, μή τί γε τῶν ἄλλων ἡγεμών [Refs 4th c.BC+]; later, not to mention, οὐκ οἴδατε ὅτι ἀγγέλους κρινοῦμε; μήτι γε βιωτικά [NT]: with a word between, ὡς.. δώσοντι δίκην, μή τι ποιήσαντί γε [Refs 5th c.BC+]
