ἔχθρα

échthra — G2189

Transliteration: échthra

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2189

Derivation: feminine of G2190 (ἐχθρός);

Definition

hostility; by implication, a reason for opposition

KJV Translation

enmity, hatred

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἔχθρ-α,
Ionic dialect ἔχθρη, ἡ, hatred, enmity, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: in philosophical sense, ={νεῖκος}[Refs 3rd c.AD+]; ἔ. τινός hatred for, enmity to one, [NT+5th c.BC+]; δι᾽ ἔχθρας μολεῖν, ἀφῖχθαί τινι, to be at feud with one, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; πρὸς ἔχθραν from personal enmity, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; ἔ. συμβάλλειν, συνάπτειν τινί, to engage in hostility with.., [Refs 5th c.BC+], of undying hatred, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
