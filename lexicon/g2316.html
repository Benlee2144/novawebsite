Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G2316

θεός

theós — G2316

Transliteration: theós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2316

Derivation: of uncertain affinity; a deity, especially (with G3588 (ὁ)) the supreme Divinity;

Definition

figuratively, a magistrate; by Hebraism, very

KJV Translation

X exceeding, God, god(-ly, -ward)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

θεός, ὁ,
Boeotian dialect θιός, Laconian dialect σιός (see. below), [Refs 5th c.AD+], Cretan dialect θιός [Refs], Doric dialect also θεύς [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; vocative (only late) θεός, also θεέ [LXX+NT]; but classical in compound names, Ἀμφίθεε, Τιμόθεε:—God, the Deity, in general sense, both singular and plural (εἰ καὶ ἐπὶ θεοὺς καὶ ἔτι μᾶλλον ἐπὶ θεὸν ἁρμόζει μεταφέρειν [Refs 8th c.BC+]; σὺν θ. εἰρημένον [Refs 5th c.BC+]: so in plural, σύν γε θεοῖσιν [Refs 8th c.BC+]; οὐ θεῶν ἄτερ pi.[Refs 8th c.BC+]; ὑπὲρ θεόν against his will,[Refs 4th c.BC+]; θεῶν συνεθελόντων, βουλομένων, [Refs 5th c.BC+]bless you! good heavens! for heaven's sake!
