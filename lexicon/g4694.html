Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4694

σπιλάς

spilás — G4694

Transliteration: spilás

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4694

Derivation: of uncertain derivation;

Definition

a ledge or reef of rock in the sea

KJV Translation

spot (by confusion with G4696 (σπίλος))

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

σπῐλάς, άδος, ἡ,

__A rock over which the sea dashes (opposed to ὕφαλοι πέτραι in [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἐφ᾽ ὑψηλαῖς σπιλάδεσσι [Refs 5th c.BC+]; πλαγκταὶ σ. [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; ῥεῖθρον ἀπὸ σ. [Refs 3rd c.BC+]: generally, slab, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὧδ᾽ ὑπὸ τὸ σπιλάδος μέλαθρον, i.e. under this tombstone, [Refs]; hollow rock, cave, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__A.II as adjective, stony, or perhaps marly, i.e. γῆ, [Refs 4th c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Jude 1:12

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

σπιλάς, -άδος, ἡ
(on the gender in Ju, l.with, see Mayor, Ju., 41),
__1. poët. in cl., a rock or reef over which the sea dashes (Polyb., FlJ, al.). Metaphorical, of men whose conduct causes danger to others, Jude 12, R, txt. (but see infr.).
__2. In late writers = σπίλος (which see), a spot, stain; metaphorically, Ju, l.with, R, mg. (cf. Mayor, 41, but see supr.).†
