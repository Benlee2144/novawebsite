μετάθεσις

metáthesis — G3331

Transliteration: metáthesis

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3331

Derivation: from G3346 (μετατίθημι);

Definition

transposition, i.e. transferral (to heaven), disestablishment (of a law)

KJV Translation

change, removing, translation

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μετάθεσις, εως, ἡ,
(μετατίθημι) change of position, transposition, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; τοῦ ἐμβρύου [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; couching of a cataract, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 generally, change, θεὸς οὐδεμίαν ἐπιδεχόμενος μ. [NT+5th c.BC+]; especially change of sides or opinions, ἐπὶ τὸ βέλτιον [Refs 2nd c.BC+] (hence, amendment, τῶν ἡμαρτημένων [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; going over, πρός τινα [Refs]
__3 exchange, barter, [Refs]
__4 Grammars, change of a letter, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]; also, metathesis, transposition of letters, as κραδίη for καρδία, [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__5 plagiarism, opposed to μίμησις, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II power or right of changing sides, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
