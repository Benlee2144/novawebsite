εὐγενής

eugenḗs — G2104

Transliteration: eugenḗs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2104

Derivation: from G2095 (εὖ) and G1096 (γίνομαι);

Definition

well born, i.e. (literally) high in rank, or (figuratively) generous

KJV Translation

more noble, nobleman

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

εὐγεν-ής, ές,
in [Refs 8th c.BC+] εὐηγενής (which see), and in [Refs] ἠϋγενής: (γένος):— well-born, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τὸ μὲν ἐστίχθαι εὐγενὲς κέκριται being tattooed is esteemed a mark of nobility, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 in Trag. etc. with the connotation noble-minded, generous (more properly γενναῖος,[Refs 5th c.BC+]; διαφέρει φύσις γενναίου σκύλακος.. νεανίσκου εὐ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 of animals, high-bred, ἵππος [Refs 6th c.BC+]; of plants, of a good sort, [Refs 4th c.BC+]: so in comparative, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: metaphorically, of a wife, ὥσπερ εὐγενῆ χώραν ἐντεκνώσασθαι παρασχεῖν [Refs 1st c.AD+]
__4 of outward form, noble, δέρη, πρόσωπον, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of style, τὸ εὐ. τῆς λέξεως [Refs 1st c.BC+]
__II adverb -νῶς nobly, bravely, κατθανοῦμεν [Refs 5th c.BC+]
