Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5479

χαρά

chará — G5479

Transliteration: chará

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5479

Derivation: from G5463 (χαίρω);

Definition

cheerfulness, i.e. calm delight

KJV Translation

gladness, X greatly, (X be exceeding) joy(-ful, -fully, -fulness, -ous)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

χᾰρά, ἡ:
(χαίρω):— joy, delight, first in [Refs 7th c.BC+] of a hungry man, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; with genitive objecti, joy in or at a thing, μελέων [Refs 5th c.BC+]; πρὸς χαρὰν λόγων in accordance with joyous tidings, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; κέρτομος θεοῦ χ. a joy sent by some mocking god, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; λέγεις μοι χαράν wishest me joy, [NT+5th c.BC+]with joy, [LXX+5th c.BC+]; χαρᾶς ὕπο [Refs 5th c.BC+]; opposed to λύπη, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II in concrete sense, a joy, of persons, χ. μου [NT]
