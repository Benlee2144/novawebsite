Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4359

προσοφείλω

prosopheílō — G4359

Transliteration: prosopheílō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4359

Derivation: from G4314 (πρός) and G3784 (ὀφείλω);

Definition

to be indebted additionally

KJV Translation

over besides

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

προσοφείλω,
owe besides or still, ἔτι πολλά [Refs 5th c.BC+], etc.: absolutely, προσοφείλοντας ἡμᾶς ἐνέγραψεν [Refs 4th c.BC+]:— passive, to be still owing, ὁ προσοφειλόμενος μισθός [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἡ ἔχθρη ἡ προσοφειλομένη ἐς Ἀθηναίους ἐκ τῶν Αἰγινητέων the hatred which was still due from the Aeginetans to the Athenians, i.e. their ancient feud, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II to be behindhand, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]

Appears In (1 verse)

Philemon 1:19
