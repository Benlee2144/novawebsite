ἄτερ

áter — G817

Transliteration: áter

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G817

Derivation: a particle probably akin to G427 (ἄνευ);

Definition

aloof, i.e. apart from (literally or figuratively)

KJV Translation

in the absence of, without

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἄτερ [ᾰ],
Epic dialect, Ionic dialect, Trag. preposition with genitive, without, apart from, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἄ. Ζηνός without his will, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; οὐ θεῶν ἄ. 'non sine dis', [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II aloof, apart from, ἄ. ἥμενον ἄλλων[Refs 8th c.BC+]:—frequently in Trag., mostly after its case, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; but before it in [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—also in late Prose, as [LXX+1st c.BC+]
__III with dative, ἄτερ ἄστρασιν[Refs 1st c.AD+]
