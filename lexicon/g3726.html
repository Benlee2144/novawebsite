Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3726

ὁρκίζω

horkízō — G3726

Transliteration: horkízō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3726

Derivation: from G3727 (ὅρκος);

Definition

to put on oath, i.e. make swear; by analogy, to solemnly enjoin

KJV Translation

adjure, charge

🏛#x1f3db; Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὁρκ-ίζω,
Doric dialect future ὁρκιξέω [Refs 4th c.BC+], make one swear, administer an oath to a person, τιν; rejected by [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὁ. ἐφ᾽ ᾧ ἔσται [Refs 2nd c.BC+]: with double accusative, ὁ. τινὰς ὅρκον [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; ὁ. τινά, with infinitive, [LXX]; adjure, δαίμονας, with infinitive, [LXX+NT+5th c.AD+]:—passive, to be sworn, ὡρκισμένοι νόμῳ ἰητρικῷ [Refs 5th c.BC+]
