ὀθόνη

othónē — G3607

Transliteration: othónē

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3607

Derivation: of uncertain affinity;

Definition

a linen cloth, i.e. (especially) a sail

KJV Translation

sheet

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὀθόν-η, ἡ,
fine linen, in [Refs 8th c.BC+] always plural, fine linen cloths, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; of a woman's dress, ἀργεννῇσι καλυψαμένη ὀθόνῃσιν [Refs 8th c.BC+]: singular, a cloth, [NT+2nd c.AD+]
__2 later, sails, πνεύσεται εἰς ὀθόνας [Refs 1st c.BC+]: singular, sail-cloth, sail, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__3 in plural, of the membranes that enclose the pupil of the eye, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
