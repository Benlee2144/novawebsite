ὑπογραμμός

hypogrammós — G5261

Transliteration: hypogrammós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5261

Derivation: from a compound of G5259 (ὑπό) and G1125 (γράφω);

Definition

an underwriting, i.e. copy for imitation (figuratively)

KJV Translation

example

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὑπογραμμός, ὁ,
writing-copy, pattern, model, outline, [LXX+NT]; ὑ. παιδικοί copy-heads for children, containing all the letters of the alphabet, of which three forms have been preserved by [Refs 2nd c.AD+] —μάρπτε σφὶγξ κλὼψ ζβυχθηδόν, βέδυ ζὰψ χθὼμ πλῆκτρον σφίγξ, and κναξζβὶ χθύπτης φλεγμὼ δρόψ, which last was wrongly ascribed to [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II outline, σκιὰ καὶ ὑ. [Refs 1st c.AD+]
