⚠ A Note on These Sources

These reference works were written by 19th and early 20th century scholars who, despite their expertise, carried centuries of church tradition into their definitions and articles. You will find theological interpretations presented as fact — particularly around words like “hell,” “repent,” “eternal,” “church,” and “pastor.”

We include these works because they are valuable for historical context, cultural background, and archaeological data. But where their definitions contradict what the original Hebrew and Greek actually say, we’ve marked those entries with ⚠ Our Analysis Differs notes linking to our studies with the full evidence.

Always check the original language. That’s what this entire site is built to help you do.
