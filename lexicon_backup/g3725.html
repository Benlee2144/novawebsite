Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3725

ὅριον

hórion — G3725

Transliteration: hórion

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3725

Derivation: neuter of a derivative of an apparently primary (a bound or limit);

Definition

a boundary-line, i.e. (by implication) a frontier (region)

KJV Translation

border, coast

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ὅριον, τό,
={ὅρος}, diminutive only in form, boundary, limit, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: mostly in plural, boundaries, bounds, frontier, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ἐπὶ τοῖς ὁ. on the frontier, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὅρια κελεύθου limits of a road, i.e. the road itself, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; territories, [LXX]
__2 Astrology texts, a subdivision of a zodiacal sign, appropriated to a planet, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__II plural, rules, e.g. for the use of wine, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III ὅριον· τείχισμα, φραγμόν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
