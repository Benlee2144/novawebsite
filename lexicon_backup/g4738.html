Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G4738

στῆθος

stēthos — G4738

Transliteration: stēthos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G4738

Derivation: from G2476 (ἵστημι) (as standing prominently);

Definition

the (entire external) bosom, i.e. chest

KJV Translation

breast

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

στῆθος, εος, τό,
breast, of both sexes, being the front part of the θώραξ, divided into two μαστοί [Refs 8th c.BC+] in post-Homeric verse; found once in [Refs 8th c.BC+]; of animals, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; σ. φάσσης ἑψημένης [Refs 2nd c.AD+]: as the seat of the voice and breath, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; more frequently as the seat of the heart, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; chest, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; τὰ σ. breasts of a woman, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II metaphorically, the breast as the seat of feeling and thought, as we use heart, frequently in [Refs 8th c.BC+], but always in plural, θυμὸν ἐνὶ στήθεσσιν ὄρινε [
