μνῆμα

mnēma — G3418

Transliteration: mnēma

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3418

Derivation: from G3415 (μνάομαι);

Definition

a memorial, i.e. sepulchral monument (burial-place)

KJV Translation

grave, sepulchre, tomb

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

μνῆμα,
Doric dialect and Aeolic dialect μνᾶμα, ατος, τό, (μνάομαι, μιμνήσκω) memorial, remembrance, record of a person or thing, with genitive, μνῆμ᾽ Ἑλένης χειρῶν [Refs 8th c.BC+]; Νικοκλέος μνᾶμα κελαδῆσαι [Refs 4th c.BC+]; λυγρᾶς μνήματα Τροίας, of the sufferings of the Greeks, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; μ... διὰ χειρὸς ἔχων, i. e. the dead body of his son, [Refs]
__2 mound or building in honour of the dead, monument, tomb, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; coffin, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 memorial dedicated to a god, [Refs 4th c.BC+]: generally, monument, [Refs 6th c.BC+]
__II ={μνήμη}, memory, μ. ἔχουσ᾽ ἀγαθῶν [Refs 6th c.BC+]
