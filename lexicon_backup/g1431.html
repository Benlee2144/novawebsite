Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1431

δωρεά

dōreá — G1431

Transliteration: dōreá

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1431

Derivation: from G1435 (δῶρον);

Definition

a gratuity

KJV Translation

gift

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

δωρ-εά,
Ionic dialect δωρ-εή, ἡ: δωρειά in earlier Attic Inscrr., [Refs]:— gift, present, especially bounty (={δόσις ἀναπόδοτος} [Refs 5th c.BC+]; πορεῖν[Refs 5th c.BC+]; δ. δέχεσθαι, λαμβάνειν, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; δωρειὰν καὶ χάριν [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of a legacy, [Refs 4th c.BC+]; δωρεαί privileges and immunities, opposed to δῶρα, gifts in cash or kind, [Refs 2nd c.AD+]
__2 estate granted by a king, fief, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__II accusative δωρεάν as adverb, as a free gift, freely, [LXX+5th c.BC+]; so κατὰ δωρεάν [Refs 1st c.AD+]; ἐν δωρεᾷ προσνεῖμαι [Refs 2nd c.BC+]; but γῆν (ἀμπελῶνα, etc.) ἐν δωρεᾷ ἔχειν to hold land by a royal grant, PRev. Laws [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__II.2 to no purpose, for naught, [NT]
