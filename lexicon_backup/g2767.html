κεράννυμι

keránnymi — G2767

Transliteration: keránnymi

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2767

Derivation: prolonged form of a more primary (which is used in certain tenses);

Definition

to mingle, i.e. (by implication) to pour out (for drinking)

KJV Translation

fill, pour out

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

κεράννυμι
[in LXX for מָסַךְ, Isa.5:22, al. ;]
to mix, mingle, chiefly of the diluting of wine: Rev.18:6; by oxymoron, κεκεράσμενος ἄκρατος, Rev.14:10.†
SYN.: μίγνυμι. κ. implies "a mixing of two things, so that they are blended and forma compound, as in wine and water, whereas μ. implies a mixing without such composition, as in two sorts of grain" (LS, see word κρᾶσις) (AS)
