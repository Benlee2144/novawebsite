τὶς

tìs — G5100

Transliteration: tìs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5100

Derivation: an enclitic indefinite pronoun;

Definition

some or any person or object

KJV Translation

a (kind of), any (man, thing, thing at all), certain (thing), divers, he (every) man, one (X thing), ought, + partly, some (man, -body, - thing, -what), (+ that no-)thing, what(-soever), X wherewith, whom(-soever), whose(-soever)

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

τις, neut., τι, genitive, τινός,
enclitic indefinite pron., related to interrog. τίς as πού, πως, ποτέ to ποῦ, πῶς, πότε.
__I. As subst.,
__1. one, a certain one: Luk.9:49, Jhn.11:1, Act.5:25, al.; pl., τίνες, certain, some: Luk.13:1, Act.15:1, Rom.3:8, al.
__2. someone, anyone, something, anything: Mat.12:29, Mrk.9:30, Luk.8:46, Jhn.2:25, Act.17:25, Rom.5:7, al.; = indef., one (French on), Mrk.8:4, Jhn.2:25, Rom.8:24, al.; pl., τινες, some, Mrk.14:4, al.
__II. II. As adj.,
__1. a certain: Mat.18:12, Luk.1:5 8:27, Act.3:2, al.; with proper names, Mrk.15:21, Luk.23:26, al.; with genitive partit., Luk.7:19, al.
__2. some: Mrk.16:[8], Jhn.5:14, Act.17:21 24:24, Heb.11:40, al.
