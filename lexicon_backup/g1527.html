εἷς καθ’ εἷς

heîs kath’ heîs — G1527

Transliteration: heîs kath’ heîs

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1527

Derivation: from G1520 (εἷς) repeated with G2596 (κατά) inserted;

Definition

severally

KJV Translation

one by one

📜 Extended Lexicon (Abbott-Smith / STEPBible)

severallyrally, one by one, single person, united

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

εἷς, μίᾰ, ἕν
(μίη only in late Ionic dialect Prose): genitive ἑνός, μιᾶς, ἑνός:—Epic dialect ἕεις [Refs 8th c.BC+]:—Doric dialect ἧς [Refs 3rd c.BC+]:—Epic dialect, Aeolic dialect, and Ionic dialect feminine ἴᾰ [Refs 8th c.BC+]: neuter dative (ἰῷ κίον ἤματι) [Refs 5th c.BC+] (Orig. ἕνς, assim. ἔν (δ) probably in [Refs] Indo-European sem-(compare ὁμός); μία from sm-ί; ἴα is not related to μία, but probably to pronominal stem i-(Latin is), compareἰός.)
