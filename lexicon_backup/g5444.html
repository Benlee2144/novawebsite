Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5444

φύλλον

phýllon — G5444

Transliteration: phýllon

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5444

Derivation: from the same as G5443 (φυλή);

Definition

a sprout, i.e. leaf

KJV Translation

leaf

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

φύλλον, τό
leaf; in Epic dialect and [Refs 5th c.BC+] always in plural leaves, or collectively foliage, φύλλα καὶ ὄζους [Refs 8th c.BC+]; πλεκτὰ φύλλα wreathed leaves, [Refs]; φύλλον ἐλαίας, poetry for ἐλάα, S.[same place]: metaphorically of choral songs, φύλλ᾽ ἀοιδᾶν [Refs 5th c.BC+]; of leaves used as voting-papers, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__2 of flowers, petal, [ῥόδον] ἔχον ἑξήκοντα φύλλα [Refs 5th c.BC+]; ὑακίνθινα φ., λειμώνια φ., [Refs 3rd c.BC+]
__II plant, in general, φ. ὂν ἐπινηχόμενον τῷ ὕδατι [Refs 1st c.AD+]; ἡ κατὰ φύλλον (with or without γεωμετρία) survey according to plants, i. e. crops grown, [Refs 2nd c.BC+]: especially of medicinal herbs, φ. εἴ τι νώδυνον κάτοιδε [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2 as a name of definite species:
__II.2.a ={βρυωνία}, dog Mercury, Mercurialis perennis, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
