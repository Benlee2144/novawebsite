ἐπιορκέω

epiorkéō — G1964

Transliteration: epiorkéō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1964

Derivation: from G1965 (ἐπίορκος);

Definition

to commit perjury

KJV Translation

forswear self

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐπιορκ-έω,
also ἐφιορκέω [Refs 3rd c.BC+], etc.: future -ήσω [Refs 8th c.BC+]: aorist ἐπιώρκησα [Refs], infinitive -ορκῆσαι [Refs 5th c.BC+]: perfect ἐπιώρκηκα [Refs]:—swear falsely, forswear oneself, οὐδ᾽ ἐπιορκήσω πρὸς δαίμονος 8th c.BC: Ilias Homerus Epicus “Illiad” 19.188: also, c.accusative, of things sworn by, τὰς βασιληΐας ἱστίας ἐπιώρκηκε has sworn falsely by the royal hearth, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; [θεούς] [Refs 4th c.BC+]; opposed to εὐορκέω, Legal cited in [Refs]
__II. simply, ={ὄμνυμι}, swear, [Refs]
