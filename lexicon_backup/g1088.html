γέρων

gérōn — G1088

Transliteration: gérōn

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1088

Derivation: of uncertain affinity (compare G1094 (γῆρας));

Definition

aged

KJV Translation

old

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

γέρων, οντος, ὁ,
old man, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__2 γέροντες, οἱ, Elders, Chiefs, κίκλησκεν δὲ γέροντας ἀριστῆας Παναχαιῶν [Refs 8th c.BC+]; in other states, as at Elis, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II as adjective, old, γέρον σάκος [Refs 8th c.BC+]; οἶνος [Refs 4th c.BC+]; πέπλος, λέμβος, [Refs 3rd c.BC+]: rarely in Prose, οἱ γέροντες τῶν ἰχθύων [Refs 4th c.BC+]; of stags, [Refs]; Ἀντίγονος ὁ γ. Antigonus the Elder, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__III part of the spinning-wheel, [Refs 5th c.BC+]. (Sanskrit járant- 'old', járati 'render infirm'; compare γέρας.)

John 3:4
