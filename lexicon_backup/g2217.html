ζόφος

zóphos — G2217

Transliteration: zóphos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2217

Derivation: akin to the base of G3509 (νέφος);

Definition

gloom (as shrouding like a cloud)

KJV Translation

blackness, darkness, mist

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ζόφος, ὁ,
nether darkness, ἱεμένων Ἔρεβόσδε ὑπὸ ζόφον [NT+8th c.BC+]; Ἀΐδης δ᾽ ἔλαχε ζόφον ἠερόεντα obtained the realms of gloom for his share, [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__2 generally, gloom, darkness, [NT+8th c.BC+]; χειμέριος ζ. the gloom of winter, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II the dark quarter, i.e. the West, ἤδη γὰρ φάος οἴχεθ᾽ ὑπὸ ζόφον [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ποτὶ ζόφον, opposed to πρὸς ἠῶ τ᾽ ἠέλιόν τε, [Refs 8th c.BC+] (the North accusative to [Refs 1st c.BC+]; Γαδείρων τὸ πρὸς ζόφον to the west of.., [Refs 5th c.BC+] —poetical and later Prose. (Prob. _cognate_ with ζέφυρος.)
