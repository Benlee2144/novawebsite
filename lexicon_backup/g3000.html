Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G3000

λατρεύω

latreúō — G3000

Transliteration: latreúō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3000

Derivation: from (a hired menial);

Definition

to minister (to God), i.e. render religious homage

KJV Translation

serve, do the service, worship(-per)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

λατρ-εύω,
Elean λατρείω (which see), work for hire or pay, [Refs 6th c.BC+]: to be in servitude, serve, [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__2 λ. τινί to be subject or enslaved to, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: with accusative person, serve, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; λ. νόμοις obey, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; λ. καιρῷ, = Latin temporibus inservire, [Refs 1st c.AD+]; τῷ κάλλει λ. to be devoted to.., [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__3 serve the gods with prayers and sacrifices, λ. Φοίβῳ [Refs 5th c.BC+] render due service, [Refs]; πόνον.. τόνδ᾽ ἐλάτρευσα θεᾷ [Refs]
