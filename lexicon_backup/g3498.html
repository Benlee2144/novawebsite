νεκρός

nekrós — G3498

Transliteration: nekrós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3498

Derivation: from an apparently primary (a corpse);

Definition

dead (literally or figuratively; also as noun)

KJV Translation

dead

νεκρός, -α, -ον,
[in LXX chiefly for מֵת ;]
dead,
__I. as adj.,
__1. prop.: Act.5:10 20:9, Jas.2:26, Rev.1:18, al.; ὡσεὶ ν., Mat.28:4, Mrk.9:26, Rev.1:17; of that which is subject to death, Rom.8:10.
__2. Metaphorical,
__(a) of persons: Luk.15:24, 32; of those immersed in worldly cares, Mat.8:22, Luk.9:60; of spiritual death, Jhn.5:25, Rom.6:13, Eph.5:14, Rev.3:1; τ. παραπτώμασιν, Eph.2:1, 5 Col.2:13; of the opposite condition, ν. τῇ ἁμαρτίᾳ, Rom.6:11;
__(b) of things regarded as inoperative, devoid of power: ἁμαρτία, Rom.7:8; πίστις, Jas.2:17, 26; ἔργα, Heb.6:1 9:14.
__II. As subst., νεκρός, ὁ (Hom., al.), chiefly in pl.
