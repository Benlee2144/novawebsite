ὕδωρ

hýdōr — G5204

Transliteration: hýdōr

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5204

Derivation: from the base of G5205 (ὑετός);

Definition

water (as if rainy) literally or figuratively

KJV Translation

water

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

[Refs 8th c.BC+]; of rivers, ὕ. Αἰσήποιο, Στυγός, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; so in Lyric poetry and Trag., ὕ, Ἀσώπιον [Refs 5th c.BC+]: frequently in plural (but only once in [Refs 8th c.BC+] the waters of Cephisus, [Refs 5th c.BC+]: spring-water, drinking-water, οἶνον ἔμισγον καὶ ὕ. [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ὕ. πίνων a water-drinker, [Refs 5th c.BC+] water for washing the hands, see at {χεί; φέρτε χερσὶν ὕ}.
