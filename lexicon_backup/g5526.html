Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G5526

χορτάζω

chortázō — G5526

Transliteration: chortázō

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G5526

Derivation: from G5528 (χόρτος);

Definition

to fodder, i.e. (generally) to gorge (supply food in abundance)

KJV Translation

feed, fill, satisfy

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

χορτ-άζω,
feed, fatten, properly of cattle [Refs 8th c.BC+]; χορτάσω τὸν κάνθαρον (the beetle being comically treated as a horse), [Refs 5th c.BC+]; with accusative of things, [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, eat their fill, of cattle, [Refs]; τινος of a thing, [Refs 4th c.BC+]
__II of persons, feed, βολβοῖς ἐμαυτὸν χορτάσω [Refs 4th c.BC+]: with genitive, fill full of.., θεράπευε καὶ χόρταζε τῶν μονῳδιῶν (metaphorically) [Refs 5th c.BC+]:—passive, with accusative, χορταζόμενοι γάλα λευκόν [LXX+5th c.BC+]: absolutely, feast, κεχόρτασμαι.. οὐ κακῶς [NT+4th c.BC+]
