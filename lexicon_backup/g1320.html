Lexicon • Greek • Strong’s G1320

διδάσκαλος

didáskalos — G1320

Transliteration: didáskalos

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G1320

Derivation: from G1321 (διδάσκω);

Definition

an instructor (genitive case or specially)

KJV Translation

doctor, master, teacher

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

δῐδάσκᾰλ-ος, ὁ
(but feminine, [Refs 4th c.BC+], compare ξυμφορὴ γίνεται δ. [Refs 2nd c.AD+], teacher, master, μαντείης [Refs 5th c.BC+]; διδάσκαλον λαβεῖν get a master, [[Refs 5th c.BC+]; εἰς διδασκάλου (i.e. οἶκον) φοιτᾶν go to school, [Refs 5th c.BC+]; διδασκάλων or ἐκ διδασκάλων ἀπαλλαγῆναι leave school, [Refs]; ἐν διδασκάλων at school, [Refs]
__II trainer of a dithyrambic or dramatic chorus, producer of a play, etc., ἴτω δὲ καὶ τραγῳδίας ὁ Κλεομάχου δ. [Refs 5th c.BC+]
