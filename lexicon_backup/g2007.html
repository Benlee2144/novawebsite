ἐπιτίθημι

epitíthēmi — G2007

Transliteration: epitíthēmi

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G2007

Derivation: from G1909 (ἐπί) and G5087 (τίθημι);

Definition

to impose (in a friendly or hostile sense)

KJV Translation

add unto, lade, lay upon, put (up) on, set on (up), + surname, X wound

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

ἐπιτίθημι,
passive mostly furnished by ἐπίκειμαι: active, lay, put or place upon, of offerings laid on the altar, ἐπὶ μηρία θέντες Ἀπόλλωνι [Refs 8th c.BC+]; set meats on the table, εἴδατα πόλλ᾽ ἐπιθεῖσα [Refs 8th c.BC+]; πάντ᾽ ἐπιθεῖτε on the car, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; τινὶ κύρτον καὶ κώπαν, as a grave-monument, [Refs 7th c.BC+]: but also c.genitive, ἐ. λεχέων τινά [Refs 8th c.BC+]; κεφαλὴν ἐπὶ στέρνα τινός [Refs 5th c.BC+]: with accusative only, put upon, set up, ἐ. φάρμακα apply salves, [Refs 8th c.BC+]; ἐ. μνημεῖά τινι to him, [Refs]
__2 set upon, turn towards, Ἑκτορέοις ἐπὶ φρένα θῆχ᾽ ἱεροῖσιν [Refs 8th c.BC+]; but τῇ δ᾽ ἄρ᾽ ἐπὶ φρεσὶ θῆκε with infinitive, put it into her mind to.., [Refs 8th c.BC+]
__II put on a covering or lid, ὡς εἴ τε φαρέτρῃ πῶμ᾽ ἐπιθείη [Refs]; λίθον δ᾽ ἐπέθηκε θύρῃσι, i.e. put a stone as a door to the cave, put it before the door, [Refs]; also, put a door to, κολλητὰς ἐπέθηκα θύρας [Refs 5th c.BC+]
__II.2
