λειτουργός

leitourgós — G3011

Transliteration: leitourgós

Pronunciation:

Language: Greek

Strong’s: G3011

Derivation: from a derivative of G2992 (λαός) and G2041 (ἔργον);

Definition

a public servant, i.e. a functionary in the Temple or Gospel, or (genitive case) a worshipper (of God) or benefactor (of man)

KJV Translation

minister(-ed)

🏛 Classical & Secular Greek Usage (Liddell-Scott-Jones)

λειτουργ-ός, ὁ,
(λήϊτος, ἔργον) one who performed a λειτουργία (which see), [Refs 3rd c.AD+]; λ. τῶν ἐν παισὶ λειτουργιῶν [Refs]
__II public servant, ἡ στάσις τῶν λ. [τοῦ Σαλομῶνος] [LXX]; of workmen, carpenters, etc., οἰκοδόμοι καὶ λ. [Refs 3rd c.BC+]; at Magnesia, an official of the γερουσία, [Refs]; = Latin lictor, [Refs 1st c.AD+] ministering to my need, [NT]
__II.2 private servant, [LXX]
__III in religioussense, minister, [θεοῦ][LXX+NT+1st c.BC+]; θεοῖς λιτουργοί (sic) [Refs 1st c.BC+]; attendant at sacrifices, acolyte, [Refs]
__IV Astrology texts, λειτουργοί, οἱ, astral gods subordinate to the δεκανοί, [Refs 4th c.AD+]
