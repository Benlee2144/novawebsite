About This Site

What we do, why we do it, and how every study gets built

What Is The Unveiled Word?

The Unveiled Word is a free Bible study resource that goes back to the original Hebrew and Greek texts to show you what Scripture actually says—before centuries of translation, tradition, and theological bias shaped how we read it.

Every study on this site traces key words back to their original language, examines how they were used in their historical context, and shows you where English translations got it right—and where they lost something important along the way.

This isn’t about attacking any translation or denomination. It’s about giving you access to what the original authors actually wrote, in the languages they actually wrote it in, so you can read the Bible with your own eyes instead of someone else’s interpretation.

What Makes This Different?

Original language first. Every claim in every study is traced back to Hebrew (Old Testament) or Greek (New Testament). We cite Strong’s numbers, show you the actual word, and link to established lexicons so you can verify everything yourself.

Honest about disagreements. When scholars disagree—and they often do—we show you both sides with their textual evidence. We don’t pretend there’s always one obvious answer.

Practical, not academic. Knowing that metanoeō means “to change one’s mind” is interesting. Knowing what that means for how you live on a Tuesday afternoon is the point.

Free. No ads. No paywalls. This site exists to serve people who want to understand Scripture better. That’s it.

What’s on This Site?

  • 33+ Deep Studies — Topics from salvation to Satan, hell to heaven, baptism to blood covenants. Each study includes 15–30+ original language word explorations and 50–100+ cross-references.
  • Full Bible Interlinear — 1,189 chapters covering every book of the Bible. See the original Hebrew and Greek alongside English, word by word.
  • Greek & Hebrew Lexicon — Search and explore original language words with definitions, usage, and cross-references.
  • Reference Library — Easton’s Bible Dictionary, ISBE, and other reference works for deeper context.

Our Methodology

Every study follows the same process:

  1. Start with the original text. Hebrew for Old Testament, Greek for New Testament. Never start with an English translation and work backward.
  2. Verify every word. Every Greek/Hebrew word, Strong’s number, and definition is cross-checked against established lexicons (Thayer’s, Brown-Driver-Briggs, BDAG).
  3. Check the context. Words mean different things in different contexts. We look at the surrounding passage, the book’s purpose, the historical situation, and how the same word is used elsewhere in Scripture.
  4. Separate fact from interpretation. “This word means X” is a verifiable fact. “Therefore this passage teaches Y” is interpretation. We clearly distinguish between the two.
  5. Show our work. Every claim links to the lexicon entry, the interlinear text, or the cross-reference so you can check it yourself.

A Note on Honesty

We are not infallible. No Bible study resource is. If you find an error—a wrong Strong’s number, a misquoted verse, a definition that doesn’t match the lexicon—we want to know about it. The goal isn’t to be right. The goal is to be honest about what the text says.
